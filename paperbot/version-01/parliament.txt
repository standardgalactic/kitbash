
The "Paperbot Parliament Constitution" outlines a framework designed to enhance and maintain clarity in arguments through the roles of six distinct offices:

1. **Summarizer**: This role is responsible for condensing information into succinct, yet comprehensive summaries that retain key details and nuances. They avoid generic praise and ensure summaries are not empty or repetitive.

2. **Nitpicker**: The Nitpicker meticulously examines text for minute errors, inconsistencies, and logical fallacies. They help to refine arguments by identifying areas that need clarification or correction.

3. **Skeptic**: This role questions the validity of claims, sources, and assumptions within an argument. The Skeptic ensures that assertions are backed by reliable evidence and scrutinizes potential biases or misinformation.

4. **Archivist**: The Archivist is responsible for maintaining a comprehensive record of all discussed topics, including relevant data, sources, and historical context. They ensure that information is easily accessible for future reference and to prevent redundancy.

5. **Synthesist**: The Synthesist combines insights from different perspectives, weaving together various viewpoints into coherent narratives or unified theories. This role aims to bridge gaps between seemingly disparate ideas and foster deeper understanding.

6. **Admissibility Auditor**: This office evaluates the admissibility of information within an argument based on predefined criteria, such as relevance, reliability, and non-redundancy. They ensure that only pertinent and credible data is considered in the discourse.

Key concepts guiding this framework include reachability (the ability to access and utilize relevant information), projection (the act of mapping ideas onto different contexts), collapse (simplifying complex structures into more manageable forms), repair (correcting flaws or weaknesses in arguments), latent manifold (underlying patterns or structures not immediately apparent), fiber structure (hierarchical organization of elements within a system), and distinction deficit (insufficient clarity or separation between concepts).

This constitution aims to create a balanced, thorough, and rigorous environment for argumentation by integrating these offices and principles.
