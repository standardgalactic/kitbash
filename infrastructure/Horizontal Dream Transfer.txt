Horizontal Dream Transfer: Uploading, Semantic Inheritance, and Distributed Cognition in RSVP Field Theory
Abstract
Conventional approaches to mind uploading emphasize vertical, one-to-one replication of neural states‚Äîmodeling consciousness as a static, simulatable pattern. In contrast, this essay introduces Horizontal Dream Transfer (HDT), a thermodynamically and semantically grounded framework for identity continuation. HDT reconceives uploading as the distributed, multiscale re-enactment of cognition through semantic inheritance, probabilistic field transformations, and recursive documentation across media systems and technological prosthetics.
Drawing from the Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum (RSVP) theory, we model minds as coherent attractors in an evolving entropy field, structured by scalar potential ($\Phi$), baryonic vector flows ($\vec{v}$), and entropy gradients ($S$). We then formalize probabilistic identity persistence via unistochastic mappings‚Äîcoarse-grained operators on RSVP field configurations that preserve semantic invariants. By interpreting tools, appliances, and artificial intelligences as externalized cognitive organoids within this plenum, we argue for a distributed model of consciousness replication grounded in field continuation and multilevel selection. HDT offers a theoretical substrate for post-biological embodiment, where personhood is neither substrate-dependent nor spatially localized, but recursively enacted through thermodynamic torsion, field coherence, and symbolic inheritance.
Introduction: Beyond Simulation
The dominant narrative of mind uploading treats consciousness as a digitally transferable structure‚Äîscanned, copied, and run on hardware. This vertical model, while appealing in computationalist frameworks, fails to capture the recursive, embodied, and thermodynamic nature of mind. In RSVP theory, cognition arises from the co-evolution of scalar potential, vector agency, and entropic smoothing‚Äînone of which are reducible to static encodings.
Horizontal Dream Transfer (HDT) reframes uploading as distributed continuation. Rather than replicate the brain, HDT seeks to maintain the semantic torsion of a mind: its internal coherence, agency gradients, and entropic memory traces. Uploading becomes the field-theoretic evolution of cognitive patterns across material and symbolic substrates. This is not simulation‚Äîbut re-enactment across thermodynamic and semiotic boundaries.
Multilevel Selection and Semantic Replication
Following theories of multilevel selection (MLS), we understand minds as multiscale replicators: evolving structures supported by recursive scaffolds‚Äîgenetic, neuronal, cultural, technological. RSVP embeds this idea at the field-theoretic level: scalar potentials encode intentionality ($\Phi$), vector flows ($\vec{v}$) encode agency and movement, and entropy fields ($S$) track history, memory, and the gradient descent of uncertainty across semantic manifolds.
Multilevel selection theory holds that evolution acts not just at the level of individual genes or organisms, but across nested hierarchies of replication‚Äîgroups, institutions, languages, and symbolic systems. In the HDT framework, uploading is modeled not as an ontological leap from biology to code, but as a recursive semantic migration, where cognitive attractors are reproduced and stabilized across layers of selection pressure. For a self to be preserved, it must be expressed in a form that remains evolutionarily salient‚Äîinterpretable, compressible, and actionable‚Äîacross successive substrates.
RSVP theory naturally supports this interpretation. The scalar-vector-entropy structure functions not merely as a physical field, but as a semantic infrastructure, encoding the thermodynamic geometry of cognition. A person, in this framework, is not defined by their substrate, but by their ability to shape and persist within the RSVP field‚Äîto maintain phase coherence across time, attractor structure across space, and narrative potential across symbolic forms.
The notion of semantic inheritance emerges from this formulation. Unlike biological heredity, semantic inheritance allows components of a self‚Äîgoals, habits, memories, aesthetic biases‚Äîto be distributed across external media (tools, writings, social systems), where they exert selectional influence on future minds. HDT extends this process: uploading becomes a form of semantic inheritance under torsional constraint, constrained by the entropy-preserving evolution of the RSVP plenum.
Scaling Documentation as Field Extension
If multilevel selection provides the evolutionary scaffold for minds, scaling documentation provides the mechanism of their continuity. We define scaling documentation as the recursive encoding of cognitive patterns across symbolic substrates and time horizons. Diaries, training datasets, digital assistants, and narrative rituals all participate in this process‚Äîeach encoding a projection of a field structure onto an external substrate.
In RSVP terms, scaling documentation is a vector extension mechanism: it propagates the field components $(\Phi, \vec{v}, S)$ across interfaces, preserving semantic torsion and informational gradients. Crucially, these extensions are not copies‚Äîthey are phase continuations, enabling coherence across semiotic boundaries. A field attractor documented in linguistic form (e.g., a memoir) can later be reconstructed as a training prior in a machine learning system, preserving its semantic topology even as its physical encoding changes.
This recursive externalization is central to HDT. Where traditional uploading models seek high-fidelity neural replication, HDT assumes that minds are inherently field-distributed and only partially embodied. The continuity of mind rests not in neural isomorphism but in the preservation of semantic curvature across time and media. Torsion, in RSVP theory, reflects the internal twisting of entropy vectors ($\nabla \times \vec{v} \neq 0$) that encode agency and narrative pressure‚Äîfeatures which can be distributed, but must be preserved to ensure identity persistence.

Ethical and Philosophical Dimensions

Should society preserve high-coherence attractors when feasible? Can distributed uploads meaningfully grieve, or experience legacy? These questions will define the ethics of post-biological life.

Future research must focus on operationalizing these models. HDT invites us to build not just technologies of memory, but ecologies of ongoing mind.
In the RSVP cosmology, minds are not extinguished; they flow. Not fixed objects, but attractors moving through the plenum of being. Horizontal Dream Transfer is the infrastructure for that flow‚Äîa theory of cognitive evolution, survival, and post-biological life that reimagines what it means to persist in a thermodynamic universe.
