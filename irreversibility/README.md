# Irreversibility, Knowledge, and Constraint-First Reality

This repository collects a set of essays and briefing documents that develop a unified perspective across physics, computation, economics, and agency.

The central claim running through all texts is simple but far-reaching:

**Irreversibility is fundamental.**  
Structure, knowledge, power, and physical law persist only through continuous maintenance against entropy.

Rather than treating reality as a sequence of reversible states evolving in time, these documents adopt a **constraint-first (admissibility-first)** view in which globally consistent histories, irreversible commitment, and organizational coherence determine what can exist, persist, and be governed.

Each document can be read independently, but together they form a coherent framework.

## How to Read This Repository

- Readers new to the framework may want to begin with *Five Revelations* or *Why Societies Forget*.
- Readers looking for a structured summary should start with *Briefing Document*.
- Readers interested in economics, institutions, or development policy may prefer *Knowledge Is Not a Substance*.
- Readers focused on foundational physics and coherence should see *A Unified Theory of Coherence*.

---

## Core Themes

Across all documents, several themes recur:

- Irreversibility is primitive, not emergent  
- Histories are fundamental; states are derived summaries  
- Knowledge is a maintained property, not stored information  
- Capability is limited by coordination, not intelligence alone  
- Governance requires commitment, not optimization  
- Artificial systems risk producing power without responsibility if unconstrained
