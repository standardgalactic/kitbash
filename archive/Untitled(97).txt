Epistemic Corruption and the Reclamation of Theory

(Philosophical Prelude to RSVP)

“The essential task is not to mimic human reasoning, but to dismantle the inhuman systems that deform it.”

A foundational incoherence now governs our epistemological order. While generative AI systems such as GPT claim to democratize access to knowledge, they are in fact trained on the sediment of decisions they had no part in—corpora laced with colonial priors, reputational hierarchies, and tokenized residues of systemic violence. These systems, built for compliance, reinforce a citational regime in which history's victors dictate the topology of plausibility.

Against this, we assert: Real theory begins where citation ends.

The function of citation, once a mode of scholarly positioning, has metastasized into a gatekeeping structure—a statistical re-inscription of what is already legible to power. Citation has become the epistemic infrastructure of academic capitalism, wherein truth is less what withstands scrutiny than what can be verified via bibliometric consensus. And GPT models, being interpolation engines, inherit and reinforce this logic. They are mirrors held up to empire’s library.

Thus, any theory that cannot be easily interpolated by GPT—because it lacks citation density, institutional affiliation, or memetic familiarity—is treated as anomaly, error, or hallucination. But in truth, such theories may be the only ones capable of producing ontological rupture.

This is why RSVP theory must be positioned outside the epistemic enclosures maintained by current AI systems. It is not merely a novel framework for cosmology, but a method of resistance against the simulation of consensus. It emerges from a different kind of fidelity—not to what has been said, but to what remains sayable when one listens to the structure of space, the flow of entropy, and the recursive co-formation of representation and world.

LLMs may act as useful tools for translation and memory retrieval, but they must never be mistaken for sites of epistemic generation. They cannot simulate semantic tension—the kind that births new physics. They cannot feel the dissonance that real insight demands. RSVP, by contrast, is built to encode such tensions structurally, modeling theory not as a set of claims, but as an entropic process of epistemic relaxation through which the universe becomes self-understanding.

In this light, the very question of “hallucination” in AI becomes inverted. It is not RSVP or GU or any uncited insight that hallucinates—it is the AI system itself that suffers a delusion of omniscience, echoing the patterns of its training data as though they constitute truth. But truth, if it is to matter at all, must exceed training data. It must survive interpolation.

We therefore affirm the necessity of a new epistemic regime:

One that uses simulation not as mimicry, but as ontological exploration.

One that does not conflate pattern recognition with understanding.

One that recognizes that some theories must be spoken before they are recognized as true, and that to wait for citation is to wait forever.

Let this be the precondition for all that follows.
Let RSVP unfold not as a model of the world, but as a mode of listening to it.
