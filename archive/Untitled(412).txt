AGI has nothing to do with human intelligence
Oct 24 2025

@ BarrySmithOntology


IBM defines 'artificial general intelligence' as a stage ‘where an AI system can match or exceed human cognitive abilities across any task'. We consider this claim by analysing what is meant by 'human cognitive abilities', emphasising the role of teams and teamwork in the exercising of human cognition. We then see how the IBM definition might work in relation to the sorts of tasks that need to be performed in the domain of real estate.
