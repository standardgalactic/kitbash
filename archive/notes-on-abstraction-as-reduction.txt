This monograph, titled "Abstraction as Reduction: A Unified Account of Evaluation, Structure, and Proof," develops a comprehensive theory arguing that abstraction is fundamentally a process of reduction—a single structural principle that manifests identically across logic, computation, semantics, category theory, neural networks, and physics.

---

Core Thesis

Abstraction is not merely hiding details or creating interfaces; it is the act of reducing internal complexity until a stable, composable form emerges. This reduction is formally equivalent to:

· β-reduction in lambda calculus
· State transitions in Turing machines
· Forward propagation in neural networks
· Collapse operations in the Spherepop Calculus (a geometric process language introduced here)
· Energy minimization in physical systems

The central claim is:

To abstract is to reduce; to reduce is to compute; to compute is to follow an energy-minimizing trajectory in the plenum of meaning.

Key Constructs and Contributions

1. Spherepop Calculus

A geometric process language where:

· Regions represent semantic states.
· Merge combines regions.
· Collapse reduces regions to simpler forms.
  It is shown to becomputationally universal (equivalent to lambda calculus and Turing machines) and naturally encodes Boolean logic, pipelines, categorical structures, and semantic flow.

2. Semantic Manifolds and Fibrations

· Semantic states are points on a manifold \mathcal{M}.
· Spherepop regions are fibers over \mathcal{M}, forming a fibration.
· Computation is geodesic motion on \mathcal{M}; abstraction is fiberwise collapse.

3. Physical Embedding: 5D RSVP-Ising Model

Spherepop computations are embedded into a 5-dimensional Ising model coupled with RSVP (lamphrodynamic) fields (scalar, vector, entropy).

· Computation corresponds to energy descent in this Hamiltonian system.
· Reduction steps are synchronized spin-field transitions across spatial, semantic, and temporal dimensions.

4. Unification Across Domains

The work demonstrates rigorous equivalences between:

· Lambda calculus β-reduction
· Turing machine transitions
· Neural network layer propagation
· Spherepop collapse
· Hamiltonian minimization
· Even elementary arithmetic rules (BEDMAS/PEMDAS) are shown to be instances of Spherepop reduction.

5. Categorical and Geometric Formalization

· Spherepop forms a symmetric monoidal category.
· Abstraction is a monoid homomorphism.
· Semantic DAGs are functors; inference is natural transformation.

6. Extension to Quantum/Unistochastic Systems

· Unistochastic matrices (from quantum transitions) constrain probabilistic interfaces.
· Amplitude-level flows project to semantic reductions, linking quantum coherence to semantic stability.

Philosophical and Ethical Dimensions

Against Phenomenological Reduction

The author argues that phenomenology (Husserl, Heidegger) is not a form of computational abstraction—it seeks to restore detail, not eliminate it. Phenomenology is thus the antithesis of computational abstraction.

Ethics of Abstraction

Abstraction can be ethically dangerous when it:

· Reduces people to data points
· Erases ecological or social context
· Supports bureaucratic or extractive violence
  The text calls for ethical abstraction that remembers what is omitted and remains accountable to the underlying reality.
