\begin{abstract}
This paper formalizes the structure of memeplexes---coherent systems of interdependent beliefs---as collectively autocatalytic sets of theory-laden facts. Drawing from autocatalytic set theory, epistemology, and field-theoretic modeling via the Relativistic Scalar Vector Plenum (RSVP) framework, it develops discrete kinetic equations, spectral criteria for persistence, and a continuum embedding describing entropic and semantic flux. A case study of \textit{The Technological Republic} demonstrates how ideological systems maintain closure through mutual justification and resist disruption by exogenous noise.
\end{abstract}

\begin{narrative}
Human cultures preserve themselves not by the mere replication of symbols but by sustaining webs of mutual intelligibility. Each belief, theory, and narrative provides the catalytic context for others to be interpreted, rehearsed, and maintained. These structures---memeplexes---function like metabolic networks of thought: self-sustaining, adaptive, and resistant to dilution by contradiction. They thrive when the rate of conceptual regeneration exceeds the rate of informational decay.
\end{narrative}

\section*{1. Discrete Model of Memetic Catalysis}

Let each meme \(m_i\) be associated with an activation \(x_i(t)\). Its growth rate depends on catalytic input from other memes and exogenous stimuli \(u_i\):
\[
\dot{x}_i = \beta_i \, \sigma\!\left(\sum_j A_{ij} x_j^\alpha \right) + u_i - \lambda_i x_i,
\]
with \(\sigma(z) = (1 + e^{-z})^{-1}\) and adjacency matrix \(A\).  

A set \(S\) of memes is \emph{collectively autocatalytic} if every \(m_i \in S\) is generated by a catalytic combination of other members of \(S\).  
The spectral criterion for persistence is
\[
\rho(A_{SS}) > \tau = \frac{\lambda}{\beta c}, \quad c=\sigma'(0)=\tfrac{1}{4}.
\]
This follows from the Perron–Frobenius theorem: a positive equilibrium exists when the dominant eigenvalue of the linearized system exceeds zero.

\begin{narrative}
In cognitive terms, this means that a culture endures only when the density of interpretive links within its discourse surpasses a threshold of coherence. Below this threshold, concepts dissipate into noise; above it, meaning self-replicates.
\end{narrative}

\section*{2. Field-Theoretic Embedding (RSVP)}

Continuum variables capture coarse-grained semantic density \(\rho(\mathbf r,t)\), scalar potential \(\Phi\), and entropy \(S\):
\begin{align*}
\partial_t \Phi &= D_\Phi \Delta \Phi - \kappa_\Phi \Phi + \mathcal{F}_\Phi(\Phi,S,\rho),\\
\partial_t S     &= D_S \Delta S + \mathcal{F}_S(\Phi,S,\rho),\\
\partial_t \rho &= -\nabla\!\cdot(\rho \mathbf v) + \mathcal{R}(\Phi,S,\rho) - \Lambda(\Phi,S,\rho), \\
\mathbf v &= -\mu\nabla \Phi.
\end{align*}
Under zero-flux boundaries, the total energy
\(E=\int(\Phi^2+S^2+\rho^2)\,d\mathbf r\)
satisfies \(\dot E\le0\), ensuring stability when all reaction terms are dissipative.

\begin{narrative}
Here, belief networks blur into semantic fields. Meanings drift, merge, and dissipate as gradients in interpretive potential. Ideological stability thus appears as a form of field equilibrium---a stationary distribution of interpretive energy in the plenum of discourse.
\end{narrative}

\section*{3. Case Study: The Technological Republic}

\begin{narrative}
Karp and Zamiska’s \textit{The Technological Republic} exemplifies a two-node autocatalytic loop: faith in technological mastery (\(m_1\)) sustains the belief in inevitable progress (\(m_2\)), which in turn justifies the primacy of mastery. Each meme catalyzes the interpretive environment required for the other’s survival.
\end{narrative}

The reduced two-node dynamics:
\[
\dot{x}_1 = \beta a \sigma(x_2) - \lambda x_1, \qquad
\dot{x}_2 = \beta b \sigma(x_1) - \lambda x_2,
\]
admit sustained activation if \(\sqrt{ab} > \lambda / (\beta c)\).  
Ideological collapse occurs when external shocks increase effective entropy \(S\) such that this inequality is violated.

\section*{4. Simulation of the 3-Node Memetic Cycle}

\begin{narrative}
To visualize persistence and collapse, consider a minimal 3-node cycle where each meme catalyzes the next. When catalytic coupling \(\beta\) falls below threshold, activations decay; above it, they converge to a stable nonzero equilibrium---the simplest model of cultural resonance.
\end{narrative}

\section*{5. Discussion and Implications}

\begin{narrative}
The autocatalytic model reframes ideology as a kinetic phenomenon: belief systems persist not by veracity but by maintaining a supercritical density of mutual implication. The threshold between subcritical decay and supercritical convergence defines the boundary between skepticism and dogma, between cultural entropy and coherence.
\end{narrative}
