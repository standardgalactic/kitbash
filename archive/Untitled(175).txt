The Ark Anthology

A surreal–political fable universe based on Giordano Bruno’s lost allegory.

Series Overview

The Ark Anthology is a serialized metafable in which hierarchy, rebellion, cosmology, and world-geometry intertwine. The story is framed through Bruno's allegorical animal world on Noah’s Ark, where donkeys toil, foxes administer, lions rule symbolically, and Lorenzo the Llama mediates the tensions between them. Across the season, the Ark destabilizes under the weight of new ideas, suppressed rebellions, and dimensional insight.

Major Themes

Hierarchy as geometry

Mediation as structural complicity

Rebellion drained by systemic curvature

Closed-world cosmology versus multiplicity

Narrative scripts as mechanisms of control

Imagination as a world-breaking force

World Architecture

The Ark is not a mere ship; it's a constrained socio-narrative manifold whose curvature enforces hierarchy. Decks form gradient strata from donkeys up through foxes, lions, Noah, and ultimately the Bureau of Arks beyond reality. Deck 0, the hidden layer, absorbs suppressed revolt energy.
