

Introducing MAGI: Manifold-Aligned Generative Inference

A unified framework asserting that generative models succeed by restricting their dynamics to the low-dimensional semantic manifold of data. The core principle is the No-Noise Prediction Theorem: semantically aligned models must have zero update component in the normal directions to the manifold.

This work integrates differential geometry, Morse theory, and sheaf theory to explain the success of clean-prediction models (like JiT) and the instability of noise-predicting models, framing hallucination as a geometric misalignment.
