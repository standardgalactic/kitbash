\section*{Appendix B: Mathematical Form of the CLIO Variational Principle} \subsection*{B.1 Variational Objective and Constraints} Let $\Phi(\mathbf{x},t)$ denote scalar semantic potential, $\mathbf{v}(\mathbf{x},t)$ the vector flow (syntactic/behavioral expression), and $S(\mathbf{x},t)$ the entropy density. Generation and recognition are represented by an adjoint pair $\mathcal{R} : \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{sem}} \to \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{obs}}$ and $\mathcal{R}^\ast : \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{obs}} \to \mathcal{M}_{\mathrm{sem}}$. We define the CLIO variational problem over a time window $[0,T]$: \begin{equation} \label{eq:clio-functional} \mathcal{J}[\Phi,\mathbf{v},S] = \int_{0}^{T} \!\!\int_{\Omega} \Big( \underbrace{\tfrac{1}{2}\|\mathbf{v}-\mathcal{R}[\Phi,S]\|^2}_{\text{projection mismatch}} + \underbrace{\tfrac{\lambda}{2}\, (S - S^\ast)^2}_{\text{target entropy}} + \underbrace{\tfrac{\alpha}{2}\|\partial_t \Phi\|^2 + \tfrac{\beta}{2}\|\partial_t \mathbf{v}\|^2}_{\text{inertial/regularization}} \Big)\, d\mathbf{x}\, dt, \end{equation} subject to RSVP coupling constraints (as soft penalties or hard constraints): \begin{align} \label{eq:rsvp-coupling-1} \partial_t \Phi &= -\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v} + \mathcal{H}(\Phi,\mathbf{v},S),\\ \label{eq:rsvp-coupling-2} \partial_t S &= -\Phi \,\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v}. \end{align} Here $S^\ast$ is the entropy level of coherent interpretation; $\alpha,\beta,\lambda>0$ tune temporal smoothness and tolerance for uncertainty. \subsection*{B.2 Euler--Lagrange and Adjoint Conditions} Introduce Lagrange multipliers $\pi_\Phi,\pi_S$ for the RSVP constraints \eqref{eq:rsvp-coupling-1}--\eqref{eq:rsvp-coupling-2} and define the augmented action $\widetilde{\mathcal{J}} = \mathcal{J} + \int\!\!\int \pi_\Phi(\partial_t \Phi + \nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v}-\mathcal{H}) + \pi_S(\partial_t S + \Phi \nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v})$. Stationarity $\delta \widetilde{\mathcal{J}}=0$ yields: \begin{align} \label{eq:EL-v} \mathbf{v} - \mathcal{R}[\Phi,S] - \beta\,\partial_{tt}\mathbf{v} + \nabla \pi_\Phi + \Phi\,\nabla \pi_S &= 0, \\ \label{eq:EL-Phi} -\mathcal{R}'_{\Phi}[\Phi,S]^{\!\top}(\mathbf{v}-\mathcal{R}[\Phi,S]) - \alpha\,\partial_{tt}\Phi + \partial_t \pi_\Phi - \pi_S \,\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v} + \partial_{\Phi}\mathcal{H}\,\pi_\Phi &= 0, \\ \label{eq:EL-S} -\lambda (S - S^\ast) - \mathcal{R}'_{S}[\Phi,S]^{\!\top}(\mathbf{v}-\mathcal{R}[\Phi,S]) + \partial_t \pi_S + \partial_{S}\mathcal{H}\,\pi_\Phi &= 0, \end{align} together with the forward RSVP dynamics \eqref{eq:rsvp-coupling-1}--\eqref{eq:rsvp-coupling-2} and boundary/terminal conditions (e.g., natural or fixed end-states for $(\Phi,\mathbf{v},S)$ and adjoints $(\pi_\Phi,\pi_S)$). Equations \eqref{eq:EL-v}--\eqref{eq:EL-S} encode the CLIO adjunction: $\mathcal{R}$ drives generation (projection), while $\mathcal{R}^\ast$ appears via $\mathcal{R}'[\cdot]^{\!\top}$ in the recognition gradients, pulling observations back into the semantic field. \subsection*{B.3 Linear Stability and Uncertainty Oscillations} Let $(\Phi^\star,\mathbf{v}^\star,S^\star)$ be a steady state (recognition equilibrium) satisfying $\mathbf{v}^\star=\mathcal{R}[\Phi^\star,S^\star]$ and $\partial_t S^\star=0$. Linearize around the equilibrium by setting $\delta\Phi=\Phi-\Phi^\star$, $\delta\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{v}-\mathbf{v}^\star$, $\delta S=S-S^\star$. To first order, \eqref{eq:EL-v} becomes a damped second-order operator on $\delta\mathbf{v}$: \begin{equation} \label{eq:lin-v} \beta\,\partial_{tt}\delta\mathbf{v} + \underbrace{\Big(I - \mathcal{R}'[\Phi^\star,S^\star]\Big)}_{=: \mathsf{K}_v}\,\delta\mathbf{v} + \underbrace{\Big(-\mathcal{R}'_{\Phi}\,\delta\Phi - \mathcal{R}'_{S}\,\delta S + \nabla \delta\pi_\Phi + \Phi^\star \nabla \delta\pi_S\Big)}_{=: \mathsf{C}_{v\Phi S}[\delta\Phi,\delta S]}=0, \end{equation} while the coupled $(\delta\Phi,\delta S)$ obey a similar second-order system driven by $\delta\mathbf{v}$ (from \eqref{eq:EL-Phi}--\eqref{eq:EL-S} and the RSVP constraints). Neglecting weak cross-couplings for intuition, the dominant modes satisfy \begin{equation} \beta\,\partial_{tt}\delta\mathbf{v} + \mathsf{K}_v\,\delta\mathbf{v} \approx 0, \qquad \alpha\,\partial_{tt}\delta\Phi + \mathsf{K}_\Phi\,\delta\Phi \approx 0, \end{equation} with stiffness operators $\mathsf{K}_v,\mathsf{K}_\Phi$ given by the local Jacobians of the projection and RSVP couplings. Hence the perturbations oscillate with modal frequencies \begin{equation} \omega_v \;\approx\; \sqrt{\lambda_{\min}(\mathsf{K}_v)/\beta}, \qquad \omega_\Phi \;\approx\; \sqrt{\lambda_{\min}(\mathsf{K}_\Phi)/\alpha}. \end{equation} \textbf{Interpretation.} These underdamped oscillations are the \emph{uncertainty oscillations} observed in CLIO: confidence (inverse uncertainty) lags as the system traverses curvature (Hessian) of the recognition landscape. High curvature (stiff $\mathsf{K}$) produces faster, low-amplitude oscillations; shallow curvature produces slower, larger swings. \subsection*{B.4 Torsion and Cross--Frequency Coupling} Define the torsion field $\mathbf{T} := \nabla \times \mathbf{v} - \nabla \Phi$. Linearizing gives \begin{equation} \partial_t \mathbf{T} \;\approx\; \nabla \times \partial_t \delta\mathbf{v} - \nabla \partial_t \delta\Phi, \end{equation} so the coupled second--order dynamics yield \emph{carrier} oscillations in $\delta\mathbf{v}$ (gamma-like) modulated by \emph{envelope} oscillations in $\delta\Phi$ (theta-like), naturally producing cross-frequency coupling signatures (theta–gamma) as the torsion relaxes toward zero. This recovers empirical oscillatory markers of recognition and matches CLIO’s reported confidence cycles. \subsection*{B.5 Discrete (Patom/Graph) Form} For a graph of patoms $i=1,\dots,N$ with neighborhood $\mathcal{N}(i)$ and phase/amplitude states $(\phi_i, \mathbf{v}_i)$, a discrete analogue of \eqref{eq:clio-functional} reads \begin{equation} \mathcal{J}_{\mathrm{disc}} = \sum_{t}\sum_{i} \Big( \tfrac{1}{2}\|\mathbf{v}_i - \mathcal{R}_i(\phi_i,S_i)\|^2 + \tfrac{\lambda}{2}(S_i-S_i^\ast)^2 + \tfrac{\alpha}{2}\|\Delta_t \phi_i\|^2 + \tfrac{\beta}{2}\|\Delta_t \mathbf{v}_i\|^2 + \tfrac{\kappa}{2}\!\sum_{j\in\mathcal{N}(i)} \|\phi_i-\phi_j\|^2 \Big), \end{equation} with Euler–Lagrange updates yielding coupled, damped Kuramoto–type oscillators: \begin{equation} \beta\,\Delta_{tt}\mathbf{v}_i + \mathbf{v}_i - \mathcal{R}_i(\phi_i,S_i) + \cdots = 0, \qquad \alpha\,\Delta_{tt}\phi_i + \kappa\!\sum_{j\in\mathcal{N}(i)}(\phi_i-\phi_j) + \cdots = 0. \end{equation} Consensus ($\phi_i \approx \phi_j$) reflects layered agreement; residual phase slips correspond to localized torsion and elevated uncertainty. \subsection*{B.6 Practical Diagnostics for CLIO Systems} Define an \emph{uncertainty observable} \begin{equation} U(t) \;:=\; \int_{\Omega} \Big( \|\mathbf{v}-\mathcal{R}[\Phi,S]\|^2 + \lambda (S-S^\ast)^2 \Big)\, d\mathbf{x}, \end{equation} and a \emph{torsion energy} \begin{equation} \mathcal{T}(t) \;:=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\int_{\Omega} \|\nabla \times \mathbf{v} - \nabla \Phi\|^2\, d\mathbf{x}. \end{equation} Near equilibrium, $U(t)$ and $\mathcal{T}(t)$ exhibit damped oscillations with modal frequencies $(\omega_v,\omega_\Phi)$ predicted above; convergence of both to constants indicates successful recognition (entropy stationarity $\partial_t S \!\to\! 0$). Peaks in $\dot{U}(t)$ align with phase resets (e.g., hippocampal theta), providing a principled trigger for CLIO’s “in-situ” intervention steps. \subsection*{B.7 Summary} The CLIO loop is the adjoint variational solution of the RSVP recognition problem: minimizing projection mismatch and entropy subject to scalar–vector coupling. Its characteristic uncertainty oscillations arise from underdamped second–order dynamics governed by the local curvature of the recognition landscape. In continuous fields this appears as torsion relaxation with cross-frequency coupling; in discrete patom graphs it appears as phase consensus in coupled oscillators. Both regimes converge when $\mathcal{R}^\ast \mathcal{R}\Phi \approx \Phi$ and $\mathbf{T}\to 0$.
