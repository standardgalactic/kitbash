\sectionhead{Logline}
In 12th-century C\'ordoba, a philosopher--judge confronts a wave of apparent miracles that threaten to dissolve law, medicine, and reason itself; when he uncovers that the signs are engineered by the state to manage belief, he must decide whether clarity is worth exile, disgrace, and the loss of everything he loves.

\sectionhead{Screenplay}
This document is formatted as a readable, production-facing screenplay draft in \LaTeX. Scene headings follow standard conventions. Action is written for the camera and sound to carry philosophical meaning without explanatory weight.

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{1. EXT. C\'ORDOBA --- COURTYARD OF A MEDICAL SCHOOL --- DAY}

\action{Midday light cuts hard edges into white stone. The air is hot, dry, and exact. In the courtyard, a low table stands under a thin shade cloth. A brass basin of water reflects a slice of sky with clinical indifference.}

\action{IBN RUSHD (AVERROES), early 60s, ink-stained fingertips, physician's composure, sits with a patient. His posture is precise, not gentle. His attention is gentleness.}

\action{A STUDENT circle forms: Muslim, Jewish, Christian, a few too young to shave, some older with tradesman hands. In the periphery, a hooded figure in a student djellaba watches closely. This is A\`ISHA BINT RUSHD, disguised, stillness practiced.}

\action{Averroes takes the patient's wrist. The sound of pulse is not literal, but we hear his internal count as a faint rhythmic emphasis, more felt than heard. He releases. He looks at the basin, at the light, at the patient's breath.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{to the students}
\dialogue{Do not ask first what God intends. Ask what the body is doing. Intention is not a substitute for observation.}

\action{A student, eager, leans forward.}

\character{STUDENT}
\dialogue{Master, is it not arrogant to speak as if the body obeys rules?}

\action{Averroes does not react with irritation. He reacts with the patience of someone who has seen this question injure people.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Arrogance is to pretend one sees without looking. The body is a text. If you refuse to read it, you will call your ignorance piety and your negligence humility.}

\action{He lifts a small knife, not threatening, merely a tool, and opens a swollen abscess with a practiced incision. The patient flinches, then exhales. Dark fluid drains. The courtyard holds its breath, then resumes.}

\action{A\`isha watches the procedure with a steadiness that is too mature. She does not watch for drama. She watches for method.}

\action{As the wound is cleaned, the camera finds a clepsydra in a corner: a simple water clock in an alcove, drip and measure. It is part of the environment, as natural as shadow.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{2. INT. COURT OF LAW --- HALL OF JURISTS --- DAY}

\action{A different chamber, same light. Geometry everywhere: carved arches, repeating patterns. The room is full, but quiet. The air smells of paper, sweat, and restraint.}

\action{Averroes now sits as QADI, Chief Judge. His hands are the same hands. Ink, medicine, law. On one side, a BUILDER with lime dust in his beard; on the other, a LABORER with an arm bound and bruised.}

\action{A collapsed wall has injured the laborer. Testimony hangs on causality: negligence or misfortune, poor mortar or sudden wind, rushed work or bad stone.}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{The wall fell because God willed it. Who am I to argue with the decree?}

\action{The phrase lands softly, like a blanket offered to responsibility. A murmur of approval in the audience.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{God wills that we act with knowledge. When a wall falls, it falls by the weight of stone and the weakness of what binds it. If you build with sand where lime is required, do you call the collapse a miracle?}

\action{A JUNIOR JURIST, sharp-eyed, rises with the careful courage of someone speaking where speech is dangerous.}

\character{JUNIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{Master, if you say the wall must fall from weakness, do you not bind the Creator to a chain of necessity?}

\action{The room stills. The clepsydra is not present here, but the same rhythm appears as a faint emphasis in the silence.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{To say that a thing has a nature is not to deny its Maker. It is to affirm that creation is not a jest. If nothing follows from anything, then judgment is theatre and law is noise.}

\action{He turns back to the builder, not cruel, just exact.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Bring the mortar. Bring the stones. We will examine what you used. If you did not know, you are negligent. If you knew and did it anyway, you are culpable. God is not insulted by evidence.}

\action{The builder swallows. The laborer exhales. In the audience, a few faces harden: not disagreement yet, but the first sensation of being deprived of a comforting explanation.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{3. EXT. GREAT MOSQUE --- ARCaded COURTYARD --- LATE AFTERNOON}

\action{Crowds spill from shade into light. The sound is layered: footsteps, murmurs, distant recitation, water in channels.}

\action{At the center of the marble expanse, a large iron BRAZIER has been prepared. Coals glow. The heat is visible in a slight shimmer.}

\action{A CONDEMNED MAN stands before the brazier, wrists bound. Officials read a sentence. The crowd is not bloodthirsty; it is hungry for moral reassurance.}

\action{Averroes is present among jurists, not presiding, observing. A\`isha is there as well, still disguised.}

\action{The condemned man is made to step near the flame, closer than safety permits. A guard pushes him. His tunic is heavy linen, perhaps. Ordinary enough.}

\action{The flame catches the hem. We hear the expected whisper of cloth and fire. It begins. Then, abruptly, it does not proceed. The hem blackens a little, then stops. The man cries out, but not in pain. In fear, in shock.}

\action{The crowd surges. Some shout praise. Some weep. A preacher, unknown to Averroes, lifts his hands.}

\character{PREACHER}
\dialogue{Do you see? He burns whom He wills, and preserves whom He wills. Fire has no law. It has only the command.}

\action{Averroes steps forward, not to interrupt, but to look. He looks at the tunic's drape, at the way it hangs. It is wrong, but only to someone who has handled many textiles.}

\action{A\`isha looks at faces, not at the brazier. She registers relief, the way a crowd leans into an answer that removes burden.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{4. INT. INFIRMARY --- PRIVATE ROOM --- NIGHT}

\action{Oil lamps. Low light. A calm enclosure. The condemned man lies on a mat, trembling. A guard waits outside.}

\action{Averroes examines him as a physician, not a judge. He lifts the tunic carefully. Beneath the line where cloth covered, the skin shows small blisters, not severe, but real, and not consistent with a divine suspension of fire.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{softly}
\dialogue{Tell me what you wore. Tell me what they gave you.}

\action{The man avoids his eyes.}

\character{CONDEMNED MAN}
\dialogue{They said it was a mercy. They said it would not burn. They said I would live.}

\action{Averroes touches the fabric between thumb and forefinger. He feels weight. He listens for rustle. He hears almost nothing.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Who said it.}

\action{A knock. Hard. A guard enters without waiting for permission.}

\character{GUARD}
\dialogue{Enough. The Council requests your presence tomorrow. There is talk in the streets.}

\action{Averroes holds the tunic for a moment longer, then lets it fall back. The lamp flame flickers. The room feels smaller.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{5. EXT. CITY SQUARE --- DUSK}

\action{The light turns amber. Shadows lengthen into corridors. The crowd has gathered around a raised platform.}

\action{The ASH\textquotesingle ARITE PROSECUTOR, late 40s, austere white robe, persuasive calm, stands like a man who has made peace with uncertainty by declaring it holy. He does not look at the crowd to win them. He looks above them, as if speaking upward.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{You have been told that the world is a machine of causes. That if you know the parts, you can predict the outcome. But what is that machine, if not a cage built around the Living Will?}

\action{Faces lift. The rhetoric does not instruct; it relieves.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{If fire burns because it must, then prayer is decoration. If the sick recover because bark and blade insist, then mercy is a name we give to necessity. But the Merciful is not necessity. He is freedom.}

\action{A murmur of assent. Not all hostile, but all warmed by the idea that unpredictability is intimacy.}

\action{In the back, Averroes watches. He does not show anger. He shows fatigue, the kind born of recognizing a rival method: not falsity, but a competing comfort.}

\action{A\`isha watches the crowd. She notices how people hold each other more tightly as the Prosecutor speaks, as if the doctrine itself were a blanket.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{6. INT. LIBRARY --- NIGHT}

\action{A chamber of shelves, shadowed alcoves, and careful quiet. A\`isha enters alone, still disguised. She lights a small lamp.}

\action{She opens Aristotle in Arabic translation. She copies with speed and accuracy. Her hand is steady. She pauses at a line where a translator has softened a causal claim into something vaguer, safer.}

\action{She hesitates, then corrects the word. The ink is dark, decisive. A tiny act of treason against ambiguity.}

\action{A LIBRARIAN appears from the shadows, older, cautious.}

\character{LIBRARIAN}
\dialogue{Do you know what you risk when you correct a text?}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{I risk being wrong.}

\character{LIBRARIAN}
\dialogue{No. You risk being precise. Precision attracts attention. Attention attracts men who do not read.}

\action{A\`isha closes the book gently, as if to protect it from the room itself.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{If we remove the bones from the argument, it becomes a story. Stories are easy to burn.}

\action{The librarian does not answer. He only extinguishes a second lamp in the far corner, making the room darker, safer, and smaller.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{7. EXT. IRRIGATION CHANNEL --- MARKET DISTRICT --- DAY}

\action{A bright morning. Market noise. Baskets, cloth, shouting, bargaining. Water runs in a stone channel beside the stalls, a mundane civic miracle.}

\action{Then it begins to flow the wrong way. Not violently, not like fantasy, but enough that anyone watching feels the subtle nausea of contradiction.}

\action{Women cry out. Men laugh in disbelief, then stop laughing. The Prosecutor's followers arrive quickly, too quickly. They point. They narrate.}

\character{FOLLOWER}
\dialogue{Do you see? Habit is a veil. The Lord lifts it when He wills.}

\action{Averroes arrives late, pushed through a crowd. He kneels by the channel. He touches the water. He looks along the stone line as if reading a sentence.}

\action{He hears, faintly, the drip of a water clock that is not present. The film supplies the rhythm as a question.}

\action{The flow returns to normal before he can trace the full line. The crowd treats the return not as correction but as closure. They have received their sign.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{8. INT. PRIVATE STUDY --- DAY}

\action{A room inside Averroes' house. Papers piled. A writing board. A small brazier. The clepsydra is present in an alcove, drip and measure. Its sound is calm, but now it reads like a deadline.}

\action{Averroes writes. The title line is formed: \emph{Tah\={a}fut al-Tah\={a}fut}. The Incoherence of the Incoherence. His hand is not inspired; it is disciplined.}

\action{He pauses, not from doubt, but from awareness of the city pressing in. He dips the pen again. He writes as if building a wall against a tide.}

\action{A\`isha enters silently with a ceramic jar, careful not to spill. She sets it down. Inside is a strip of cloth, folded. Ordinary enough.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{This was left near the brazier after the punishment. No one claimed it.}

\action{Averroes does not touch it immediately. He looks at it the way one looks at an insect one suspects carries a fever.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{They have begun to manufacture God.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{Or to manufacture the people.}

\action{Averroes finally lifts the cloth. It hangs wrong. The sound is too quiet. The clepsydra continues: drip, measure, drip, measure.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{If the city can be made to doubt the reliability of fire, it can be made to doubt anything. It will not be a city of law. It will be a city of interpretation.}

\action{A\`isha watches him, not pleading, not arguing yet. She is learning what kind of war this is.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{END OF PART I}

\slug{FADE OUT.}

\sectionhead{Screenplay — Part II}

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{9. INT. AVERROES' STUDY --- CONTINUOUS --- NIGHT}

\action{The same room as before, but later. Lamps burn lower. The clepsydra continues its patient drip.}

\action{Averroes writes, pauses, crosses out a sentence, rewrites it with greater austerity. This is not revision for elegance, but for necessity.}

\action{A\`isha sits opposite him, copying a passage from Aristotle onto a separate sheet, her handwriting slightly tighter than his.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{You are answering him as if the city were still listening.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I am answering him as if the argument were immortal. Cities close their ears. Texts do not.}

\action{She hesitates, then chooses to speak.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{Do you remember what you told me about Fez?}

\action{Averroes looks up. The question catches him not emotionally, but temporally.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Which telling?}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{About the woman who built a school because she believed knowledge needed a home sturdier than any ruler.}

\action{Averroes leans back. The clepsydra drips.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Fatima al-Fihri. Yes. She founded al-Qarawiyyin with inheritance money. Not for prestige. For continuity.}

\action{He gestures at the walls, the shelves, the city beyond.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{When princes change their minds, buildings remain. When doctrines fracture, institutions carry memory forward.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{And they still teach there. Law. Medicine. Astronomy.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{They teach that learning is not a spectacle. It is a discipline that survives rulers by refusing to serve them directly.}

\action{A\`isha looks at the manuscript between them.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{Then why not leave? Why stay and argue with men who have already decided they want miracles instead of syllabi?}

\action{Averroes does not answer immediately.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Because Córdoba was built to be such a place. If we abandon it without resistance, we concede that the only future for learning is exile.}

\action{He returns to the page.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{And because arguments written under threat are often the ones that travel farthest.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{10. INT. JEWISH OPTICIAN'S WORKSHOP --- DAY}

\action{A narrow room flooded with angled light. Bronze mirrors, glass lenses, astrolabes. Everything is orderly, dangerous, precise.}

\action{The OPTICIAN, older, hands stained with polish and resin, adjusts a concave mirror. He treats it with professional intimacy.}

\action{Averroes stands nearby, watching the reflection of the window detach and reform as the mirror tilts.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{This mirror can show you the heavens or make a fool believe an angel has landed in the square.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{The difference being the crowd.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{The difference being intent.}

\action{A flare of light dances briefly on the wall, forming a shape that looks almost purposeful, then dissolves.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{I have been asked to lend this mirror to the Governor's men. I said no. They asked again, this time with smiles.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{And you still said no.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{I said that mirrors do not lie. People do. They did not appreciate the distinction.}

\action{Averroes watches the mirror one last time. Its reflections shift subtly, never stable.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{In Bologna, they argue about law as if it were geometry.}

\action{The Optician raises an eyebrow.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{Italy?}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Yes. A place where jurists gather and call themselves a universitas. A corporation of minds. They believe that argument itself can be an institution.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{Does it work?}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{For now.}

\action{A pause.}

\character{OPTICIAN}
\dialogue{Will it work here?}

\action{Averroes does not answer. He does not need to.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{11. EXT. ALCAZAR --- CALIPH'S PRIVATE GARDEN --- NIGHT}

\action{Engineered calm. Water channels. Lemon trees. Moonlight filtered through leaves.}

\action{The CALIPH stands alone. Averroes approaches. The clepsydra's rhythm returns faintly, now tense.}

\character{CALIPH}
\dialogue{They say you wish to turn Córdoba into Bologna.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I wish to keep it from becoming a shrine to confusion.}

\character{CALIPH}
\dialogue{Fez can afford endurance. Bologna can afford debate. I must afford obedience.}

\action{The Caliph turns, weary.}

\character{CALIPH}
\dialogue{You think institutions preserve truth. I think they preserve habits. The people no longer trust habits. They want signs.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Signs decay. Habits build.}

\character{CALIPH}
\dialogue{Habits also rebel.}

\action{Silence. Water moves through channels.}

\character{CALIPH}
\dialogue{Write if you must. Teach if you can. But understand this: the city will not follow you into a world where God behaves predictably.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Then the city will have to learn elsewhere.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{12. INT. GOVERNOR'S CHANCELLERY --- NIGHT}

\action{Ledgers. Maps. The concave mirror now wrapped in cloth, catalogued.}

\action{The SECRETARY marks a schedule.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{Fez produces scholars. Bologna produces lawyers. Córdoba produces arguments. Arguments are unstable.}

\action{He gestures to a timetable.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{Tomorrow, we produce certainty.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{FADE OUT.}

\sectionhead{Screenplay — Part III}

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{13. EXT. EASTERN GATE --- MORNING}

\action{The gate is massive, Roman stone repurposed by centuries. Morning light catches dust in the air. A crowd gathers with anticipatory quiet, not excitement. They have learned how to wait for wonder.}

\action{The ASHʿARITE PROSECUTOR stands among them, not presiding, but witnessing. His expression is composed, yet a hairline fracture has appeared—an attentiveness that borders on suspicion.}

\action{A MERCHANT approaches a fountain near the gate. He holds an empty palm aloft.}

\character{MERCHANT}
\dialogue{By God who provides, I ask for relief from debt.}

\action{The merchant dips his hand into the water. When he lifts it, coins glint wetly in his palm.}

\action{A gasp ripples outward. Knees bend. Some weep.}

\action{The Prosecutor steps closer than he has before. He watches not the coins, but the merchant’s eyes, the angle of his wrist, the timing of the crowd’s intake of breath.}

\action{As the merchant turns away too quickly, the Prosecutor steps forward—and his foot catches on something.}

\action{A faint metallic sound: not a ring, but a duller note.}

\action{He looks down. Between the stones lies a misshapen lead slug, one face stamped, the other blank, attached to a thin gut-string that vanishes into the fountain’s shadow.}

\action{The sound of the crowd fades. We hear only the Prosecutor’s breathing and, faintly, the drip of water that sounds suddenly like counting.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{14. INT. GOVERNOR’S CHANCELLERY --- LATE NIGHT}

\action{Maps, ledgers, oil lamps. The room hums with administrative purpose. The SECRETARY packs documents into a chest.}

\action{The Prosecutor stands in the doorway, the counterfeit coin clenched in his fist. His robe is disordered, his certainty frayed.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{I found this at the gate.}

\action{He drops the coin onto a ledger. It lands with a dead sound.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{The world is untidy. Even miracles leave scraps.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{Do not mock what you pretend to serve. I saw your men. They were not praying. They were counting.}

\action{The Secretary closes the chest calmly.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{You believe you are defending God. I am defending the city. We are not the same profession.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{You are making a puppet of the Almighty.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{I am making certainty. The people cannot live in the “perhaps” of philosophers. They need the “is.”}

\action{He steps closer, voice low.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{If you tell them the coins were tricks and the water was valves, you will not free them. You will leave them alone with their hunger.}

\action{The Prosecutor’s face tightens. His faith is not shattered; it is cornered.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{If the miracle is a lie, then what we protect is hollow.}

\character{SECRETARY}
\dialogue{Then pray harder. Or look away. Both are acceptable.}

\action{The Secretary sweeps the coin into a refuse bucket without ceremony.}

\action{The Prosecutor leaves. The bucket remains. The ledger remains. The city’s theology has been itemized.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{15. INT. AVERROES’ STUDY --- NIGHT}

\action{Stacks of parchment. The brazier glows low. The clepsydra drips more loudly now, as if the room were smaller.}

\action{Averroes reads a draft, grimaces, and feeds it into the fire. The paper curls, blackens, vanishes.}

\action{A\`isha watches from the doorway, holding a satchel.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{That was persuasive.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{That is why it must die.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{Persuasion is how texts survive long enough to be understood.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Persuasion is how tricks survive long enough to become traditions.}

\action{A\`isha steps forward and retrieves a half-burned page with tongs, stamping out sparks.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{Then I will carry the drafts you refuse to save. Someone must bridge the distance between clarity and hunger.}

\action{Averroes looks at her—really looks—and sees not defiance, but strategy.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{You are preparing for exile.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{I am preparing for survival.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{16. EXT. CÓRDOBA STREETS --- NIGHT}

\action{Averroes walks alone. Shuttered schools. Chained library doors. Smoke carries the chemical tang of ink and skin.}

\action{He enters an INFIRMARY courtyard.}

\slug{INT. INFIRMARY --- CONTINUOUS}

\action{ZAYNAB, a senior physician, works over a fevered child. A blessed stone rests uselessly on the girl’s chest.}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{They tell me touching her pulse interferes with God’s test.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{And the fever?}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{They say it is a sign. Signs do not respond to willow bark.}

\action{She lowers her voice.}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{They asked today whether I taught your daughter her letters.}

\action{Averroes stiffens.}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{The city is retreating from explanation. When that happens, it looks for bodies to blame.}

\action{She meets his eyes.}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{Leave. Take her north. Fez endures because it does not argue with hunger. Bologna endures because it argues with everything. Córdoba wants miracles.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{17. EXT. GREAT MOSQUE --- COURTYARD --- DAY}

\action{The public disputation. The brazier again. The mineral cloth prepared, folded, plausible.}

\action{Averroes stands opposite the Prosecutor. A\`isha watches from the edge.}

\action{Averroes calls witnesses. Weaver. Dyer. Builder. Procedure replaces rhetoric.}

\action{Ordinary linen chars and smokes. The mineral cloth does not. Silence where crackle should be.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{You see? Even now He answers arrogance with mercy.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Look closer.}

\action{Water poured onto the cloth. No steam through the weave.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{It is stone spun into thread. It does not burn because it has already endured greater heat.}

\action{Averroes lifts the cloth from the fire with bare hands. The crowd recoils.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{The miracle is a dyer’s secret and a politician’s coin.}

\action{The Prosecutor’s face collapses inward.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{If this is a trick… then the sign is gone.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{The world remains.}

\action{No cheer. Only embarrassment. People look away.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{18. EXT. AVERROES’ HOUSE --- DUSK}

\action{Red dye marks the door. Silence where neighbors once greeted.}

\action{Inside, Zaynab helps A\`isha pack. Efficiency without sentiment.}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{You murdered comfort. They will never forgive you for that.}

\action{Guards appear at the street’s end.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{19. EXT. REAR ALLEY --- NIGHT}

\action{Averroes and A\`isha slip away with a single satchel. Firelight rises behind them—ordinary fire, obedient to wind.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{20. EXT. PROVINCIAL TOWN --- DAY}

\action{Averroes teaches a handful of bored youths. They ask whether philosophy will help them advance.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{No.}

\action{They leave.}

\transition{DISSOLVE TO:}

\sectionhead{Screenplay — Part IV (Integration and Coda)}

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{26. EXT. PROVINCIAL HOUSE --- NIGHT}

\action{A modest dwelling. No geometry, no marble, no ornament. Wind moves dust across packed earth.}

\action{Inside, AVERROES lies on a low bed. His breathing is shallow but measured, as if even now he refuses drama.}

\action{A\`ISHA sits beside him, older now, her disguise abandoned. The satchel rests against the wall, patched and repaired many times.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Do you hear it?}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{The wind?}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{No. The same thing I heard in Córdoba.}

\action{Silence. Then, faintly, a rhythmic sound enters—not literal, but familiar. A hammer striking iron somewhere beyond the house.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Even here.}

\action{A\`isha understands. She does not respond.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I wanted the argument to be unassailable. I forgot that arguments do not travel alone.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{They never have. They travel with hunger, trade, fear, and accident. That is not corruption. It is logistics.}

\action{Averroes smiles faintly.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{You sound like a merchant.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{Fatima al-Fihri used inheritance money to build a school. That was commerce too.}

\action{Averroes closes his eyes.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Then promise me this.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{What?}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{If they argue over my words, let them. If they misunderstand me, let them. But do not let them forget that I believed the world could be known without diminishing God.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\dialogue{They will not remember that.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Then they will rediscover it.}

\action{His breathing slows. Then stops. No music. No emphasis. The wind continues.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{27. EXT. UNIVERSITY OF PARIS --- DAWN}

\action{Cold light. Stone walls emerging from shadow. Bells ring for morning lecture.}

\action{Students move through courtyards carrying books, arguing as they walk. Their breath is visible in the air.}

\action{Inside a lecture hall, the REGENT MASTER writes on a slate: \emph{Causa et Effectus}.}

\character{REGENT MASTER}
\dialogue{If the effect is severed from the cause, the intellect has no ladder by which to ascend.}

\action{Pens scratch. The sound echoes.}

\action{A\`ISHA, now elderly, stands at the threshold. She does not enter. She listens.}

\action{Beside her, on a bench, rests the Arabic manuscript. Its pages are brittle, ink fading, margins scarred by travel.}

\action{She opens it once more, tracing a line with her finger.}

\character{A\`ISHA}
\parenthetical{to herself}
\dialogue{You survived badly. That is how truth survives.}

\action{She closes the book and leaves it on the bench.}

\action{The camera lingers. A student sits, notices the unfamiliar script, frowns, then returns to his Latin notes.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{28. EXT. BLACKSMITH’S FORGE --- CONTINUOUS}

\action{Iron is struck. STRIKE. STRIKE. STRIKE.}

\action{Sparks fly. Heat radiates. No miracle. Only work.}

\action{The sound carries outward, merging with the murmur of the city, the bells, the scratching of pens.}

\transition{FADE OUT.}

\slug{END}

\sectionhead{Final Note}

This concluding movement closes the loop without resolution-as-vindication. Averroes does not triumph; he persists indirectly. The institutions of learning outlive the city that rejected them, not because they are purer, but because they are adaptable. The film ends not with certainty restored, but with causality resumed as background infrastructure—no longer debated, simply used.
