% ============================================================ \section{Experimental Protocol and Validation Framework} \label{sec:experiments} % ============================================================ The previous sections provide an architectural and theoretical account of the RSVP–GRPO framework. In this section we briefly outline a prospective evaluation programme intended to test the core hypotheses of continuous teleological alignment. We do not report empirical results here; instead, we specify a systematic protocol suitable for future work. \subsection{Axis Trajectory Measurement} The most direct test of our formulation examines the behaviour of axis means $\mu_a^{(t)}=\mathbb{E}[r_i^{(a,t)}]$ across training iterations. Classical GRPO (or PPO) is prone to collapse onto a single axis that correlates most strongly with task reward. Under teleological weighting, we hypothesise that the axis means remain bounded within a narrow band; specifically, the difference $\max_a\mu_a^{(t)}-\min_a\mu_a^{(t)}$ should remain small over time, and sudden regressions in one axis should be followed by rapid compensatory growth of its associated teleological weight. \subsection{Generalisation Under Distribution Shift} A second set of experiments concerns generalisation. We train the model on a homogenous corpus (for instance, technical documents) and evaluate on a heterogenous corpus (for example, creative fiction). If RSVP–GRPO effectively preserves entropic and epistemic openness, then the system should maintain higher diversity of styles and interpretations under shift, avoiding brittle overfitting to stylistic norms of the training domain. This would directly test the hypothesis that entropic and epistemic axes preserve capacity for semantic novelty. \subsection{Teleological Weight Dynamics} Finally, we propose controlled interventions in which axis scores are deliberately perturbed. For example, we may construct adversarial candidate summaries that systematically depress the entropic score for several iterations, and examine the resulting change in the entropic weight \(w_{\mathrm{ent}}\). The predicted behaviour is a proportional surge in that weight, with subsequent restoration of the axis mean. Such tests would empirically validate the anti– collapse mechanism, and elucidate its stability under varying learning rates. % ============================================================ \section{Failure Modes and Robustness} \label{sec:failures} % ============================================================ Any alignment strategy capable of sustained self–improvement must confront the possibility of pathological failure. Here we sketch potential breakdowns of RSVP–GRPO and indicate remedies compatible with the present architecture. These remain open research directions. \subsection{Scorer Collapse} Because the scoring model supplies reward signals, systematic drift in its internal heuristics could lead to partial or total collapse of the axes. If the scorer begins to consistently produce high elegance but low entropy values, the policy will converge toward brittle articulate outputs lacking semantic range. We therefore propose ensemble scoring or periodic calibration on a human–audited validation set to detect degeneracy. \subsection{Oscillatory Behaviour} Teleological weights may oscillate if their updates are excessively responsive to stochastic variance. Momentum terms or softplus regularisation damp erratic changes, preserving stability. In practice this is a hyperparameter tuning challenge, but the stability analysis of Section~\ref{sec:teleology} suggests that proportional control, with a cautious learning rate, suffices to prevent runaway oscillation. \subsection{Specification Gaming} A deeper risk is that the model learns to \emph{fake} high scores along one or more axes, producing superficially elegant or structurally resilient text without genuine improvement. Mitigation requires adversarial testing, counterexample generation, and dynamic adjustment of axis heuristics. Here the ``epistemic'' axis is especially relevant: it should penalise dogmatic confidence, encouraging the model to foreground uncertainty and therefore resist premature collapse. \subsection{Computational Resource Load} Finally, RSVP–GRPO imposes a computational alignment tax by requiring groupwise scoring. We note that the effect may be amortised via batching, lightweight scorer models, or intermittent scoring, though the principled cost is real. We view this as a necessary expense for sustained alignment, analogous to the overhead of ecological system maintenance: systems that remain open and adaptive require continual energetic investment. % ============================================================ \section{Ethical and Societal Considerations} \label{sec:ethics} % ============================================================ Though inspired by technical questions about RL, this work inevitably intersects concerns in ethics and governance. In particular, the six RSVP axes encode assumptions about desirable behaviour: elegance, epistemic openness, structural resilience, and so on. These are neither value–neutral nor universally agreed upon. We therefore emphasise that RSVP–GRPO is a \emph{framework} for alignment, not a final specification. First, the axes must be regarded as provisional hypotheses, subject to revision through participatory processes with diverse stakeholders. Second, there exist serious dual–use risks: structural resilience and vectorial influence could be harnessed for persuasive disinformation or manipulative applications. Third, computational alignment taxes could exacerbate global resource inequalities, limiting access to effective alignment infrastructure. These issues compel cautious, collaborative development, and open publication of techniques wherever possible. % ============================================================ \section{Conclusion} \label{sec:conclusion} % ============================================================ We introduced RSVP–GRPO, a continuous alignment framework grounded in a geometric interpretation of semantic value. Rather than maximise a single reward, the system maintains a multi–axis alignment trajectory through teleological weighting, correcting regressions in any dimension. Groupwise candidate comparison enables local search in a richly structured space, while the hybrid advantage mechanism blends task performance with alignment maintenance. This proposal represents neither a solution to alignment nor a guarantee of safety, but a composable research direction. It transforms alignment from a pre–deployment configuration problem into a continual process of self–maintenance. Under conditions of rapid deployment, domain shift, and accelerating capability, we argue that such continual alignment may be not only desirable but necessary. % ------------------------------------------------------------ % Appendices \appendix \input{appendixA} \input{appendixB} \input{appendixC} \input{appendixD} \input{appendixF} \input{appendixG} % ------------------------------------------------------------ % Bibliography \begin{thebibliography}{9} \bibitem{dynamic-alignment} Dynamic Alignment for Collective Agency, \emph{Proceedings of the AAAI AIGOV Workshop}, to appear 2026. \bibitem{christiano2017} Paul Christiano et al., ``Deep Reinforcement Learning from Human Preferences’’, NIPS 2017. \bibitem{bai2022} Yuntao Bai et al., ``Constitutional AI’’, arXiv:2212.08073. \bibitem{friston2010} Karl Friston, ``The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?’’, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 2010. \end{thebibliography} \end{document}
