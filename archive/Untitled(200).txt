Early platforms functioned catalytically: they lowered transaction costs, reduced friction between agents, and enabled decentralized participation. Contemporary dominant platforms, by contrast, operate extractively. They do not merely facilitate exchange; they deepen curvature around themselves, siphoning surplus through positional control of infrastructure and predictive leverage over users. This paper reformulates that shift in geometric and thermodynamic terms. We treat the informational substrate as a manifold $(\mathcal{M}, g)$, and we model platform dominance as curvature concentration within that manifold. Extraction is not merely unfair distribution; it is a phase instability characterized by geodesic convergence and entropy misallocation. PlenumHub is introduced as a substrate-level response. Instead of relying on exogenous regulatory correction, it embeds structural invariants directly into the compute layer. The unit of knowledge becomes a typed semantic sphere, evolution is bounded by entropy budgets, value is governed by conservation laws of coherence, and global validity is enforced via sheaf-theoretic closure. The core claim is that structural antitrust can be implemented algebraically. If extraction corresponds to negative diffusion and curvature concentration, then a properly constrained substrate can preclude those regimes by design. \section{Definitions and Formal Objects} We collect here the principal formal objects used throughout the paper. \begin{definition}[Information Manifold] An information substrate is a smooth manifold $(\mathcal{M}, g)$, where $\mathcal{M}$ is a differentiable manifold and $g$ is a Riemannian or pseudo-Riemannian metric encoding informational distance between states. \end{definition} \begin{definition}[RSVP Scalar and Vector Fields] Let $\phi : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a scalar potential field representing accumulated informational density or leverage. Let $\mathbf{A}$ be a smooth vector field on $\mathcal{M}$ representing directed flows of semantic evolution or agent trajectories. \end{definition} \begin{definition}[Semantic Sphere] A semantic sphere is a tuple \[ \sigma = (I, T, M, E, S), \] where $I$ is a content-hash bound identity in a set $\mathcal{I}$, $T$ is a finite set of modality types, $M: T \to \mathcal{V}$ assigns payloads to modalities in a value space $\mathcal{V}$, $E \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$ is semantic entropy, and $S$ is a finite directed acyclic graph encoding provenance. \end{definition} \begin{definition}[Execution Trace] An execution trace is a finite sequence of rule applications \[ \tau = r_1; r_2; \dots; r_n, \] where each rule $r_i$ is a partial function $r_i : \Sigma \to \Sigma$ on the space $\Sigma$ of semantic spheres. \end{definition} \begin{definition}[Entropy Budget] Each rule $r$ carries a declared entropy budget $\epsilon_r \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$. For a trace $\tau$, cumulative entropy potential is \[ \Phi(\tau) = \sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_{r_i}. \] \end{definition} \begin{definition}[Texture and Time Valuation] Texture valuation is \[ TC(\sigma) = H(\sigma) - \sum_{i \neq j} MI(M(k_i), M(k_j)), \] where $H$ is Shannon entropy over modality payload distributions and $MI$ denotes mutual information between modalities. Time valuation is \[ TiC(\sigma) = \sum_{r_i \in S} e^{-\lambda (t_{\mathrm{now}} - t_i)} q(r_i), \] with decay constant $\lambda > 0$ and quality metric $q$. \end{definition} \section{Formalizing RSVP Geometry} The geometric language of curvature and scalar wells must be made precise. Let $(\mathcal{M}, g)$ be the information manifold. Curvature is characterized by the Riemann curvature tensor $R$, Ricci tensor $\mathrm{Ric}$, and sectional curvature $K$. \begin{definition}[Curvature Concentration] A region $U \subset \mathcal{M}$ exhibits curvature concentration if sectional curvature satisfies \[ K(\Pi) > \kappa_0 > 0 \] for a non-negligible family of two-planes $\Pi \subset T_x\mathcal{M}$, $x \in U$, or equivalently if $\mathrm{Ric}$ has positive eigenvalues exceeding a threshold. \end{definition} \begin{definition}[Scalar Well] A scalar well is a region where the Laplacian of the scalar field satisfies \[ \Delta \phi(x) > 0 \] for $x \in U$, indicating local accumulation of potential. \end{definition} \begin{proposition}[Geodesic Convergence under Positive Curvature] Let $\gamma_s(t)$ be a one-parameter family of geodesics with separation vector $J(t)$ satisfying the Jacobi equation. If sectional curvature $K > 0$ in $U$, then $J(t)$ exhibits convergence, implying geodesic focusing. \end{proposition} \begin{proof}[Sketch] The Jacobi equation is \[ \frac{D^2 J}{dt^2} + R(J, \dot{\gamma})\dot{\gamma} = 0. \] If $K>0$, then the curvature term induces restoring forces driving $J$ toward zero. Hence initially diverging geodesics reconverge, producing focusing effects. \end{proof} Interpretationally, extractive platforms increase $\phi$ locally such that $\Delta \phi > 0$, inducing curvature concentration. Agent trajectories, modeled as geodesics of $(\mathcal{M}, g)$ perturbed by $\nabla \phi$, converge toward the platform center. This provides a geometric account of herding and manifold collapse. \section{Entropy Soundness} We now formalize entropy evolution. Let $E(\sigma)$ be Shannon entropy over the joint distribution of modality payloads and provenance structure. Formally, let $P_\sigma$ be the induced probability distribution over tokens, symbols, or semantic states encoded in $M$ and $S$, and define \[ E(\sigma) = - \sum_{x} P_\sigma(x) \log P_\sigma(x). \] \begin{theorem}[Entropy Soundness] For any execution trace $\tau = r_1; \dots; r_n$ applied to $\sigma_0$ yielding $\sigma_n$, if each rule $r_i$ satisfies \[ E(r_i(\sigma)) \leq E(\sigma) + \epsilon_{r_i}, \] then \[ E(\sigma_n) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \Phi(\tau). \] \end{theorem} \begin{proof} Proceed by induction on $n$. Base case $n=0$ is trivial. Assume the bound holds for $n-1$. Let $\sigma_{n-1}$ satisfy \[ E(\sigma_{n-1}) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \epsilon_{r_i}. \] Then by rule constraint, \[ E(\sigma_n) = E(r_n(\sigma_{n-1})) \leq E(\sigma_{n-1}) + \epsilon_{r_n}. \] Substituting yields \[ E(\sigma_n) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_{r_i}. \] \end{proof} \begin{remark} The bound is non-vacuous only if $\epsilon_r$ budgets are constrained by governance rules. Unbounded budgets collapse the invariant. Therefore entropy budgets must be auditable, bounded, and staked. \end{remark} \section{Sheaf-Theoretic Closure} We next formalize semantic closure. Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a poset of semantic contexts. Define a presheaf $\mathcal{F} : \mathcal{C}^{op} \to \mathbf{Set}$ assigning to each context $U$ the set of semantic assignments $M(U)$. \begin{definition}[Sheaf Condition] $\mathcal{F}$ is a sheaf if for any covering $\{U_i\}$ of $U$: \[ M(U) \cong \left\{ (s_i) \in \prod_i M(U_i) \;\middle|\; s_i|_{U_i \cap U_j} = s_j|_{U_i \cap U_j} \right\}. \] \end{definition} This condition enforces that local semantic coherence implies unique global coherence. No agent can present contradictory modality assignments across contexts without violating the gluing axiom. \section{Limitations} The geometric model abstracts informational dynamics as smooth manifolds, whereas real platforms operate on discrete graphs. The entropy budget mechanism requires trusted enforcement and cannot prevent collusive inflation if governance is compromised. The welfare properties of the crystal economy are conjectural; full Nash equilibrium characterization remains open. Finally, the manifold collapse argument, while formally grounded in curvature analysis, depends on modeling assumptions about informational metrics that require empirical calibration. \end{document}
