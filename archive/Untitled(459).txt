extension of the \emph{Relativistic Scalar–Vector Plenum} (RSVP) framework. It offers a universal account of coherence---bridging cosmology, cognition, and computation---by treating the local emergence, interaction, and dissolution of structured regions (``pops'') as morphisms in a higher differential category of entropic fields. Each spherepop is a bounded negentropic region embedded in the plenum, equipped with scalar potential $\Phi$, flow $\vv$, and local entropy density $S$. Linear differentials are replaced by a \emph{radial-coherence derivative} $\dP$, tensor products by an \emph{entropy-preserving merge} $\muo$, and additive integrals by a \emph{nested-pop integral}. We formulate the geometry (curvature flow), topology (cobordisms), algebraic topology (pop homology/cohomology), and quantization (BV--AKSZ) of this calculus, and we derive renormalization-style \emph{coherence flow equations} whose fixed points explain persistent structures across scales. The thesis is simple: \emph{coherence is conserved through transformation}. Spherepop Calculus is the resulting language of recursive coherence. % --- Table of Contents (optional; will still show numbers inside ToC text) --- \tableofcontents \thispagestyle{empty} % ========================================================= % Chapter 1: Mathematical Introduction % ========================================================= \chapter{Mathematical Introduction} \section{The plenum as base category} We begin with the RSVP plenum $\Pl$, a derived smooth space supporting the scalar--vector--entropy triad $(\Phi,\vv,S)$. The scalar $\Phi:\Pl\to\mathbb{R}$ is a potential of coherence, $\vv:T\Pl\to T\Pl$ encodes flow, and $S$ is local entropy density (Jacobson (1995); Verlinde (2011)). In our formalism, a \emph{spherepop} is a local morphism of derived stacks $f:\Sph_r\to\Pl$, where $\Sph_r$ is a shifted derived sphere with symplectic inheritance in the sense of PTVV (PTVV (2013)). \section{Pop derivative} We define the \emph{pop derivative} as the curvature-weighted radial change of $\Phi$ along spherical embeddings: \begin{equation} \dP \Phi \;=\; \lim_{\epsilon\to 0}\frac{\Phi(\Sph_{r+\epsilon})-\Phi(\Sph_r)}{\epsilon} \;=\; (\nabla\cdot \mathbf{n})\,\partial_r \Phi \;+\; \vv\cdot\nabla\Phi. \label{eq:pop-derivative} \end{equation} Here $\mathbf{n}$ is the outward normal on the pop boundary. Positive $\dP\Phi$ indicates emergent coherence; negative values signal dissolution. \section{Merge product and entropy constraint} Given $\Phi_1,\Phi_2$ on overlapping pops, the \emph{merge} $\muo$ is defined by a coherence-preserving convolution \begin{equation} (\Phi_1\,\muo\,\Phi_2)(x)\;=\;\int_{\Sph_{12}} w(x,y)\,\Phi_1(y)\Phi_2(y)\,dV_y, \label{eq:merge} \end{equation} with kernel $w$ respecting entropy flux. Categorically, $\muo:\Sph\times\Sph\to\Sph$ is monoidal, associative up to a homotopy encoding global conservation $dS_{\text{total}}=0$ (Baez and Dolan (1995); Mac~Lane (1978)). \section{Pop integral} Global coherence is reconstructed by nested integration over spherical boundaries: \begin{equation} \popint \Phi \;=\; \sum_{r_i}\int_{\Sph_{r_i}}\Phi\,dA_{r_i}. \label{eq:pop-integral} \end{equation} This “pop integral’’ is RSVP’s counterpart to spacetime integration, weighted by boundary entropy. % ========================================================= % Chapter 2: Visual and Geometric Interpretation % ========================================================= \chapter{Visual and Geometric Interpretation} \section{Geometry of a pop} A pop is a bubble of negentropy nucleating in the plenum: curvature concentrates, $\Phi$ steepens across a thin membrane, and $\vv$ circulates tangentially. The human instinct to ``pop bubbles’’ in play mirrors a primal cognitive behavior: \emph{visual foraging} seeks high-curvature, high-surprise loci in the field of view, continually rediscovering the same act of coherence-seeking. \section{Merge and dissolve} When two pops overlap with aligned gradients, the merge $\muo$ yields constructive interference of $\Phi$; opposing gradients yield dissolution and entropy radiation. These are geometric versions of monoidal product and inverse morphism. \section{Curvature flow and the pop derivative} Concentric shells visualize \eqref{eq:pop-derivative}; positive flow expands coherence, negative collapses it. This is a mean-curvature flow modulated by RSVP dynamics (Arnol'd (1992)). \section{The coherence foam} Iterated pops, merges, and dissolves form a dynamic tessellation---a \emph{coherence foam}. RSVP’s “entropic smoothing’’ arises as the macroscopic envelope of these micro-events. % ========================================================= % Chapter 3: Topological Foundations % ========================================================= \chapter{Topological Foundations} \section{Cobordisms of coherence} Abstracting from metric data, each pop becomes a cobordism between sphere boundaries; merges are topological gluings, dissolves are orientation-cancellations. The resulting category $\mathsf{Cob}^{\mathrm{pop}}_n$ is monoidal under disjoint union and differential under entropy-preserving currents $d\sigma=0$ (Freed (1992); Schommer-Pries (2011)). \section{Orientation and duality} Reversing the pop orientation $(\Phi,\vv,S)\mapsto(\Phi,-\vv,-S)$ yields a negentropic dual; dissolving is composition with the dual. \section{Derived cobordisms} Lifting to the tangent $\infty$-topos, spherepops become derived stacks $\mathcal{S}_r=\mathrm{Map}(\Sph_r,\Pl)$ with shifted symplectic forms (PTVV (2013); Lurie (2009); Gaitsgory and Rozenblyum (2017)). The differential relations \begin{equation} \dP^2=0,\qquad dS=0 \label{eq:topo-relations} \end{equation} encode coherence continuity. \section{Pop homology} Define a chain complex $(C^{\mathrm{pop}}_\bullet,\dP)$ with $k$-chains representing $k$-dimensional coherence. Homology classes $H_k^{\mathrm{pop}}$ are persistent structures: $H_0$ (entities), $H_1$ (interaction loops), $H_2$ (membranes) (Hatcher (2002); May (1999)). % ========================================================= % Chapter 4: Homological and Cohomological Formalism % ========================================================= \chapter{Homological and Cohomological Formalism} \section{Chains, boundaries, persistence} The boundary operator $\dP:C^{\mathrm{pop}}_k\to C^{\mathrm{pop}}_{k-1}$ satisfies $\dP^2=0$. Pop homology \begin{equation} H_k^{\mathrm{pop}}=\ker(\dP)/\mathrm{im}(\dP) \end{equation} captures what persists through transformation. \section{Entropy as a cocycle} Entropy is a cochain $S\in C^k_{\mathrm{pop}}=\mathrm{Hom}(C_k^{\mathrm{pop}},\mathbb{R})$ with coboundary $\delta_P S=S\circ \dP$. Coherence conservation is $\delta_P S=0$, i.e. $S$ closed. \section{Pairing and pop integral} Homology--cohomology pairing yields the pop integral: \begin{equation} \langle[\Phi],[S]\rangle \;=\; \int_{\mathrm{pop}}\Phi, \end{equation} interpreting every stable structure as carrying an entropic meaning. \section{Merge as cup product} Merges define a graded-commutative cup product $\smile_\circ$ on cochains, making $(C^\bullet_{\mathrm{pop}},\smile_\circ,\delta_P)$ a DGA, the algebraic shadow of the monoidal geometry (Kelly (1982)). \section{Pop Laplacian} Define the Laplacian \begin{equation} \Delta_P\;=\;\dP\,\delta_P+\delta_P\,\dP, \label{eq:pop-laplacian} \end{equation} whose eigenmodes $\Delta_P\Phi_\lambda=\lambda \Phi_\lambda$ reveal the coherence spectrum. % ========================================================= % Chapter 5: Derived Quantization (BV–AKSZ) % ========================================================= \chapter{Derived Quantization and BV--AKSZ Formulation} \section{Shifted symplectic structure} Following PTVV (2013), the derived mapping stack of pop fields carries a $(-1)$-shifted symplectic form \begin{equation} \omega_{\mathrm{pop}}=\int_{\Sph}\delta\Phi\wedge \delta\vv, \end{equation} inducing a BV bracket and operator (Alexandrov et al. This is the quantum analogue of $dS=0$ (Costello and Gwilliam (2016–2021)). \section{Gauge symmetry and BV fields} Reparameterizations of coherence boundaries yield ghosts $c$ and antifields. The BRST differential acts by $s\Phi=\mathcal{L}_c\Phi$, $s\vv=[c,\vv]$, $sc=-\tfrac12[c,c]$ with $s^2=0$. \section{Partition function} The quantum partition function integrates over coherence geometries: \begin{equation} Z=\int e^{\tfrac{i}{\hbar}S_{\mathrm{AKSZ}}}\,\mathcal{D}\Phi\,\mathcal{D}\vv\,\mathcal{D}c\,\mathcal{D}\Phi^*\,\mathcal{D}\vv^*\,\mathcal{D}c^*. \end{equation} % ========================================================= % Chapter 6: Quantized Observables and Derived Feynman Graphs % ========================================================= \chapter{Quantized Observables and Derived Feynman Graphs} \section{Graphs of interaction} Stationary-phase expansion organizes contributions by derived Feynman graphs $\Gamma$; vertices encode merges, edges propagate entropy influence.
