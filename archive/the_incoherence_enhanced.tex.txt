% --- LOGLINE ---
\sectionhead{LOGLINE}

\action{In 12th-century Córdoba, philosopher-physician Ibn Rushd (Averroes) confronts a series of apparent miracles that threaten the foundations of rational medicine and law. When he discovers the "signs" are being manufactured by the state to control public belief, he must choose between speaking the truth that will cost him everything—or protecting the institutions he has built by remaining silent.}

\vspace{1em}

\sectionhead{THEMES}

\action{THE INCOHERENCE explores the tension between faith and reason, institutional power and intellectual honesty, and the vulnerability of knowledge in times of political crisis. It examines how authority uses spectacle to replace argument, and how ideas survive through exile, translation, and accident rather than triumph.}

\clearpage

% --- BEGIN SCREENPLAY ---

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{1\quad EXT. CÓRDOBA -- MEDICAL SCHOOL COURTYARD -- DAY}

\action{Midday. The courtyard is flooded with white Andalusian light that throws hard-edged shadows across limestone paving. Heat shimmers above the stones.}

\action{A low wooden table stands beneath a canvas awning stretched between two columns. On the table: brass instruments, ceramic bowls, strips of clean linen, a shallow basin filled with water that reflects sky like polished metal.}

\action{IBN RUSHD—AVERROES—sits cross-legged on a cushion. Early sixties, lean, with the careful hands of someone who has spent decades holding scalpels and pens with equal precision. His fingers are permanently stained with ink and henna. His eyes are patient but exacting.}

\action{Before him, a PATIENT—male, forties, laborer's build—sits with his tunic pulled aside, exposing a swollen shoulder joint, skin taut and discolored.}

\action{Around the table, a semicircle of STUDENTS: twelve in all, mixed ages and backgrounds. Muslim, Jewish, Christian. Some are barely old enough to grow beards. Others have the weathered hands of former tradesmen returning to learning late in life.}

\action{Among them, seated at the back, is a figure in a plain student's djellaba, hood drawn low. This is A'ISHA BINT RUSHD, nineteen, disguised. Her stillness is practiced, deliberate. She does not fidget. She does not look away when the others do.}

\action{Averroes takes the patient's wrist between thumb and forefinger. He closes his eyes. The courtyard quiets. We hear:}

\action{The faint rhythmic pulse beneath skin. Not literal sound, but suggested—perhaps a barely audible percussion, or simply the visual rhythm of Averroes' breathing syncing with what he feels.}

\action{He releases the wrist. Opens his eyes. Looks at the basin of water, then at the angle of shadow on the wall, measuring time without a clock.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{to the students, measured}
\dialogue{The pulse is fast but regular. The shoulder is hot to touch, which means the humors are gathering. The skin is red, not black. This tells us the inflammation is active, not yet corrupt.}

\action{A young STUDENT—eager, sharp-featured—leans forward.}

\character{STUDENT 1}
\dialogue{Master, how do we know the humors are the cause and not... something else?}

\action{Averroes does not react with impatience. He treats the question as though it were made of glass.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{What else would you propose?}

\character{STUDENT 1}
\dialogue{Some say illness is a test. That the body's suffering is... ordained.}

\action{A murmur among the other students. Some nod. Others look uncomfortable.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{gently, but without compromise}
\dialogue{If you believe that, you may leave this table and pray. That is an honorable path. But if you stay, you will learn to observe what is before you, not what you wish were true.}

\beat

\dialogue{The body is not mysterious. It is complex. These are not the same thing.}

\action{He turns back to the patient, lifts a small iron lancet from the table. He holds it up so the students can see.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{This tool does not know whether you are pious. It does not care whether you are a prince or a beggar. It will cut equally. That is not cruelty. That is reliability.}

\action{He makes a small, precise incision at the edge of the swelling. The patient winces but does not cry out. Dark fluid—not blood, but something thicker, amber-tinged—begins to drain.}

\action{The students watch. Some lean in. One turns away.}

\action{A'isha watches with a focus that is different from the others. She is not watching for drama. She is watching for method—how Averroes positions the blade, the angle of entry, the depth of the cut.}

\action{As the fluid drains into a bronze cup, the camera finds, in a shadowed alcove at the courtyard's edge:}

\action{A CLEPSYDRA—a water clock. Simple, elegant. Water drips from one vessel into another at a measured rate. Drip. Drip. Drip. It is part of the architecture, unremarkable, but suddenly foregrounded by sound.}

\action{The dripping becomes rhythmic, almost musical—a pulse beneath the scene.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{as he works}
\dialogue{When you see a wound like this, you do not ask why God permitted it. You ask: what is the wound doing? Is it healing or worsening? Is the body responding or failing to respond?}

\beat

\dialogue{To call this arrogance is to misunderstand what medicine is. Medicine is not philosophy. It is carpentry.}

\action{He finishes draining the abscess, wipes the blade clean, presses a linen compress to the wound.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{to the patient}
\dialogue{Keep this clean. Change the dressing twice daily. If the redness spreads beyond this line—}

\action{He traces a finger around the shoulder.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{—return immediately.}

\action{The patient nods, rises carefully, and is helped away by an assistant.}

\action{The students begin to disperse. A'isha remains seated, watching her father pack the instruments with the same care he used to unpack them.}

\action{Averroes notices her stillness. He does not acknowledge it directly, but there is a flicker—recognition, perhaps concern.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{2\quad INT. COURT OF LAW -- HALL OF JURISTS -- DAY}

\action{A different chamber. Same quality of light, but filtered through high clerestory windows that cast geometric patterns across the floor—interlocking stars and polygons, Islamic tessellation made architectural.}

\action{The room is long, narrow. Walls lined with shelves holding bound volumes, scrolls, legal precedents. The air smells of vellum, sweat, and stone dust.}

\action{The space is full but controlled. Perhaps forty people: LITIGANTS, WITNESSES, OBSERVERS, scribes. They sit on cushions or stand along the walls. No one speaks above a murmur.}

\action{At the far end, seated on a slightly raised platform: AVERROES, now in the role of QADI—Chief Judge. Same hands, same face, different context. He wears a dark robe and white turban, simple but formal.}

\action{Before him stand two men:}

\action{THE BUILDER—fifties, thick-set, with lime dust permanently embedded in the creases of his hands and the folds of his clothing. He shifts his weight, uncomfortable under scrutiny.}

\action{THE LABORER—thirties, wiry, with one arm bound in a sling. His face is tight with pain and anger held in check.}

\action{Between them, on a wooden tray, lie pieces of rubble: broken stone, fragments of mortar, a splintered wooden beam.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{to the laborer}
\dialogue{Describe what happened. Be specific.}

\character{LABORER}
\dialogue{I was working on the wall. Third course up from the foundation. The mortar had been laid the day before. I was setting the next stone when the whole section gave way.}

\beat

\dialogue{Fell on my arm. Broke in two places.}

\action{He gestures with his good hand, miming the collapse.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{to the builder}
\dialogue{What mortar did you use?}

\action{The builder hesitates. Not defiance—calculation.}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{Lime and sand. Standard mixture.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{What proportion?}

\character{BUILDER}
\parenthetical{too quickly}
\dialogue{Three parts sand to one part lime.}

\action{Averroes picks up a fragment of mortar from the tray. He holds it between thumb and forefinger, rubs it gently. It crumbles too easily.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{This is not three to one.}

\action{A ripple of attention through the room. The scribes lean forward.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{This is four to one. Maybe five. You added more sand to save money on lime.}

\action{The builder's face hardens.}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{The wall fell because God willed it. I cannot control what the Almighty decrees.}

\action{The phrase lands softly, like a curtain dropped over the proceedings. Several observers nod. The murmur of approval is quiet but unmistakable.}

\action{Averroes sets the mortar fragment down carefully.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{not angry, but immovable}
\dialogue{God wills that a wall built with proper mortar stands. God wills that a wall built with sand and wishes falls. These are not the same will.}

\beat

\dialogue{If I build a house with straw and call the fire that consumes it a divine mystery, am I pious or am I a fool?}

\action{A JUNIOR JURIST—late twenties, sharp eyes, carefully trimmed beard—rises from his seat along the wall. He speaks with the cautious precision of someone who knows this is dangerous ground.}

\character{JUNIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{Master Qadi, with respect—}

\beat

\dialogue{If you say the wall \emph{must} fall when the mortar is weak, do you not bind the Creator to a chain of necessity? Do you not make Him a prisoner of His own creation?}

\action{The room goes still. The question is not rhetorical. It is a test.}

\action{Averroes looks at the junior jurist for a long moment. The silence stretches. Somewhere, faintly, we hear the drip of water—not the clepsydra, which is not present here, but perhaps a fountain in an adjacent courtyard. The rhythm is the same.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{To say that a thing has a nature is not to deny its Creator. It is to say that the Creator made it with purpose, not caprice.}

\beat

\dialogue{If fire does not burn, then fire is a word without meaning. If walls do not fall when poorly built, then there is no such thing as a good builder or a bad one. There is only luck.}

\beat

\dialogue{And if that is true, then this court is theater. Justice is theater. We are all performing for an audience that does not exist.}

\action{He turns back to the builder.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{Bring me the sand you used. Bring me the lime. If you cannot tell me where they came from, you are negligent. If you \emph{can} tell me and still chose to weaken the mixture, you are culpable.}

\beat

\dialogue{In either case, you will compensate this man for his injury and for the work he has lost.}

\action{The builder's jaw tightens. He does not argue. He simply bows stiffly and steps back.}

\action{The laborer exhales—relief mixed with something else. Vindication, perhaps, but also unease. He has won, but the terms of victory feel precarious.}

\action{In the audience, a few faces have hardened. Not open hostility, but the first stirrings of it. The look of people who have been deprived of a comfortable explanation.}

\action{The junior jurist sits back down, his expression unreadable.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{3\quad EXT. GREAT MOSQUE -- ARCADED COURTYARD -- LATE AFTERNOON}

\action{The courtyard is vast—white marble paving, rows of horseshoe arches casting repeating shadows, a forest of columns. The space is designed to make human scale feel both small and deliberate.}

\action{Crowds spill from the interior of the mosque into the open air. The sound is layered: footsteps echoing on stone, low murmurs of conversation, distant recitation from within, the trickle of water in the ablution channels that run along the courtyard's edges.}

\action{At the center of the marble expanse, a large iron BRAZIER has been set up. It is elevated on a stone platform so that everyone can see. Coals glow red-orange beneath a lattice of iron bars. The heat is visible in the air—a shimmer, like the courtyard itself is breathing.}

\action{Surrounding the brazier: a crowd of perhaps two hundred. Merchants, students, laborers, women in veils, children held on shoulders. They are not bloodthirsty. They are waiting for moral clarity.}

\action{Near the brazier, flanked by GUARDS in simple tunics and leather vests, stands the CONDEMNED MAN—mid-thirties, thin, face pale with fear but also resignation. His wrists are bound with rope. His feet are bare.}

\action{A COURT OFFICIAL—older, grave—reads from a scroll in Arabic. His voice is formal, detached.}

\character{COURT OFFICIAL}
\dialogue{...for the crime of theft from a public granary in a time of need, the sentence is execution by fire, unless God in His mercy intervenes.}

\action{The phrase "unless God intervenes" is not a formality. It is weight-bearing architecture. The crowd leans on it.}

\action{Averroes stands among a group of JURISTS and WITNESSES near the edge of the crowd. He is observing, not presiding. His face is neutral, but his eyes are active—cataloging, measuring.}

\action{A'isha is present as well, still in her disguise, standing in a cluster of younger students. She watches not the condemned man, but the crowd.}

\action{The guards move the condemned man closer to the brazier. One of them—a thick-shouldered man with a professional demeanor—pushes him forward with a firm hand.}

\action{The condemned man stumbles, rights himself. He is now close enough that the heat must be unbearable. His tunic—heavy, undyed linen—hangs loosely on his frame.}

\action{A guard uses a long iron rod to push the hem of the tunic toward the coals.}

\action{The fabric touches the edge of the fire.}

\action{We hear the expected sound: a faint \emph{whoomph} as flame begins to take. The hem blackens. Smoke rises.}

\action{And then—}

\action{It stops.}

\action{The flame does not spread. The fabric does not curl and consume. It blackens at the edge, smolders faintly, and then... nothing. The fire refuses to climb.}

\action{The condemned man gasps—not in pain, but in shock. He stumbles backward, staring at his own tunic as though it has betrayed him.}

\action{The crowd surges. Voices erupt:}

\character{VOICE IN CROWD 1}
\dialogue{A sign! God has spared him!}

\character{VOICE IN CROWD 2}
\dialogue{Blessed be the Merciful!}

\action{Some people weep. Others fall to their knees. The guards step back, uncertain.}

\action{From the edge of the crowd, a man steps forward—a PREACHER, forties, dressed in white robes, with the commanding presence of someone accustomed to being heard. His voice cuts through the noise.}

\character{PREACHER}
\dialogue{Do you see? Do you see what your reason cannot grasp?}

\beat

\dialogue{Fire does not burn because it \emph{must}. It burns because He commands it. And when He withholds that command, the flame dies.}

\beat

\dialogue{This man was condemned by your laws. But God's mercy is not bound by mortar and stone. It is free. It is alive. It is \emph{now}.}

\action{The crowd's approval is palpable—a wave of relief, joy, vindication.}

\action{Averroes does not move. He watches the condemned man's tunic. The way it hangs. The way it moves—or rather, doesn't move—in the wind.}

\action{A'isha watches the crowd. She registers the relief on their faces. The way people reach for each other, hold each other. She sees what her father sees, but she also sees what he sometimes misses: the need this moment is fulfilling.}

\action{The preacher continues, hands raised.}

\character{PREACHER \contd}
\dialogue{Let this be a lesson. Habit is a veil. Necessity is a prison. But God lifts the veil when He wills, and the prison becomes a garden.}

\action{More cheers. The condemned man is helped away by the guards—spared, not by the court, but by spectacle.}

\action{Averroes turns and walks toward the edge of the courtyard, away from the crowd. A'isha follows at a distance.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{4\quad INT. INFIRMARY -- PRIVATE EXAMINATION ROOM -- NIGHT}

\action{A small, windowless room lit by two oil lamps. The walls are plain white plaster. The air smells of olive oil, linen, and human fear.}

\action{The condemned man lies on a low wooden bed, covered by a thin blanket. He is no longer bound. A guard waits outside the door, visible through a half-open curtain.}

\action{Averroes enters quietly, carrying a leather satchel. He sets it down, lights a third lamp, and positions it near the bed.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{gently, as a physician}
\dialogue{I am not here to question you as a judge. I am here to examine you as a physician. Do you understand?}

\action{The condemned man nods but does not speak. His eyes are wide, still shocked.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{May I see the tunic?}

\action{The man hesitates, then slowly pulls the garment from beneath the blanket and hands it over.}

\action{Averroes holds it up to the lamplight. The hem is blackened but intact. He feels the fabric between his fingers—rubbing, testing weight, listening to the sound it makes.}

\action{It is wrong. Too stiff. Too heavy. The sound it makes when moved is not the soft rustle of linen, but something duller, more mineral.}

\action{He sets the tunic aside and gestures for the man to sit up.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I need to look at your skin. Where the tunic covered you near the flame.}

\action{The man obeys. Averroes lifts the edge of the undershirt, exposing the man's lower abdomen and thighs.}

\action{There, along a line where the heavy tunic would have hung, are small blisters—red, tender, but not severe. Not the burns of direct flame, but the burns of radiant heat partially blocked.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{These are real.}

\beat

\parenthetical{quietly}
\dialogue{Who gave you the tunic?}

\action{The condemned man looks away.}

\character{CONDEMNED MAN}
\parenthetical{barely audible}
\dialogue{A man I did not know. He came the night before. He said it was a mercy. He said I would not burn.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Did he say anything else?}

\character{CONDEMNED MAN}
\dialogue{He said... he said God works through preparation.}

\action{Averroes absorbs this. His face does not change, but his hands tighten slightly on the fabric.}

\action{A knock—sharp, sudden. The guard from outside pushes the curtain aside without waiting.}

\character{GUARD}
\dialogue{Enough. The Governor's council requests your presence tomorrow morning. There is... concern about your questions.}

\action{Averroes does not rise immediately. He holds the tunic for a moment longer, as if memorizing its weight.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Tell the council I will attend.}

\action{The guard nods and withdraws. The curtain falls back.}

\action{Averroes folds the tunic carefully and places it in his satchel. He looks at the condemned man one last time.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{You were spared. But not by what they think.}

\action{The condemned man says nothing. He lies back down and pulls the blanket over himself, as if trying to disappear.}

\transition{FADE TO BLACK.}

\transition{END OF ACT ONE}

\clearpage

\sectionhead{ACT TWO}

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{5\quad EXT. CITY SQUARE -- DUSK}

\action{The light is amber, almost orange. Long shadows stretch across the square like dark rivers. The air is cooling but still holds the day's heat in the stone.}

\action{A raised wooden platform has been erected in the center of the square—simple, functional, about three feet high. Around it, a crowd of perhaps three hundred has gathered: merchants closing their stalls, students, laborers finishing their shifts, veiled women, children.}

\action{The mood is not festive, but it is expectant. People wait with the patience of those who have learned that spectacle will eventually arrive.}

\action{On the platform stands the ASH'ARITE PROSECUTOR—late forties, lean, with a neatly trimmed beard going gray at the edges. He wears a white robe, immaculately clean, and a dark turban. His posture is upright but not rigid. He does not perform authority. He \emph{is} authority, calmly inhabited.}

\action{He speaks without shouting. His voice carries naturally, trained by years of addressing courts and congregations.}

\character{PROSECUTOR}
\dialogue{You have been told—by physicians, by judges, by men who measure the world with rulers and scales—that if you understand the parts, you can predict the whole.}

\beat

\dialogue{You have been told that fire burns because it \emph{must}. That rain falls because vapor \emph{must} condense. That a man dies because his heart \emph{must} cease.}

\action{He pauses. The crowd is silent, attentive.}

\character{PROSECUTOR \contd}
\dialogue{But if this is true—if the world is a machine of iron necessity—then what are you praying to?}

\action{A murmur of recognition. Heads nod.}

\character{PROSECUTOR \contd}
\dialogue{If fire \emph{must} burn, then your prayer for mercy is theater. If sickness \emph{must} follow from bad humors, then your plea for healing is noise.}

\beat

\dialogue{You are not praying to a force. You are praying to a \emph{person}. And a person can choose. A person is not a mechanism. A person is free.}

\action{The rhetoric does not instruct. It \emph{relieves}. We see it in faces: tension easing, shoulders dropping, people leaning into the idea like a warm current.}

\character{PROSECUTOR \contd}
\dialogue{When the physicians tell you that the body obeys laws, they are building a cage around the Living Will. They are saying: God cannot surprise us. He cannot intervene. He is bound by His own creation like a craftsman trapped in his workshop.}

\beat

\dialogue{But the Merciful is not necessity. He is freedom. And when He lifts the veil of habit, you see the world as it truly is: not a machine, but a \emph{miracle}.}

\action{More murmurs. People grip each other's arms. Some weep quietly—not from sadness, but from the comfort of being told that uncertainty is intimacy.}

\action{At the back of the crowd, half-hidden by a column, stands Averroes. He does not show anger. He shows \emph{exhaustion}—the kind born not from overwork, but from recognizing a rival method that cannot be defeated by argument alone.}

\action{Beside him, partially obscured, is A'isha. She watches the crowd, not the prosecutor. She sees how people hold each other more tightly as he speaks. She sees the way belief becomes tactile, communal.}

\action{The prosecutor raises his hands in a gesture that is both benediction and dismissal.}

\character{PROSECUTOR \contd}
\dialogue{Go home. Trust not in the predictable, but in the Merciful. Trust that when the fire does not burn, it is not because the fire has forgotten its nature. It is because He has remembered His mercy.}

\action{The crowd begins to disperse, slowly, reluctantly, as though leaving warmth for cold.}

\action{Averroes turns and walks away before the square fully empties. A'isha follows.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{6\quad INT. PRIVATE LIBRARY -- NIGHT}

\action{A small, vaulted chamber lined floor to ceiling with wooden shelves. The shelves hold codices, scrolls, loose sheaves of parchment. The air smells of leather, ink, and dust.}

\action{A single oil lamp burns on a low writing desk. The light is amber, flickering faintly.}

\action{A'isha enters alone. She is no longer disguised—her head is uncovered, her hair tied back in a practical braid. She moves with the confidence of someone who belongs here, but also with caution.}

\action{She lights a second lamp and carries it to a shelf in the far corner. She selects a thick volume—Arabic translation of Aristotle's \emph{Physics}—and brings it to the desk.}

\action{She opens the book carefully, turns pages until she finds a specific passage. She reads, frowns, then selects a blank sheet of parchment and begins to copy.}

\action{Her handwriting is fast but precise. She pauses mid-sentence, rereads the Arabic, then checks a word against a second text—a lexicon.}

\action{She notices something: the translator has softened a causal claim. Where Aristotle (via Greek to Arabic) should say "necessarily follows," the text says "often follows." A small change. A significant one.}

\action{A'isha hesitates. She dips her pen, then writes the correction in the margin: the stronger phrasing, closer to the Greek intent.}

\action{A voice from the shadows:}

\character{LIBRARIAN (O.S.)}
\dialogue{Do you know what you risk when you correct a text?}

\action{A'isha does not startle. She sets down her pen and turns.}

\action{An elderly LIBRARIAN steps into the lamplight—seventies, stooped, with ink-stained fingers and eyes that have read more than they have seen. He carries a brass snuffer.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{I risk being wrong.}

\character{LIBRARIAN}
\dialogue{No. You risk being \emph{precise}.}

\beat

\dialogue{Precision attracts attention. Attention attracts men who do not read, but who decide what others may read.}

\action{He moves to the second lamp and extinguishes it with the snuffer. The room darkens.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{If we blur every sharp edge to avoid attention, what remains? A pile of agreeable nothings?}

\character{LIBRARIAN}
\dialogue{A pile of agreeable nothings can be hidden. A precise argument can be burned.}

\action{A'isha closes the book gently, as if protecting it from the air itself.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{My father says that an argument without backbone is not an argument. It is a decoration.}

\character{LIBRARIAN}
\parenthetical{sadly}
\dialogue{Your father is a great man. Great men do not always survive the cities they serve.}

\action{He extinguishes a third lamp in a far alcove. The room becomes smaller, darker, safer.}

\character{LIBRARIAN \contd}
\dialogue{If you love what he has built, do not make it more fragile by sharpening it in public.}

\action{A'isha does not answer. She stares at the closed book, her hand resting on its cover.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{7\quad EXT. IRRIGATION CHANNEL -- MARKET DISTRICT -- DAY}

\action{Morning. Bright, clean light. The market is in full swing: stalls selling vegetables, cloth, spices, pottery. Voices overlap—bargaining, shouting, laughing.}

\action{Running along the edge of the market is a narrow stone CHANNEL, perhaps two feet wide, carrying water from a cistern uphill down through the district to a lower reservoir. The water moves at a steady pace, neither fast nor slow. Predictable.}

\action{And then—}

\action{It begins to flow backward.}

\action{Not violently. Not like a wave reversing. But the water, which had been moving left to right, now moves right to left. Slowly, but unmistakably.}

\action{A woman filling a jug notices first. She stops mid-motion, staring.}

\character{WOMAN}
\dialogue{Look. Look at the water.}

\action{Others turn. A merchant drops a stack of bowls. They clatter but do not break.}

\character{MERCHANT}
\dialogue{It's... it's going the wrong way.}

\action{A man laughs—nervous, disbelieving—then stops laughing when he realizes it is real.}

\action{People begin to gather along the channel. Pointing. Some kneel to touch the water, as if contact will explain it.}

\action{Within moments—too quickly to be coincidental—a group of the PROSECUTOR'S FOLLOWERS arrives. Five or six men in white robes, moving with purpose but not haste.}

\action{One of them steps onto a crate so he can be seen and heard.}

\character{FOLLOWER}
\dialogue{Do you see? Habit is a veil. The water does not flow downhill because it \emph{must}. It flows because He wills it. And when He wills otherwise—}

\beat

\dialogue{The veil lifts. The world shows its truth.}

\action{Murmurs of awe. Some people weep. Others simply stare, relieved to have an explanation.}

\action{Averroes arrives late, pushing through the crowd. He is out of breath, disheveled—he was summoned by a student who ran to find him.}

\action{He kneels by the channel and touches the water. It is cool, ordinary. He looks along the stone line, tracing it visually uphill and downhill, reading it like a sentence.}

\action{Faintly, we hear the drip of the clepsydra—not present, but implied. The rhythm serves as a question: \emph{what changed?}}

\action{Before Averroes can follow the channel far enough to trace the source, the water reverses again. It returns to its normal flow—downhill, left to right.}

\action{The crowd exhales. Some cheer. The reversal is treated not as a correction, but as a conclusion. The sign has been given. The message received.}

\action{Averroes stands slowly. His face is unreadable.}

\action{A'isha appears at his side, also out of breath.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\parenthetical{quietly}
\dialogue{They were waiting. They arrived before the crowd even formed.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I know.}

\action{He looks at her, then at the channel, then at the dispersing crowd.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{Someone diverted the flow upstream. Opened a gate, closed another. It would take ten minutes and two men.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{Can you prove it?}

\action{Averroes does not answer. He turns and walks toward the uphill section of the channel. A'isha follows.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{8\quad INT. AVERROES' PRIVATE STUDY -- DAY}

\action{A modest room in Averroes' house. No luxury, but no poverty either. Functional. The walls are lined with shelves holding manuscripts, medical texts, legal commentaries.}

\action{A low writing desk sits near the window. Papers are piled on it—some neatly stacked, others scattered. A brass inkwell. A bundle of reed pens.}

\action{In the corner, in a shadowed alcove, the CLEPSYDRA drips. Steady. Rhythmic. Its presence is naturalized now—part of the room's pulse.}

\action{Averroes sits at the desk, writing. The page before him bears a title in elegant Arabic script:}

\action{\emph{Tah\={a}fut al-Tah\={a}fut}}

\action{The Incoherence of the Incoherence.}

\action{He pauses, crosses out a sentence, rewrites it with greater economy. This is not inspired composition. This is labor.}

\action{A'isha enters carrying a ceramic jar, careful not to spill. She sets it on the desk. Inside is a strip of cloth, folded.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{This was left near the brazier. After the condemned man. No one claimed it.}

\action{Averroes does not touch it immediately. He looks at it the way one looks at evidence of a crime.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{They are manufacturing spectacle. Not randomly. Systematically.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{Why?}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Because spectacle is easier to govern than argument.}

\beat

\dialogue{If the city believes that fire's nature is negotiable, then the city will believe that \emph{anything} is negotiable. Law. Medicine. Truth itself.}

\action{He finally lifts the cloth from the jar. It is heavy, stiff. When he shakes it gently, it makes almost no sound.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{This is not linen. This is asbestos. Stone woven into thread.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{Can you show people?}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I can try. But they do not want to see. They want to believe that the world is kinder than it is.}

\action{A'isha sits opposite him. She picks up one of his discarded drafts and reads.}

\character{A'ISHA}
\dialogue{You are writing as if the argument will outlive the city.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{It will. Cities close their ears. Texts do not.}

\action{The clepsydra drips. Drip. Drip. Drip.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\parenthetical{quietly, almost to himself}
\dialogue{If I can make the argument strong enough, someone will carry it forward. Even if I cannot.}

\action{A'isha looks at her father. She sees, perhaps for the first time, that he is preparing for defeat.}

\transition{FADE TO BLACK.}

\transition{END OF ACT TWO}

\sectionhead{NOTE TO PRODUCTION}

\action{The remaining acts (Three, Four, and Five) will continue to develop:}

\begin{itemize}
\item The ASH'ARITE PROSECUTOR's crisis of faith when he discovers the miracles are engineered
\item A'isha's decision to preserve her father's manuscripts through a network of scribes
\item The public disputation where Averroes demonstrates the asbestos cloth
\item The political consequences: exile, book burning, institutional collapse
\item The transmission of Averroes' ideas through Italy via merchants and translators
\item The final coda showing the persistence of causality as infrastructure, not doctrine
\end{itemize}

\action{Each subsequent act will maintain the same production standards: realistic dialogue grounded in historical context, visual storytelling through objects and environment (the clepsydra, the water channels, the brazier), and characters who embody ideas without becoming symbols.}

\vspace{2em}

\action{Estimated total screenplay length: 110-120 pages}

\action{Estimated runtime: 120-130 minutes}

\vspace{2em}

\sectionhead{PRODUCTION DESIGN NOTES}

\action{\textbf{Color Palette:} Andalusian white stone, amber lamplight, indigo shadows, the red-orange of coals and sunset. Avoid romanticization—show the heat, dust, and material reality of 12th-century urban life.}

\action{\textbf{Sound Design:} The clepsydra's drip as recurring motif. Market noise as layered reality. Silence as a tool of emphasis (e.g., the moment the water reverses).}

\action{\textbf{Costume:} Functional, not ornamental. Ink stains, wear patterns, the weight of fabric. The asbestos cloth should look \emph{wrong} even before its nature is revealed.}

\action{\textbf{Performance:} Averroes should never be righteous. He should be weary, precise, and aware that clarity is a losing position. A'isha should carry the future without melodrama—she is pragmatic, not idealistic.}
