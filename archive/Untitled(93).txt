Let $\mathbf{Circ}$ denote the category of circulatory systems, and let $\mathcal{S}$ denote the sheaf of geometric obstructions (stanchions, straight paths, rigid queues). We define the **derived circulation category**:

[

## 4.1

### *Lemma 4.1: Exactness of Derived Circulation Functors*

**Statement:** Let $X^\bullet$ be a bounded complex of Plasticocene enclosures, and let $\mathbb{L}\mathcal{C} : D(\mathbf{P}) \to D(\mathbf{Circ})$ be the derived circulation functor. Then $\mathbb{L}\mathcal{C}$ is exact up to quasi-isomorphism on projective resolutions of $X^\bullet$.

**Proof Sketch:**

1. Replace $X^\bullet$ by a projective resolution $P^\bullet$ in $D(\mathbf{P})$.
2. Apply the functor $\mathcal{C}$ termwise: $\mathcal{C}(P^\bullet)$.
3. Since $P^\bullet$ is projective, acyclic complexes map to acyclic complexes, preserving cohomology.
4. Therefore, $\mathbb{L}\mathcal{C}(X^\bullet) \simeq \mathcal{C}(P^\bullet)$ up to quasi-isomorphism.

**Interpretation:** All latent circulatory potential in the park, even obstructed by queues or stanchions, is faithfully represented in the derived category.
