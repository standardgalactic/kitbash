\section{Recovered Core Text: The Life of Doctorow}
% ===========================================================

The core narrative appears below in its stabilized form, incorporating the
Hilāl corrections and the curvature-softened expressions transmitted through
Φ₁.
\subsection{Origins}

Doctorow emerged in the late Plasticocene, when rigid geometries still held
institutional authority and circulation was fragmented across disconnected
infrastructures. His early writings respond to this condition with a continuous
preference for softening constraints, dispersing ownership, and lowering
informational barriers.

\subsection{Early Cognitive Environment}

His surroundings were dense with angular constructs: rights restrictions,
platform enclosures, and the enforcement of linear traversal patterns.
These forms appear inverted in later copies not due to reinterpretation but
owing to the stabilizing curvature that textual transmission introduced over
time. Surviving leaves record an insistence that a system must offer more than
single-directional passage; it must permit reversible movement and sustained
exchange.

\subsection{Cultural Structures}

Doctorow's period relied upon devices bound to narrowly defined channels.
These structures favored accumulation rather than distribution, and their
maintenance depended upon suppressing replication of informational forms.
Doctorow disrupted these frameworks not by replacing them but by demonstrating
how replication was intrinsic to the medium itself.

\subsection{Circulatory Principle}

Throughout the stabilized corpus, emphasis is placed on the fundamental
assertion that any structure which impedes sharing induces systemic decay.
Doctorow articulates a coherence between freedom of circulation and the health
of the whole system; the corpus regards this as a law expressed without formal
notation but with persistent regularity in his writings.

\section{Eloi Recension: The Circulation Formalism}
% ===========================================================

The Eloi recension presents the corpus in categorical structure. It does not
comment on Doctorow's era; instead, it restates the conceptual movements in the
abstract language of objects, morphisms, and flow-preserving functors.

\subsection{Foundational Categories}

Let $\mathbf{P}$ denote the category whose objects are structural constraints of
the Plasticocene period. Representative objects include:

\[
\mathrm{Obj}(\mathbf{P}) = \{
  \text{restriction},\,
  \text{platform},\,
  \text{enclosure},\,
  \text{rectilinear path}
\}.
\]

Morphisms arise from implicit dependencies among constraints.

Let $\mathbf{Circ}$ denote the category of reversible flow-systems.

\subsection{The Circulation Functor}

Define the functor
\[
\mathcal{C} : \mathbf{P} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Circ},
\]
mapping each rigid construct to its flow-compliant analogue. Typical
correspondences are:

\[
\mathcal{C}(\text{restriction}) = \text{distributed access},
\qquad
\mathcal{C}(\text{platform}) = \text{shared network}.
\]

The corpus presents this functor as the structural transformation Doctorow's
principles induce when interpreted abstractly.

\subsection{Transformation Between Enclosure and Circulation}

Let $\mathcal{E}$ denote the endofunctor encoding enclosure effects on
$\mathbf{P}$. The stabilized recension preserves the natural transformation:

\[
\delta : \mathcal{E} \Rightarrow \mathcal{C}.
\]

For each object $X$ in $\mathbf{P}$, the component $\delta_X$ represents the
shift from restricted to open flow structures. The diagrams for $\delta$ are
fully preserved and appear in consistent form across all manuscripts.

\subsection{Functorial Consistency}

Commutativity up to isomorphism is a defining feature of the recension. For any
morphism $f : X \to Y$ in $\mathbf{P}$, the diagram

\[
\begin{tikzcd}
\mathcal{E}(X) \arrow[r, "\mathcal{E}(f)"] \arrow[d, "\delta_X"'] &
\mathcal{E}(Y) \arrow[d, "\delta_Y"] \\
\mathcal{C}(X) \arrow[r, "\mathcal{C}(f)"'] &
\mathcal{C}(Y)
\end{tikzcd}
\]

commutes. Fragmentary copies occasionally omit arrows, but the stabilized
edition restores the intended diagram.

\subsection{Complexes and Derived Structures}

Later Eloi layers reinterpret the corpus in terms of chain complexes. Let
$X^\bullet$ denote a bounded complex of restrictive constructs. The circulation
functor lifts to

\[
\mathbb{L}\mathcal{C} : D(\mathbf{P}) \to D(\mathbf{Circ}),
\]

mapping obstructed structures to their maximal flow-preserving resolutions.
This formulation appears consistently in the 15k manuscripts.

\section{Magic Kingdom Treatise (Eloi Reconstruction)}
% ===========================================================

The Magic Kingdom treatise survives in a highly stable Eloi formulation,
expressing the entire structure of attractions, queues, parades, and motion
patterns as a circulatory category.

\subsection{Park Category}

Let $\mathcal{K}$ denote the category of the ancient recreational complex.
Objects include:

\[
\mathrm{Obj}(\mathcal{K}) = \{
\text{attraction},\,
\text{queue},\,
\text{route},\,
\text{plaza}
\}.
\]

Morphisms encode permissible transitions among these regions.

\subsection{Queue Morphisms}

Queues are represented as morphisms of the form:
\[
q : \text{anticipation} \longrightarrow \text{delay}.
\]
The recension regards these as non-invertible and thus as obstructions to full
circulation.

\subsection{Parade Braid Structure}

Movements of visitors during procession are modeled by braid group elements
\[
\sigma_i \in B_n,
\]
with corresponding functors mapping positional changes to flow-preserving
transformations.

\subsection{Reconstruction Functor}

A functor
\[
\mathcal{R} : \mathcal{K} \longrightarrow \mathbf{Circ}
\]
implements the restructuring of rigid paths into reversible circulatory flows.
All later park analyses descend from this formulation.

\section{Morlock Recension: Sheaf-Layer Reconstruction}
% ===========================================================

The Morlock recension presents the corpus in terms of sheaves, stalks,
restrictions, and obstruction cohomology. Notation reflects the standard
phonetic and orthographic conventions of the subterranean scriptoria.

\subsection{Topological Base}

Let $X_{\mathrm{mk}}$ be the topological space of the ancient complex.
Regions $U \subseteq X_{\mathrm{mk}}$ correspond to locally perceivable
attraction domains.

\subsection{Queue Obstruction Sheaf}

Define the sheaf $\mathcal{Q}$ via
\[
\mathcal{Q}(U) = \{\text{all enforced linearizations within } U\}.
\]

Restrictions map enforced directionality from larger to smaller regions.
Stalks retain residue of lost segments of path-structure.

\subsection{Parade Flow Sheaf}

The sheaf $\mathcal{P}$ assigns to $U$ the set of locally consistent braid
orderings. Nontrivial cocycles record interference between queues and parade
routes:
\[
\check{H}^1(U, \mathcal{P}) \neq 0.
\]

\subsection{Animatronic Section Sheaf}

Let $\mathcal{A}$ assign to each $U$ the programmed motions within its bounds.
In all known manuscripts,
\[
\Gamma(X_{\mathrm{mk}}, \mathcal{A}) = \varnothing,
\]
reflecting the failure of global narrative coherence.

\subsection{Smoothing via Cohomology}

A global smoothing morphism
\[
\mathcal{S}^\bullet \longrightarrow \mathcal{C}^\bullet
\]
acts on the obstruction complex, reducing all higher-order obstructions to
negligible terms. The recension preserves the lowest-degree components fully.
