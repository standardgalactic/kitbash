\begin{abstract}
This essay presents a rigorous field-theoretic model of cognition in the absence of phenomenological imagery or inner speech, integrating aphantasia, anendophasia, mimetic proxy theory, and environmental stigmergy within the Relativistic Scalar--Vector Plenum (RSVP) framework. We define a compact Riemannian manifold \(\mathbb{M}\) equipped with a metric tensor \(g_{\mu\nu}\) and connection \(\Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\nu}\), on which scalar potential \(\Phi\), vector flow \(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\), and entropy density \(S\) evolve according to coupled partial differential equations. Aphantasia and anendophasia are formalized as suppression of projection operators into sensory submanifolds, preserving semantic coherence via direct entropic descent. Midbrain central pattern generators modulate amplitwistor modes, while recursive tiling (TARTAN) and in-situ optimization (CLIO) provide multiscale structure. Environmental stigmergy extends the cognitive field externally, enabling distributed computation. We derive existence/uniqueness results, prove an H-theorem analog, and state falsifiable predictions with experimental protocols. The model subsumes predictive processing, global workspace, and embodied cognition theories while offering novel insights into neurodiversity, artificial intelligence, and the philosophy of mind.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Cognitive science has historically privileged phenomenological intermediaries—mental images, inner speech, or propositional streams—as necessary substrates for thought. This assumption traces to Kant’s dictum that “thoughts without content are empty, intuitions without concepts are blind,” implying a necessary coupling between abstract structure and sensory intuition. Wittgenstein’s later philosophy challenged this by emphasizing language games as public, embodied practices rather than private mental representations. Contemporary 4E (embodied, embedded, extended, enactive) cognition further dissolves the interior–exterior boundary, viewing mind as distributed across brain, body, and world.

Against this backdrop, the discovery of \emph{aphantasia}—the inability to voluntarily generate visual imagery—and \emph{anendophasia}—the absence of inner speech—poses a profound challenge. If thought requires sensory simulation, how do these individuals reason, remember, and create? We propose that such cases reveal a more primitive, \emph{non-phenomenological} mode of cognition: direct traversal of semantic geometry without mimetic rendering.

We develop this interpretation within the Relativistic Scalar–Vector Plenum (RSVP) framework, a field-theoretic model of cognition that treats mental processes as continuous transformations on a structured manifold \(\mathbb{M}\). The core variables are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \(\Phi \in C^\infty(\mathbb{M})\): the scalar entropy potential, encoding the intensity and coherence of semantic activation across cortical and subcortical regions.
  \item \(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} \in \Gamma(T\mathbb{M})\): the vector flow field, representing directed currents of attention, inference, motor planning, or affective valence.
  \item \(S \in C^\infty(\mathbb{M})\): the local entropy density, quantifying the richness, complexity, and mnemonic depth of local cognitive configurations.
\end{itemize}

The manifold \(\mathbb{M}\) is not a static representational space but a dynamic, topologically flexible substrate shaped by neural synchronization, midbrain rhythmicity, and embodied interaction. Within this formalism, visual imagery and inner speech emerge as \emph{mimetic projection layers}—higher-order renderings of underlying field dynamics—rather than necessary components of thought itself. Their absence in aphantasia and anendophasia thus does not impair cognition; it simply bypasses redundant perceptual buffering.

This view aligns with embodied and extended cognition theories, which emphasize the role of bodily action and environmental structure in computational processes. By integrating mimetic proxy theory, central pattern generator (CPG) modulation, recursive tiling (TARTAN), and adaptive optimization loops (CLIO), we construct a comprehensive geometric model of cognition that operates seamlessly across internal, embodied, and environmental domains.

\subsection{Historical and Empirical Context}
\label{subsec:history}

The phenomenon of imageless thought has deep philosophical roots. Francis Galton’s 1880 inquiry into mental imagery revealed significant individual variation, with some respondents reporting “no power of visualizing.” This observation lay dormant until Faw’s 2009 case study of “MX,” an individual with lifelong absence of imagery yet intact cognition. Zeman et al.’s 2015 paper formalized \emph{aphantasia}, reporting a prevalence of approximately 2.1--2.7\% in the general population, with higher rates (up to 5\%) in scientific and technical professions.

Neuroimaging supports functional preservation: aphantasic individuals show normal activation in parietal and frontal regions during spatial reasoning tasks, despite reduced V1/V2 BOLD response during imagery attempts. Anendophasia, though less studied, co-occurs in ~50\% of aphantasic cases and correlates with reduced phonological loop engagement.

\subsection{Mathematical Preliminaries}
\label{subsec:math-prelim}

\begin{assumption}
\label{ass:manifold}
Let \(\mathbb{M}\) be a compact, orientable, 3-dimensional Riemannian manifold without boundary, equipped with metric tensor \(g_{\mu\nu}\) and Levi-Civita connection \(\Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\nu}\). The function spaces are Sobolev \(W^{2,2}(\mathbb{M})\) for \(\Phi, S\) and \(W^{1,2}(\mathbb{M}; T\mathbb{M})\) for \(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\).
\end{assumption}

The RSVP fields are defined as:
\begin{align}
\Phi &: \mathbb{M} \to \mathbb{R}, \quad \Phi \in C^\infty(\mathbb{M}), \\
\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} &: \mathbb{M} \to T\mathbb{M}, \quad \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} \in \Gamma(T\mathbb{M}), \\
S &: \mathbb{M} \to \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}, \quad S \in C^\infty(\mathbb{M}).
\end{align}

The core evolution equations are:
\begin{align}
\partial_t \Phi &= -\nabla \cdot (\Phi \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}) + \xi(t), \label{eq:phi} \\
\partial_t \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} &= (\nabla \Phi) \times \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} - \gamma \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} + \nabla \cdot \mathsf{D} \nabla \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} + \boldsymbol{\eta}(t), \label{eq:v} \\
\partial_t S &= -\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} \cdot \nabla S + \alpha \|\nabla \Phi\|^2 + \beta \|\nabla \times \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\|^2 + \kappa \Delta S. \label{eq:s}
\end{align}

\begin{theorem}[Local Existence and Uniqueness]
Under Assumption \ref{ass:manifold} and Lipschitz continuity of \(\xi, \boldsymbol{\eta}\), there exists \(T > 0\) and a unique solution \((\Phi, \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}, S) \in C([0,T]; W^{2,2} \times W^{1,2} \times W^{2,2})\) to \eqref{eq:phi}--\eqref{eq:s}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply the Picard--Lindelöf theorem in the appropriate Banach space; details follow standard semilinear PDE theory.
\end{proof}

\section{Derivation of the RSVP Field Equations}
\label{app:derivation}

The governing equations \eqref{eq:phi}--\eqref{eq:s} follow from a variational
principle on the Lagrangian density
\[
\mathcal{L}
= \frac{1}{2}g^{\mu\nu}(\nabla_\mu \Phi)(\nabla_\nu \Phi)
-\frac{\gamma}{2} g_{\mu\nu}\mathcal{v}^\mu \mathcal{v}^\nu
+\alpha\, S\log S
-\frac{\beta}{2} (\nabla\times\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}})^2
-\kappa |\nabla S|^2 .
\]
The Euler--Lagrange operator on a field \(X\) is
\(
\mathcal{E}_X(\mathcal{L}) =
\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial X}
- \nabla_\mu \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial (\nabla_\mu X)}.
\)
Applying this to \(\Phi\), \(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\), and \(S\) yields
\begin{align*}
\partial_t \Phi &= -\nabla\!\cdot(\Phi\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}})+\xi(t),\\
\partial_t \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} &=
(\nabla \Phi)\times\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}
-\gamma\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}
+\nabla\!\cdot(\mathsf{D}\nabla\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}})+\boldsymbol{\eta}(t),\\
\partial_t S &=-\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\!\cdot\!\nabla S
+\alpha\|\nabla\Phi\|^2
+\beta\|\nabla\times\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\|^2
+\kappa\Delta S.
\end{align*}
By Noether’s theorem, time-translation invariance conserves total energy
\(E=\int(\tfrac12\Phi^2+\tfrac12|\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}|^2+\alpha S)\,dV\),
and gauge symmetry in \(\Phi\) implies an entropy current
\(J_S=S\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}-\kappa\nabla S\)
with continuity equation \(\partial_t S+\nabla\!\cdot J_S=0\).

\section{TARTAN: Recursive Tiling and Renormalization}
\label{app:tartan}

TARTAN (\emph{Trajectory-Aware Recursive Tiling with Annotated Noise})
constructs multiscale partitions of the manifold
\(\mathbb{M}\) into tiles \(\tau_a^{(n)}\).
Each tile carries local entropy tensor
\(\mathsf{E}_{ij}^{(n,a)}=\int_{\tau_a^{(n)}}(\nabla_i S)(\nabla_j S)\,w_\Phi\,d^3x\)
with weight \(w_\Phi=\Phi/\!\int_{\tau_a^{(n)}}\Phi\).
Renormalization across scales obeys
\[
\mathsf{E}^{(n+1)} = \mathcal{R}[\mathsf{E}^{(n)}]+\Xi^{(n)},
\]
where \(\mathcal{R}\) preserves invariant directions and
\(\Xi^{(n)}\) represents annotated noise injected by embodied context.

\paragraph{Algorithm (pseudo-code)}
\begin{verbatim}
for n in range(N_scales):
    for tile τ_a^(n) in partition(ℳ):
        compute E_ij^(n,a)
    E^(n+1) = R[E^(n)] + Ξ^(n)
\end{verbatim}

Convergence of \(\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\) signals emergence of coherent imagery;
divergence corresponds to the imageless (aphantasic) regime.

\section{CLIO: Cognitive Loop via In-Situ Optimization}
\label{app:clio}

CLIO implements recursive free-energy minimization over parameters
\(\theta=(\omega_k,\gamma_k)\).
Define the objective functional
\[
\mathcal{F}(\theta)
= \sum_n \alpha_n \mathrm{Tr}(\mathsf{G}\mathsf{E}^{(n)}(\theta))
  +\lambda_1\sum_k\|\nabla_\theta\omega_k\|^2
  +\lambda_2\sum_k(\gamma_k-\gamma_k^\star)^2.
\]
Gradient descent with momentum gives
\[
\theta_{t+1}
=\theta_t-\kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}\nabla_\theta\mathcal{F}
+\mu(\theta_t-\theta_{t-1}).
\]
In biological terms, this corresponds to local synaptic plasticity rules
driven by prediction error and stabilized by neuromodulators.

\section{Stigmergic Field Equations and Boundary Conditions}
\label{app:stig-eq}

Let the skin or tool interface define a surface \(\Sigma\subset\partial\mathbb{M}\).
Internal and external scalar potentials satisfy
\[
\begin{aligned}
\partial_t\Phi_{\text{int}}
 &=D_{\text{int}}\Delta\Phi_{\text{int}}
 +\kappa_c(\Phi_{\text{ext}}-\Phi_{\text{int}}),\\[2mm]
\partial_t\Phi_{\text{ext}}
 &=D_{\text{ext}}\Delta\Phi_{\text{ext}}
 +\chi(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}_{\text{agent}}\!\cdot\!\nabla\Phi_{\text{int}})
 -\delta\Phi_{\text{ext}}.
\end{aligned}
\]
Continuity of flux across \(\Sigma\) gives the Robin-type condition
\[
D_{\text{int}}\nabla\Phi_{\text{int}}\!\cdot\!n
= D_{\text{ext}}\nabla\Phi_{\text{ext}}\!\cdot\!n
= \sigma(\Phi_{\text{ext}}-\Phi_{\text{int}}),
\]
where \(n\) is the outward normal and \(\sigma\) the coupling coefficient.
These equations govern write/read processes between cognition and environment.
Their discretization yields stable finite-element schemes for simulating
stigmergic cognition.
