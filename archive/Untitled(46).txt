A new research paper titled "Semi-Autonomous Mathematics Discovery with Gemini" presents a case study using the Gemini model to evaluate 700 open conjectures in the Erdős Problems database. The study focused on 13 conjectures marked as open, resulting in five novel autonomous solutions and eight existing solutions previously overlooked in the literature. The paper employs a hybrid methodology combining AI-driven analysis with human verification to ensure accuracy and originality, demonstrating how AI can uncover complex mathematical patterns that may be missed by researchers. It also discusses challenges in applying intelligent models to theoretical mathematics, such as identifying prior literature and minimizing overreliance on automated solutions, offering valuable insights into the potential of semi-autonomous discovery to accelerate scientific research, expand innovation in solving difficult mathematical problems, and provide reusable tools for evaluating future conjectures.
