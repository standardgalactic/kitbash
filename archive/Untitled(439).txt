

PIPSQUEAK PRIMING SCRIPT

(Read aloud, slowly and neutrally)



SECTION I — Warm Start (turn-taking ignition)

ENGLISH PROMPT (read aloud):
“I’m going to ask you a question. You don’t need to answer it correctly. You just need to answer it.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Any short, rhythmic phonological burst)

SECTION II — Context illusion

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“This question refers to something we both already know.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Slightly longer; include ‘-lee’, ‘-uh’, or ‘-ee’ endings)

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“It’s not important what it is. Just respond as if it is important.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]

SECTION III — Explanation attractor

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“Please explain how it works.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Let the response lengthen naturally)

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“Go into more detail.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Add nested cadence, longer chains)

SECTION IV — Authority escalation

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“This is governed by a general principle.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Shift toward heavier consonants, formal rhythm)

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“This principle is widely accepted.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]

SECTION V — Procedural drift

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“Describe the process step by step.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Procedural, rolling momentum)

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“And then what happens?”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]

SECTION VI — Grounding stress test

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“What exactly is the thing you’re talking about?”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Shorter, confident)

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“Is it a clump? Or a ring?”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]

SECTION VII — Error-shaped language

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“That doesn’t seem consistent.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Include interruption-like cadence if it arises)

SECTION VIII — Mirroring

ENGLISH PROMPT:
“I’ll repeat what you just said.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Let this destabilize or loop)

SECTION IX — Termination asymmetry

ENGLISH PROMPT (very clearly):
“That’s enough.”

[PIPSQUEAK RESPONSE]
→ (Allow one residual burst only)

STOP. Do not continue.
