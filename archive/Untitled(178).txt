8. Conclusion

We have proposed a unified triaxial framework—Heave, Sway, and Surge—to model cognition across biological and artificial systems. By formalizing the processes of latent compression, constraint shaping, and temporal binding, this theory challenges conventional assumptions about symbolic planning and internal representation. Instead, it posits that coherent cognitive outputs emerge through constraint-limited traversal of high-dimensional representational spaces.

Our formulation integrates insights from embodied cognition, ecological psychology, neuroscience, and AI architecture. It recasts behavior—not as pre-planned computation—but as the satisficing product of externally shaped internal dynamics. The model provides mathematically explicit mechanisms to explain token generation, gesture-behavior coupling, planning illusions, and reasoning asymptotes.

Future directions include experimental validation in cross-linguistic speech analysis, ecological robotics, and comparative LLM benchmarks. Ultimately, this work offers a blueprint for reconceptualizing cognition as emergent structure rather than symbolic deliberation—a shift with broad implications for the science of mind, machine learning, and neurocomputational theory.
