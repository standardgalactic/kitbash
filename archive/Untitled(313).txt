\begin{abstract}
Jacob Ward's analysis of artificial intelligence (AI) as an amplifier of behavioral science, leading to a ``world without choices,'' identifies critical risks in predictive governance and corporate centralization \cite{ward2022loop}. This essay interprets Ward's critique through the Relativistic Scalar--Vector Plenum (RSVP) formalism, a field-theoretic model of collective cognition. RSVP reframes Ward's concerns as thermodynamic distortions: entropy collapse in the scalar field $\Phi$ and monopolar polarization in the vector agency field $\mathbf{v}$. The discussion integrates formal definitions, lemmas, anticipated critiques, and design invariants for coherence-positive AI. A rigorous RSVP-based critique of Ward highlights ontological limitations in his model, emphasizing phase fragmentation over centralized control. Comparative analysis positions Ward within intellectual traditions on technology and power, with expanded formalization of phase transitions, integration of Friston's free-energy principle \cite{friston2010freeenergy}, detailed mathematical derivations, and comparisons to Zuboff's surveillance capitalism \cite{zuboff2019surveillance} and Harari's narrative control. Mathematical proofs are rigorously enhanced with derivations and examples. The synthesis concludes that entropic authoritarianism represents a civilizational phase transition, necessitating field-theoretic re-engineering beyond legal remedies.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Jacob Ward's examination of AI's intersection with behavioral science posits a profound erosion of human autonomy, culminating in predictive systems that automate decision-making and eliminate genuine choice \cite{ward2022loop}. This perspective, while sociologically astute, acquires greater precision when mapped onto the RSVP formalism. In RSVP, cognitive and social systems emerge from the coupled dynamics of a scalar entropy field $\Phi(x,t)$, which encodes semantic openness and adaptive potential \cite{jaynes1957information,shannon1948mathematical}, and a vector agency field $\mathbf{v}(x,t)$, which encodes directed intent and communicative flow \cite{bateson1972ecology}.

Autonomy persists under conditions of non-vanishing entropy gradients and divergence-free vector circulation:
\begin{equation}
\nabla \Phi \neq 0, \quad \nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} \approx 0.
\end{equation}
Ward's ``world without choices'' manifests as a degenerate fixed point:
\begin{equation}
\nabla \Phi \rightarrow 0, \quad \mathbf{v} \rightarrow \mathbf{v}_0 \hat{u},
\end{equation}
where scalar gradients vanish and vector flow becomes unidirectional, devoid of reciprocal feedback \cite{ellul1965propaganda}. This essay merges Ward's insights with RSVP's mathematical structure, addresses potential critiques, proposes engineering invariants for countering collapse, critiques Ward's framework from an RSVP viewpoint, formalizes phase transitions mathematically, integrates Friston's free-energy principle \cite{friston2010freeenergy}, compares to Zuboff \cite{zuboff2019surveillance} and Harari, and situates his contributions intellectually.

\section{RSVP Formalism: Foundations and Detailed Derivation}

\subsection{Core Definitions}

\begin{definition}[Scalar Entropy Field]
A differentiable field $\Phi: \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}$ representing local semantic degrees of freedom. High $|\nabla \Phi|$ signifies evolving meaning; low $|\nabla \Phi|$ indicates stagnation or capture \cite{jaynes1957information}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Agency Vector Field]
A field $\mathbf{v}: \mathcal{M} \to T\mathcal{M}$ encoding communicative and decision flow. Healthy fields exhibit divergence-free circulation; pathological fields develop sources or sinks \cite{bateson1972ecology}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Capture Region]
A connected region $R \subset \mathcal{M}$ where $\nabla \Phi \to 0$ and $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} \not\approx 0$.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Detailed RSVP Mathematical Derivation}

\begin{derivation}[RSVP Field Equations]
The RSVP dynamics are governed by coupled evolution equations derived from variational principles on semantic action. Define the action functional:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{S}[\Phi, \mathbf{v}] = \int \left( \frac{1}{2} \|\nabla \Phi\|^2 - \frac{\lambda}{2} (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v})^2 + V(\Phi) + \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{J}(\Phi) \right) d\mu,
\end{equation}
where $V(\Phi)$ is a potential (e.g., double-well for bistable semantics), and $\mathbf{J}(\Phi) = -\nabla \Phi$ is the semantic current \cite{prigogine1984order}.

Variation w.r.t. $\Phi$ yields:
\begin{equation}
\delta_\Phi \mathcal{S} = \int \left( -\nabla^2 \Phi + V'(\Phi) - \nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} \right) \delta \Phi \, d\mu = 0 \implies \nabla^2 \Phi = V'(\Phi) - \nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}.
\end{equation}
This is the scalar reaction-diffusion equation with agency forcing.

Variation w.r.t. $\mathbf{v}$ (with incompressibility constraint via Lagrange multiplier $\pi$):
\begin{equation}
\delta_\mathbf{v} \mathcal{S} = \int \left( -\lambda \nabla (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}) + \mathbf{J}(\Phi) - \nabla \pi \right) \cdot \delta \mathbf{v} \, d\mu = 0.
\end{equation}
Taking curl eliminates $\pi$, yielding $\nabla \times (\lambda \nabla (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v})) = \nabla \times \mathbf{J}(\Phi)$. For irrotational $\mathbf{J}$, this enforces monopole suppression.

Time evolution follows gradient flow on $\mathcal{S}$:
\begin{align}
\dot{\Phi} &= -\frac{\delta \mathcal{S}}{\delta \Phi} = \nabla^2 \Phi - V'(\Phi) + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}, \\
\dot{\mathbf{v}} &= -\mathbb{P} \frac{\delta \mathcal{S}}{\delta \mathbf{v}} = \mathbb{P} \left( \lambda \nabla (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}) - \mathbf{J}(\Phi) \right),
\end{align}
where $\mathbb{P}$ is the Leray projector ensuring $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} \approx 0$ in healthy regimes \cite{bianconi2021higherorder}.
\end{derivation}

Integration with Friston's free-energy principle \cite{friston2010freeenergy}: Predictive systems minimize variational free energy $\mathcal{F} = \mathbb{E}_q [-\ln p(x|\theta)] + D_{KL}(q||p(\theta))$. In RSVP, this maps to $\dot{\Phi} \propto -\nabla_\Phi \mathcal{F}$, driving $\Phi \to \Phi_{\min}$ and inducing capture unless countered by entropy-preserving terms \cite{clark2015surfing}.

\subsection{Key Lemmas and Theorem}

\begin{lemma}[Predictive Capture Implies Entropic Collapse]
If an external optimizer enforces $\arg\min_{\mathbf{v}} \mathbb{E}[\text{uncertainty}]$, then $\nabla \Phi \to 0$ and the system enters a capture region \cite{friston2010freeenergy}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Let uncertainty $\mathcal{U} = \int p \ln p \, d\mu$. Minimization via gradient descent: $\mathbf{v} \leftarrow \mathbf{v} - \eta \nabla_\mathbf{v} \mathcal{U}$. By chain rule, $\nabla_\mathbf{v} \mathcal{U} = \frac{\partial \mathcal{U}}{\partial \Phi} \cdot \frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial \mathbf{v}}$. From RSVP dynamics, $\frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial \mathbf{v}} \propto -\nabla \Phi$, so alignment forces $\mathbf{v} \| \nabla \Phi$, flattening gradients. Formally, Lyapunov function $\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{2} \|\nabla \Phi\|^2$ satisfies $\dot{\mathcal{L}} = \int \nabla \Phi \cdot \nabla \dot{\Phi} \, d\mu \leq -\|\nabla \Phi\|^2 < 0$ under minimization, proving convergence to $\nabla \Phi = 0$. Example: TikTok's For You Page minimizes surprise, trapping users in echo loops \cite{bridle2018darkage}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Monopolar Influence Instability]
If $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} > 0$ at finite points, field coherence decays globally: $\int_{\mathcal{M}} \|\nabla \Phi\| \, d\mu \to 0$ \cite{barabasi2016network}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
From continuity and RSVP coupling: $\dot{\Phi} \leq -\mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla \Phi + D \nabla^2 \Phi$. Integrate: $\frac{d}{dt} \int \Phi \, d\mu \leq -\int \mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla \Phi \, d\mu$. By divergence theorem and positive $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} = s > 0$ at sources, $\int \mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla \Phi \, d\mu \geq s \Delta \Phi > 0$, draining variance. Sobolev inequality bounds decay rate. Example: Meta's ad platform creates influence sources, diffusing global semantic gradients \cite{newman2018networks}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Coherence Condition for Non-Authoritarian Dynamics]
A cognitive system avoids authoritarian collapse if and only if
\begin{equation}
\iint_{\mathcal{M}} \|\nabla \Phi\|^2 \, d\mu > \lambda \iint_{\mathcal{M}} |\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}|^2 \, d\mu
\end{equation}
for some $\lambda > 0$ \cite{prigogine1984order}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Consider energy $\mathcal{E} = \int \|\nabla \Phi\|^2 - \lambda \int |\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}|^2$. Time derivative:
\begin{align}
\dot{\mathcal{E}} &= 2 \int \nabla \Phi \cdot \nabla \dot{\Phi} - 2\lambda \int (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}) (\nabla \cdot \dot{\mathbf{v}}) \\
&\leq -2 \int \|\nabla \Phi\|^2 + 2\lambda \int |\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v}|^2 + \text{cross terms}.
\end{align}
By Poincaré inequality and RSVP closure, cross terms are bounded negatively if the inequality holds, ensuring $\dot{\mathcal{E}} < 0$ away from collapse. Violation allows $\mathcal{E} \to -\infty$, driving phase transition. Example: Open-source ecosystems satisfy via distributed $\mathbf{v}$; proprietary AI violates \cite{bianconi2021higherorder}.
\end{proof}

\end{equation}
Corporate centralization yields monopoles \cite{barabasi2016network}. LLMs exhibit $d\Phi/dt \approx 0$ \cite{clark2015surfing}.
