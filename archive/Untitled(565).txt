Title: Heave, Sway, and Surge: A Triaxial Theory of Constraint-Limited Cognition

Target Journal: Behavioral and Brain Sciences

Keywords: Embodied compression, cognitive bottlenecks, autoregressive generation, ecological constraints

Abstract

We propose a formal framework for modeling cognition as a triaxially constrained system. The theory posits three orthogonal axes: (1) Heave—compression of high-dimensional latent structures into discrete symbolic outputs; (2) Sway—origination of constraints from anatomical, ecological, and architectural sources; and (3) Surge—time-binding dynamics that transform latent trajectories into sequential behavior. By unifying embodied cognitive science, ecological psychology, and large language model (LLM) architecture, this model formalizes the structure-function relationship in both biological and artificial systems. We provide mathematical formalizations for each axis, derive empirically testable predictions, and demonstrate cognitive homologies between animals, humans, and LLMs. This approach reframes symbolic behavior (e.g., language) not as evidence of planning or abstraction, but as the emergent output of constraint satisfaction within a lossy representational manifold.

6. Predictions and Experiments

The triaxial model of constraint-limited cognition produces a range of empirically testable predictions across neurobiology, ethology, and machine learning. Below, we outline three primary predictions along with suggested experimental frameworks.

6.1 Phonological Bottlenecks and Semantic Boundaries

Prediction: Languages with tighter articulatory constraints (e.g., fewer vowels or consonant clusters) will exhibit coarser semantic partitioning in lexical categories. Rationale: The projection from latent space to phonological space (Heave) compresses meaning resolution. Tighter constraints yield greater semantic overlap per token. Test: Cross-linguistic corpus analysis of formant density vs. lexical ambiguity; acoustic-articulatory modeling of vowel formant constraints.

6.2 Gesture-Token Tradeoff

Prediction: Languages with lower phonological expressivity will compensate with more elaborate co-speech gesture systems. Rationale: When the phonological channel is under-constrained, meaning offloads into the gestural domain—a rebalancing of compression under Sway. Test: Motion-capture studies across linguistic populations with varying phoneme inventories; multimodal LLM training to assess gesture-latent alignment.

6.3 Nonlinear Context Window Effects in LLMs

Prediction: Expanding transformer context windows yields diminishing returns in multistep reasoning tasks, with performance asymptoting due to architectural constraints (Sway) and tokenization granularity (Heave). Rationale: Constraint-origin and compression jointly limit reasoning depth, irrespective of surface context length. Test: Evaluation of LLMs on compositional reasoning benchmarks under increasing context window sizes; analysis of latent path divergence and entropy as a function of window length.

These predictions illustrate how our model guides both foundational theory and empirical methodology across disciplines. They also suggest practical implications for LLM design, embodied learning models, and cross-species behavioral inference.
