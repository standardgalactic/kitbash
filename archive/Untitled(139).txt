3. Infrastructure Role

Museums, galleries, rituals act as entropy reservoirs, storing high-uncertainty objects while society learns to metabolize them.

Once a technique becomes widely shared, entropy drops → what was once “art” becomes “craft” or “technology.”

For example:

Perspective in painting was once esoteric high art; now it’s a standard tool taught in schools.

Jazz improvisation was once opaque noise; now it’s a codified musical grammar.

We can model the cultural art budget as:

\text{Art}(t) = \sum_{i \in \mathcal{A}} H_{\text{art}}(i,t)

Too small → rigid culture, no innovation.

Too large → incoherent culture, no shared meaning.

Healthy cultures manage this entropy reservoir, seeding innovations until they canonize into craft/technique.
