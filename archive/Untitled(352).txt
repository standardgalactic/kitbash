\Scene{FADE IN:}

\Scene{EXT. NIGHT SKY – STARS – 18TH CENTURY EUROPE}

\Action{A cold scatter of STARS against a glacial void. No music. Only wind.}

\Action{Faint lines of pale light connect several stars — sketching the beginning of a geometric figure. Angular. Ancient. Wrong.}

\Action{The lines tremble… then SNAP OUT.}

\Action{Silence returns like a blade.}

\Scene{INT. KÖNIGSBERG UNIVERSITY – OBSERVATORY – NIGHT}

\Action{A cramped dome under winter sky. Brass telescope. Shelves of astronomical tables. Candles flicker in a draft that shouldn't exist.}

\Action{IMMANUEL KANT, early 40s, thin, pale, fastidious, works alone by candlelight. His quill scratches with mechanical precision.}

\Action{He turns a page — and freezes.}

\Action{On the margin of the ephemeris, penciled in a tiny hand:  
the same SIGIL he saw in the stars.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(barely audible)  
Who has touched my papers?
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{He flips another sheet. Same sigil. Perfectly identical.  
A third. A fourth.}

\Action{No signature. No signs of erasure.}

\Action{Kant's jaw tightens. He sets his quill down.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Fatigue. Nothing more.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{He removes his spectacles, rubs his eyes. Looks again—}

\Action{The sigil on the first page is GONE. Just blank paper.}

\Action{He turns slowly toward a star chart pinned to the wall.  
There, faintly between two constellations, the sigil hides in pale ink —  
as if it had always been part of the sky.}

\Scene{FLASHBACK – EXT. TOWN SQUARE – KONIGSBERG – DAY (YEARS EARLIER)}

\Action{A violent storm. Young KANT, age 12, stands under the city clock tower.  
Lightning splits the air. Rain lashes.}

\Action{The CLOCK HANDS jerk — STOP — lurch forward with a stuttering rhythm.  
TICK — silence — TICKTICK — silence.}

\Action{Young Kant grips a handmade wooden clock, trembling.}

\Action{For one lightning flash, the clocktower’s SHADOW lags behind the structure, a temporal echo.}

\Scene{INT. KANT FAMILY HOME – NIGHT (FLASHBACK)}

\Action{Young Kant repairs the model clock. Aligns gears. Adjusts tension.  
The mechanism ticks in perfect regularity. He exhales with relief.}

\Scene{BACK TO OBSERVATORY – NIGHT}

\Action{The distant town clock resumes its normal rhythm.}

\Action{Kant crosses out the remaining sigils with hard, deliberate strokes.}

\Action{A tiny victory of reason.}

\Scene{EXT. UNIVERSITY COURTYARD – DAWN}

\Action{Frost on cobblestones. Kant walks with strict, metronomic precision.  
His breath fogs in the cold.}

\Action{He steps directly over a jagged crack in the stones—  
a crude version of the sigil — without noticing.}

\Scene{INT. COFFEEHOUSE – DAY}

\Action{A smoky, crowded coffeehouse. Students, merchants, and travelers debate loudly.}

\Action{Kant sits alone, untouched coffee before him.}

\Action{Nearby, two TRAVELERS tell a tale.}

\Character{TRAVELER \#1 (O.S.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
...told us the house would burn before the night was over.  
Described the window the flames would break.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{TRAVELER \#2 (O.S.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Coincidence.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{TRAVELER \#1}
\begin{Dialogue}
He named the street. From \textit{three hundred miles away}.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant's eyes lift. He approaches.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Pardon me. You speak of Emanuel Swedenborg?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{TRAVELER \#1}
\begin{Dialogue}
Aye. Met him in Stockholm. Quiet man. Not mad.  
Said the spirits showed him.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(dry)  
Spirits have little interest in architecture.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Light laughter. The tension eases.}

\Action{Kant glances at a broadsheet illustration: a burning house.  
Negative space in the smoke almost forms—  
the sigil.}

\Action{He blinks. It’s gone.}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – EVENING}

\Action{Kant at his lectern, revising a philosophical manuscript.  
Small adjustments. Minor rituals of order.}

\Action{A knock. A hesitant STUDENT enters.}

\Character{STUDENT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Professor… a visitor. He insisted.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
I am not receiving visitors—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{STUDENT}
\begin{Dialogue}
He gave his name as… Emanuel Swedenborg.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Silence thickens. Kant's hand freezes mid-mark.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(measured)  
Show him in.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Footsteps. Candle flame tilts sideways — not from draft, but “attention.”}

\Action{SWEDENBORG enters. Late 50s. Calm. Eyes too bright.  
Their shadows lag half a second behind them before catching up.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Professor Kant. Thank you for receiving me.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Mister Swedenborg. Please—come in.
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – CONTINUOUS}

\Action{Swedenborg does not remove his coat or hat. He stands with ritual stillness. Kant motions to a chair; Swedenborg remains standing.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Your reputation precedes you.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Not always to my benefit.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{He reaches into his coat and withdraws a folded sheet of paper.  
He sets it gently on Kant’s desk.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Three days ago, this arrived at your home.  
You have shown it to no one.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant unfolds it.  
It is the exact letter from the coffeehouse — sigil included.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(voice tightening)  
How did you obtain this?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
It was shown to me.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
By whom?
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Swedenborg hesitates — not uncertainty, but searching for vocabulary.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Not by the dead, as people say.  
They see nothing more than the living.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Then by what?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
By the structures that hold the world in place.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant’s composure fractures.  
He hides it with academic calm.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
I deal in forms of intuition, the categories of understanding.  
I have little patience for revelations.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
They are not revelations.  
They are errors.  
Misalignments in the manifold.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant stiffens — “manifold” is his private term, unwritten.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
You have seen the sign.  
It returns where it should not.  
That is how it begins.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Hypothetically — if such a pattern existed —  
it could only come from human error.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
(soft)  
The world is erring.  
The great clock falters.  
You have heard it stutter.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{FLASH — young Kant beneath the storm-lurching clock.}

\Action{BACK TO SCENE.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Ask a question whose answer only you possess.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(firm)  
Very well.  
What is the angle between Jupiter and Aldebaran at this hour?
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Swedenborg opens a blank journal. Sets it down.}

\Action{Ink blooms on the page —  
in Kant’s exact handwriting —  
the correct calculation.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
You did that with sleight of hand.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
(shaking his head)  
With structure.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The sigil overlays the numbers, glowing faintly.  
The candle flame elongates unnaturally, bending toward the desk.  
Kant's shadow lags a heartbeat behind his movement.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
It is watching.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant snaps the journal shut. Nearly trembles.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Leave this house.  
At once.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
They are not finished with the prototype, Immanuel.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{He exits. The door closes with impossible quiet.}

\Action{Kant sinks into his chair, shaken.  
He turns the journal facedown —  
but the sigil has bled through the page.}

% ============================================================
%                        ACT II BEGINS
% ============================================================

\Scene{EXT. KÖNIGSBERG – PREGEL RIVERBANK – LATE AFTERNOON}

\Action{Mist coils over black water. Lantern flames stand unnaturally upright.}

\Action{Kant walks quickly, eyes darting to shadows.  
The ninth chime of the town clock arrives a full second late.}

\Action{He stops at the railing.  
The river is mirror-still.}

\Action{A ripple —  
but from below.}

\Action{An inverted CITY rises beneath the surface:  
spires pointing downward, lit by pale blue windows.  
Geometric grids that hurt to perceive.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(whisper)  
This is the dream…
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The city rotates slowly, as if turning to regard him.  
A church bell tolls inside his skull.}

\Action{Kant staggers back.  
The vision vanishes.  
Only his reflection remains —  
but it lingers a heartbeat after he moves.}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – NIGHT}

\Action{Papers everywhere. Candle stubs.  
Kant copies the sigil over and over, never precisely reproducing it.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
No… the base must be—  
Why can’t I fix it?!
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{He slams the quill. Breath ragged.}

\Action{A faint scraping —  
the chalkboard is writing itself.}

\Action{Kant approaches slowly.  
Two strokes.  
Three.  
The sigil emerges, concave, carved from inside the slate.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(whisper)  
Why me?
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A knock.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG (O.S.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
May I come in?
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – CONTINUOUS}

\Action{Swedenborg enters. Sees the recessed sigil.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
It is accelerating.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
I do not understand what it wants.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Not “it.”  
Them.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
What are they?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Imagine a city of pure relation.  
Streets of intuition.  
Walls of time.  
Beings who stand not in space,  
but in the structure that \textit{gives rise} to space.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant staggers back.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
They touch you because you are close to the truth.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(breaking)  
No—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
You are thinking the thoughts that reveal them.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Stop—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
And they wish to see what sees them.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A HUM fills the room — a resonant, architectural vibration.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Another breach.
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{EXT. KÖNIGSBERG – RIVERSIDE PATH – NIGHT}

\Action{Kant and Swedenborg walk.  
Wind dies suddenly.  
Soundscape collapses into an unnatural stillness.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
This place feels… hollow.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Yes.
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{EXT. ABANDONED BATHHOUSE – CONTINUOUS}

\Action{A dilapidated structure.  
Light flickers from within, but no smoke from the chimney.}

\Action{Inside —  
another massive sigil etched into stone.  
Warm to the touch.  
Perfect.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
This is more precise than Euclid himself—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
It did itself.
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{EXT. BRIDGE OVER THE PREGEL – AFTERNOON}

\Action{A patch of river freezes instantly in a perfect circle.  
Then rotates as a rigid disc.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
It is not the river that changes.  
It is the framework by which you perceive the river.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The disc melts.}

\Scene{INT. UNIVERSITY LECTURE HALL – MORNING}

\Action{Kant attempts to lecture.  
The chalk curves of its own volition — forming a fragment of the sigil.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Forgive me.  
A moment of distraction.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{He wipes the board —  
but the lines reappear faintly beneath the slate.}

\Scene{EXT. UNIVERSITY COURTYARD – DAY}

\Action{Kant exits the lecture hall, shaken.  
The irregular ticking returns.  
He looks up.  
The tower clock is perfectly steady.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG (O.S.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
You saw it, did you not?
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – NIGHT}

\Action{Storm outside.  
Ink runs upward on the page against gravity.  
Kant laughs — a small, broken sound.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Of course.  
It was never mine to discover.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The torn page reassembles itself.}

\Action{A shadow in the corner stretches — independently of the object casting it.}

\Scene{EXT. FOREST CLEARING – NIGHT}

\Action{A perfect circular clearing.  
A stone structure extruded from bedrock.  
A fracture opens in space — luminous, impossible.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Close it— CLOSE IT—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
I cannot.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A geometry knot forms in the crack — rotating through impossible axes.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(breaking)  
This is the end of reason—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
No.  
This is its beginning.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A VOICE speaks from the anomaly.  
Not sound — structure.}

\Character{VOICE (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Immanuel Kant.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant collapses. The anomaly flares.}

\Scene{INT. VOID-LIKE SPACE – UNKNOWN}

\Action{Pure darkness.  
Geometric forms orbit him: categories, axioms, diagrams.  
The sigil expands until it fills the sky.}

\Character{VOICE (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
You will restore the structure.  
Or the veil will fail.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The sigil collapses.}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – NIGHT}

\Action{Kant wakes violently on the floor.  
Swedenborg stands nearby, shaking.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
What are they?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
The skeleton of the real.
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{EXT. KÖNIGSBERG – EAST RIVERBANK – SUNSET}

\Action{The inverted city rises again.  
Closer than ever.  
Lights flickering like synapses.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(whisper)  
They’re building something.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Yes.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant’s shadow waves at him.  
Independently.  
Recognition.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(screaming)  
WHAT ARE YOU?!
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{EXT. FOREST CLEARING – NIGHT}

\Action{The fracture widens.  
The anomaly addresses Kant:}

\Character{VOICE (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
You will write the rule  
by which blindness is preserved.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Why me?!
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{White shockwave.  
Kant screams.}

% ============================================================

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – NIGHT}

\Action{Candlelight flutters sideways, not upward.  
The ink on Kant’s manuscript pulses, beating like a heart.}

\Action{Kant kneels over scattered pages.  
His hand shakes uncontrollably.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(whisper)  
I cannot bear it…
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{His shadow stretches — then detaches slightly from his heels,  
attempting to stand independently.}

\Action{Kant recoils. Hits the wall.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Stop — STOP —
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The room folds inward like collapsing geometry.  
The sigil appears on every wall simultaneously.}

\Action{Then everything snaps open into—}

\Scene{INT. THE CATEGORY PLANE – UNKNOWN SPACE}

\Action{Sudden silence.  
Kant floats in a void of impossible geometry.  
Grids of light ripple and tessellate like cosmic latticework.}

\Action{Massive structures loom in the dark —  
elaborate towers of pure relation,  
long-limbed silhouettes formed of intersecting lines.  
They resemble SLENDER HUMANOIDS  
only by accident of topology.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(whisper)  
This… this is the interior of Reason…
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
You stand within that which knows itself  
only through you.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{An ARCHITECT FORM appears —  
glitching across discrete positions,  
never “moving,” only updating.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Are you… conscious?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Consciousness is a boundary condition.  
You are that condition.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A tremor ripples through the plane.  
Fragments of inverted KONIGSBERG appear suspended in grids of light —  
buildings turning inside-out, unfolding, refolding.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
My city…  
It is being rewritten.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Correct.  
The manifold is losing coherence.  
You are its hinge.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant steps forward —  
the “floor” forms beneath his feet a moment after he applies weight.  
The geometry lags, then catches up.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Why choose me?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
You imagine necessity.  
You seek laws.  
You impose order on that which is otherwise unbound.  
You are constructing us.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant shudders.  
Terrified.  
Transfixed.}

\Scene{INT. CATEGORY PLANE – SHIFTED REGION}

\Action{The sky fractures into polygons.  
A humanoid figure tumbles through —  
SWEDENBORG, bound in luminous chains of geometric notation.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
My God — Emanuel!
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant rushes to him.  
Swedenborg’s face is cracked like porcelain.  
His shadow, now a massive pillar of black geometry, thrashes behind him.}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
(hissing in pain)  
It… wants… you…
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Why me?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Because you can think what they cannot.  
You can define limits.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{The chains constrict — snapping segments of his shadow back into place.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
You are dying.
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
We all are, in the wrong description.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A massive SHADOW-LIMB reaches toward Kant —  
not cast by anything, but a free form of relation.}

\Action{Swedenborg yanks Kant aside —  
and is impaled through the chest by a line of pure absence.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
NO!
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{SWEDENBORG}
\begin{Dialogue}
Listen…  
Only you can seal the breach.  
Write the rule —  
or Reason will drown the world.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{His body dissolves into geometric dust.  
The chains clatter to the ground and evaporate.}

\Scene{INT. CATEGORY PLANE – ALTAR OF STRUCTURE}

\Action{A floating dais formed of axioms and definitions.  
At its center:  
KANT’S MANUSCRIPT, glowing faintly.  
Pages orbit like planets.}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Write the boundary.  
Now.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A quill of light materializes in Kant’s hand.  
He approaches the manuscript.  
Words squirm across the pages, fighting for coherence.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(fear, awe)  
What am I to write?
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
A world that cannot collapse under its own cognition.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant lowers the quill.  
The plane trembles.  
Geometric beings observe from impossible distances.}

\Action{Each word he writes stabilizes a part of the collapsing world:  
— inverted structures return to uprightness  
— shadows return to obedience  
— clocks regain rhythm  
— space ceases to fold}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(muttering as he writes)  
Thought must be bounded  
by the conditions of possible experience…
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{A shockwave ripples.  
The Architects nod slightly,  
impossibly tall heads tilting.}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Continue.
\end{Dialogue}

\Action{Kant writes faster —  
sweating, pale, trembling.  
Words blaze as black fire.}

\Action{The giant SHADOW-ENTITY —  
a skeletal skyscraper of relation —  
approaches.  
Its limbs fold like origami knives.  
Space dimples around it.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
I am not finished—
\end{Dialogue}

\Character{ARCHITECT (V.O.)}
\begin{Dialogue}
Then finish.
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{INT. CATEGORY PLANE – COLLAPSE EVENT}

\Action{The shadow-entity reaches for him.  
Kant slams the quill onto the final page.}

\Action{A sphere of pure structure erupts outward —  
forming a PERFECT CIRCLE around him.  
The entity recoils, shrieking soundlessly.}

\Action{Light fractures into cascading axioms.  
The category plane collapses to a single point—}

\Scene{INT. KANT’S STUDY – DAWN}

\Action{Kant awakens at his desk.  
Sweating.  
Weak.  
The manuscript lies before him —  
completed.  
Ordered.}

\Action{He turns a page.  
The sigil nowhere to be seen.  
The geometry holds.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
(tearful whisper)  
It held…
\end{Dialogue}

\Scene{EXT. KÖNIGSBERG – VARIOUS – MORNING}

\Action{A montage of REPAIRED REALITY:}

\Action{— The town clock ticks with perfect precision.  
— Shadows behave correctly.  
— The river flows normally.  
— No inverted city.  
— Children run across the square without distortion.}

\Action{Life resumes, oblivious to its near eclipse.}

\Scene{EXT. CHAPEL – DAY}

\Action{MAGDALENA, now a nun, lights a candle.  
She does not remember Kant.  
The sigil is absent from her journal.}

\Scene{INT. CLOCKMAKER ALBRECHT’S SHOP – DAY}

\Action{All clocks tick simultaneously for the first time in weeks.}

\Action{Albrecht glances out the window.  
Kant passes by.  
All clocks tick ONE BEAT SLOWER for a moment —  
respectfully —  
then return to normal.}

\Scene{EXT. BRIDGE – FUNERAL PROCESSION – DAY}

\Action{Swedenborg’s coffin passes.  
The RED-COATED MAN stands at the far end of the bridge —  
motionless.  
Watching.  
Fading from existence like an overwritten line of text.}

\Scene{EXT. KÖNIGSBERG UNIVERSITY – QUADRANGLE – SUNSET}

\Action{Kant walks alone, manuscript under his arm.  
He looks up at the sky.  
The stars are still.}

\Character{KANT}
\begin{Dialogue}
Reason must never see its own bones again.
\end{Dialogue}

\Transition{FADE OUT.}

\Scene{EXT. MODERN UNIVERSITY CAMPUS – DAY}

\Action{Students bustle across a sleek, glass-and-concrete quads.  
Laptops. Coffee. Earbuds. The ambient hum of the 21st century.}

\Action{A GRADUATE STUDENT (mid-20s, overworked, brilliant, absentminded)  
crosses a puddle near the mathematics building.}

\Action{For a moment — just the length of a blink —  
the puddle REFLECTS A SIGIL:  
the same impossible geometric form Kant saw in the stars in 1757.}

\Action{A perfect figure traced in light.  
A boundary, echoing across centuries.}

\Action{The student doesn’t notice.  
They step in the puddle and the reflection vanishes instantly.}

\Action{They continue on, unaware.  
A backpack strap slips off their shoulder; they adjust it.  
They check their email.  
A completely ordinary moment.}

\Action{Camera holds on the puddle.  
It ripples ONCE — no wind, no cause.  
Then stillness.}

\Scene{INT. MODERN LIBRARY – CONTINUOUS}

\Action{The graduate student enters a high-ceilinged reading room,  
passing by shelves of philosophy — including a worn copy of  
\textit{Critique of Pure Reason}.}

\Action{As they pass, the book THUMPS lightly on the shelf,  
as if settling into its place.}

\Scene{EXT. MODERN CAMPUS – ROOFTOP – SAME TIME}

\Action{A security camera pivots on its mount —  
without mechanical sound.  
Without user input.}

\Action{The camera lens adjusts, focusing downward on the student.}

\Action{Behind the camera, for one frame:  
A TALL, THIN SHADOW stands against the ventilation unit.  
Elongated. Faceless.  
Impossible proportions.}

\Action{The next frame — nothing.  
Just an empty rooftop.}

\Scene{EXT. CAMPUS – WIDE SHOT – DAY}

\Action{The student walks toward the library steps.  
They stop briefly.  
Turn.  
As if sensing something.}

\Action{Nothing is there.  
They shrug and continue on.}

\Transition{FADE TO BLACK.}

\Scene{TITLE OVER BLACK:}

\Action{\centering\textbf{“REASON IS THE VEIL.”}}

\Scene{FADE OUT.}
