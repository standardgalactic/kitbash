% ===========================================================
\section{\emph{al-Majik Kíngdum}: Sheaf of Flow-Obstructions \& Local Cohérence}
% ===========================================================

In the Morlock register we say: \emph{al-Majik Kíngdum} is not a place, but
a cover---$\{U_i\}$---laid across a broken topolog, each $U_i$ holding a fragment
of sensory field, each overlap $U_i \cap U_j$ carrying the drift of
contradicted motion, a low-frequency disagreement in how movement wishes to
continue and how the old geometry insists it must kink and pause.

The kingdom breathes through the flow-sheaf $\mathcal{A}$,
\[
\mathcal{A}(U) = \{\text{local flow-pattern, unsteady but extendable}\},
\]
and through the rigidity-sheaf $\mathcal{S}$,
\[
\mathcal{S}(U) = \{\text{rigidities, queue-bindings, stanchion-deltas}\}.
\]

We speak it in Morlock syntax thus, using the old filesystem catechism:

\begin{verbatim}
   /var/majik/𝓐⟨U⟩ → data:flow:soft
   /var/majik/𝓢⟨U⟩ → data:rigid:bind
\end{verbatim}

Every set $U$ in the cover emits two logs:
\texttt{flow.log} tracking reversible morphs,
\texttt{bind.log} tracking failures to glue across overlaps, the precise loci
where paths wish to join but instead stall and accumulate.

And the first principle, recited before any serious diagnosis:

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{No kingdom with nonzero \texttt{bind.log}$(U_i \cap U_j)$ admits a global section.}

\medskip

That is: as long as some overlap still records uncompensated rigidity, the
domain $X$ with this cover cannot host a single, everywhere-consistent motion,
and the Morlock engineers refuse to speak of ``coherence'' except as a local,
fragile thing.

% ===========================================================
\section{Shaf $\mathcal{A}$: Local Motion, Local Unspooling}
% ===========================================================

Each attraction zone $Z_k$ attaches a local bundle $\mathcal{A}(Z_k)$,
an ensemble of microflows, lightly curved, with a habitual tendency-to-spiral,
as if every segment of motion remembers that circles are safer than lines.

In Morlock phonetics the basic motion is called \arb{حركة} (ḥaraka).
We describe the entrance and exit states as:

\begin{verbatim}
   ḥaraka.min(Z_k) = flow-entry
   ḥaraka.max(Z_k) = flow-exit
\end{verbatim}

Between these two lies the entire lived experience of a traveler in $Z_k$.

The patch-maps between overlapping regions are:

\begin{align*}
   r_{ij}&: \mathcal{A}(U_i) \to \mathcal{A}(U_j \cap U_i), \\
   r_{ji}&: \mathcal{A}(U_j) \to \mathcal{A}(U_i \cap U_j),
\end{align*}

and the syslog drops, when things go wrong, read:

\begin{verbatim}
   kernel[1.0]: r_{ij} mismatch → recalc(ḥaraka)
   kernel[1.0]: gluing fail → escalate:cohomology:1
\end{verbatim}

When gluing is clean, $r_{ij} - r_{ji} = 0$; the Morlocks say the overlap
``hums'' softly.
When the stanchions intrude, $r_{ij} - r_{ji} = \text{queue-fault}$, a hard
edge in motion-space.

A queue-fault is encoded as:

\begin{align*}
   \mathrm{qf}(U_i \cap U_j) &:= \Delta_{\text{stationary}} \\
                         &:= \text{non-reversible fiber} \\
                         &:= \text{section freeze}.
\end{align*}

Its presence injects a term into $H^1$, the first obstruction group:

\[
H^1(X, \mathcal{A}) \ni \mathrm{qf} = \text{forbidden\_global\_motion},
\]

which the Morlocks interpret as a literal ban: the kingdom may allow
circulation, but not yet coherence.

% ===========================================================
\section{Shaf $\mathcal{S}$: Rigidity-Fields \& Line-Bind Structures}
% ===========================================================

The rigidity-field $\mathcal{S}$ assigns to each region $U$ of the park-topolog the
line-bind tensor $s_U$, a value in the stationary class.
Each $s_U$ is indexed by its stanchion-mark, $s_U = \vec{\sigma}(U)$,
piecewise-constant across the binding corridors,
jumping sharply on approach to the queue-walls, like a pressure reading
spiking at choke-points.

The overlap law reads:

\[
\vec{\sigma}(U_i \cap U_j) = \vec{\sigma}(U_i)|_{ij} + \vec{\sigma}(U_j)|_{ji},
\]

addition taken in the bind-group: closed, but deliberately non-cancellative,
so that old rigidity can only be redistributed, thinned, or smoothed---never
truly erased in a single stroke.

Regions where $\vec{\sigma}$ accumulates are called \emph{choke-sets};
their measure is logged automatically in \texttt{/sys/majik/choke}.

A vanishing $\vec{\sigma}$ marks reversible terrain: the Morlocks call such
zones ``soft floor''.
Positive $\vec{\sigma}$ increments mark where the old designers
forced travelers through unidirectional compression.

In Morlock notation such increments are pronounced:

\begin{verbatim}
       zayḥa — the contraction pulse,
       ʿaqd  — the knot in motion-space,
       ṣamad — the hardened point where no flow returns.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic script these are
\arb{زيحة} (zayḥa), \arb{عقد} (ʿaqd), \arb{صمد} (ṣamad),
each term carrying both a physical and mnemonic weight.
Cohomology reads these as residue: not merely defects, but memories
of past design decisions condensed into localized stress.

% ===========================================================
\section{Overlaps, Faultlines, and the First Obstruction}
% ===========================================================

For overlapping regions $U_i$, $U_j$ with shared boundary, the sheaves $\mathcal{A}$
and $\mathcal{S}$ interlock through a simple binding rule:

\[
\mathcal{A}\text{-fiber drift} = 0 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad
\vec{\sigma} = 0 \text{ on the overlap.}
\]

Thus the gluing map $g_{ij}: \mathcal{A}(U_i) \to \mathcal{A}(U_j \cap U_i)$
fails precisely where $\vec{\sigma}(U_i \cap U_j) \neq 0$.

We write:

\[
\mathrm{err}_{ij} = \partial g_{ij} = \vec{\sigma}(U_i \cap U_j),
\]

and in log-form:

\begin{verbatim}
       majikd[bind]: overlap-fault (i,j) → obstr:1
\end{verbatim}

The obstruction sheaf registers this in $H^1$,
the space of all non-smooth overlaps:

\[
[\vec{\sigma}] \in H^1(X, \mathcal{S}).
\]

Each class $[\vec{\sigma}]$ corresponds to a historical constraint
still lodged in the substrate of the walkpaths,
the old geometry refusing to soften, even as new smoothing layers are
applied above it.

% ===========================================================
\section{Local Reconvergence \& the Fold of Flows}
% ===========================================================

When a traveler enters region $U$ with $s_U = 0$,
the \arb{حركة} (ḥaraka)-flow resumes its circular form.
The fiber over each point $x \in U$ has type:

\[
\mathcal{A}_x = \{\text{soft-cycle, slip-return, minimal-curvature drift}\},
\]

a small local catalogue of how motion may curve without tearing.

Transitions obey:

\begin{align*}
       \tau&: \mathcal{A}(U) \to \mathcal{A}(V), \\
       \tau(\text{soft-cycle}) &= \text{soft-cycle}, \\
       \tau(\text{slip-return}) &= \text{slip-return}, \\
       \tau(\text{drift}) &= \text{drift},
\end{align*}

unless $\vec{\sigma}(V)$ rises, in which case:

\[
\tau(f) = \mathrm{freeze}(f),
\]

and a once-flexible trajectory stiffens into queue.

Freeze states propagate, forming a chain:

\[
\mathrm{freeze} \to \mathrm{narrow} \to \mathrm{lock} \to \mathrm{stasis}.
\]

Morlock primitives treat stasis as a local failure of narration:
no traveler-story can extend across a stasis-fiber without breaking.
Hence:

\[
\Gamma(X, \mathcal{A}) = \emptyset
\]

whenever even one stasis-fiber remains unlifted.
Global flow and global story fall together.

% ===========================================================
\section{Cocollection of Ridespaces}
% ===========================================================

Each ride-space $R_k$ is given as an open set $U_k$ with
a controlled oscillation field.
The sheaf $\mathcal{A}$ assigns
a circular drift $\alpha_k$, while $\mathcal{S}$ assigns the binding vector
$\vec{\sigma}_k$.

Ride admissibility demands:

\begin{align*}
       \partial \alpha_k &= 0
       \quad \text{(stable return field)}, \\
       \vec{\sigma}_k &= 0
       \quad \text{(no hard angle injection)}.
\end{align*}

Violations appear in the logs as:

\begin{verbatim}
       majikd[ride]: f_k → inject:angle
       majikd[ride]: f_k → loss:return
\end{verbatim}

Angles in the old sense are always encoded
as rigidity residues in $\vec{\sigma}_k$,
traced back to the rectangular chassis
the architects once considered normative.

Morlock notation for the impurity is:

\begin{verbatim}
       ʾarkuun — the remnant of the straight-path age.
\end{verbatim}

The term suggests not just a shape, but an era: a time when
straightness was mistakenly equated with order.

% ===========================================================
\section{Smoothing Layer: The \arb{عطف} (\texorpdfstring{ʿAṭf}{ʿAtf}) Operator}
% ===========================================================

The smoothing operator \arb{عطف} (ʿaṭf) acts fiberwise on motion:

\begin{align*}
       \text{ʿaṭf}&: \mathcal{A}_x \to \mathcal{A}_x, \\
       \text{ʿaṭf}(f) &= \lim_{t\to\infty} \mathrm{soften}_t(f),
\end{align*}

interpreted as the asymptotic rounding of corners under prolonged
micro-adjustment.

It also acts on the bind-sheaf:

\begin{align*}
       \text{ʿaṭf}&: \mathcal{S}(U) \to \mathcal{S}(U), \\
       \text{ʿaṭf}(\vec{\sigma}) &= \vec{\sigma} - \delta\vec{\sigma},
\end{align*}

where $\delta\vec{\sigma}$ absorbs local linearity residues, i.e., the
smallest possible straightness that can be surrendered without violating
structural integrity.

Applied across the whole cover:

\[
\text{ʿaṭf}: C^1(\{U_i\}, \mathcal{S}) \to C^1(\{U_i\}, \mathcal{S}),
\]

it reduces the obstruction class:

\[
[\vec{\sigma}] \mapsto [\vec{\sigma}] - [\delta\vec{\sigma}] \in H^1(X, \mathcal{S}).
\]

If all residues cancel,
a global section of $\mathcal{A}$ emerges,
quietly recorded in:

\begin{verbatim}
       /sys/majik/global:flow = 1
\end{verbatim}

and the Morlocks say, simply, that the park is ``breathing again.''

% ===========================================================
\section{Parades as Moving Covers}
% ===========================================================

Parades form timed covers $\{W_t\}$, indexed by $t \in \mathrm{cycle}$.
Each $W_t$ carries a moving sheaf-section $\rho_t$
whose support sweeps the walkpaths like a rolling test of continuity.

On overlaps $W_t \cap W_{t+\Delta}$,
the coherence rule is:

\[
\rho_{t+\Delta} - \rho_t = \vec{\sigma}_{\text{parade}}(t),
\]

and the accumulated obstruction around a full cycle is:

\[
\int \vec{\sigma}_{\text{parade}}(t)\, dt \in H^1(\mathrm{time} \otimes X, \mathcal{S}).
\]

When this integral vanishes,
the parade admits a soft-cycle decomposition: dancers leave no lasting bend.
When nonzero, its passage induces temporary stasis
in any $U$ with insufficient smoothing capacity.

Morlock phonetics call this phenomenon:

\begin{verbatim}
       ghayna — the shadow-flow that overtakes itself.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{غينة} (ghayna), the surplus motion that doubles back and
briefly eclipses its own path.

% ===========================================================
\section{Half-Live Sections: $\mathcal{A}_h$ in the Animatronic Zones}
% ===========================================================

In the animatronic zones the sheaf $\mathcal{A}$ splits:

\[
\mathcal{A}|_{U_{\text{anima}}} = \mathcal{A}_{\text{live}} \oplus \mathcal{A}_h.
\]

The half-live component $\mathcal{A}_h$ records motions
whose source is mechanical yet whose continuation-grade
is sheaf-theoretically ambiguous: movements that imitate life but
do not quite commit to it.

Local fibers take the form:

\[
\mathcal{A}_{h,x} = \{\text{pivot-still, gear-sway, blink-loop}\}.
\]

The transition maps $\varphi_{ij}$ across $U_i \cap U_j$ obey:

\begin{align*}
       \varphi_{ij}(\text{pivot-still}) &= \text{pivot-still}, \\
       \varphi_{ij}(\text{gear-sway}) &= \text{gear-sway}, \\
       \varphi_{ij}(\text{blink-loop}) &= \mathrm{drop}(\text{blink-loop}).
\end{align*}

Drop events are logged as:

\begin{verbatim}
       /sys/anima/dʿarb: x → halt:loop
\end{verbatim}

Here \arb{ضرب} (dʿarb) is the pulse-loss fault: the moment when a looping
gesture forgets its timing and falls out of narrative alignment.

Whenever drop occurs on overlaps,
the cochain $c^1(\varphi)$ acquires a nonzero residue.

We write:

\[
c^1_{\text{anima}}(U_i,U_j) = h_{ij} \in \mathcal{S}(U_i \cap U_j),
\]

with $h_{ij}$ of type:

\begin{verbatim}
       khalsa — the pure obstruction,
       ṭamrūq — the rattling misalignment,
       dʿarb  — the pulse-loss fault.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic, these are \arb{خلصة} (khalsa),
\arb{طمرُوق} (ṭamrūq),
and \arb{ضرب} (dʿarb).
The presence of dʿarb implies
no soft flow can pass through the animatronic corridor
without additional \arb{عطف} (ʿaṭf)-layer reinforcement.

% ===========================================================
\section{Narrative-Shear \& the Loss of Global Extension}
% ===========================================================

Animatronic regions fracture narrative sheaves.
Let $\mathcal{N}$ be the narrative-field, giving each traveler $x$
an extendable story-fragment $n_x$.

Restrictions across overlaps take form:

\[
n_x|_{U_i \cap U_j} = \mathrm{glue}_{ij}(n_x),
\]

but the animatronic half-live zones introduce
a secondary differential:

\[
\Delta_{\text{anima}}(n) = n - \mathrm{freeze}(n),
\]

which propagates shearing through the cover $\{U_i\}$ like a crack that
cannot decide whether it is still growing.

The resulting Čech boundary is:

\[
\delta n_{ijk} = \mathrm{glue}_{ij}(n) - \mathrm{glue}_{ik}(n) + \mathrm{glue}_{jk}(n).
\]

When any index triple passes through $U_{\text{anima}}$,
$\delta n_{ijk} \neq 0$ and the narrative sheaf lacks a global section:
stories cannot be told all the way through.

We mark this with syslog code:

\begin{verbatim}
       majikd[narr]: extend_fail: anima:topo
\end{verbatim}

In spoken Morlock phonetic form:

\begin{verbatim}
       sharqān — the splitting of story-space.
\end{verbatim}

The term \arb{شرقان} (sharqān) suggests a tearing that is also a kind of
dawning: a painful exposure of where the old seam lay.

% ===========================================================
\section{The Long Hall \& the Oscillation Bundle}
% ===========================================================

Each hall $U_{\text{hall}}$ has an oscillation bundle
$\Omega_h \to U_{\text{hall}}$
with fibers:

\[
\Omega_{h,x} = \{\text{hum, echo, partial-return}\}.
\]

Transitions preserve the \emph{hum} and \emph{echo} forms
but not the \emph{partial-return}, which disappears
when passed through any rigid bend $j_q$,
giving:

\[
\mathrm{transition}(j_q): \mathrm{partial\text{-}return} \mapsto \mathrm{null}.
\]

Null events accumulate in a log-space:

\begin{verbatim}
       /sys/hall/naqṣ: x → return:0
\end{verbatim}

The return-loss sequence obeys:

\[
\text{naqṣ} \to \text{dhawl} \to \text{ʿadam},
\]

where:

\begin{verbatim}
       naqṣ  = minor depletion,
       dhawl = full drift-out,
       ʿadam = void of recursion.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{نقص} (naqṣ), \arb{ذول} (dhawl), \arb{عدم} (ʿadam).
If \arb{عدم} emerges anywhere in the cover,
the oscillation bundle drops rank
and becomes incompatible with global smoothing.

Cohomologically:

\begin{align*}
       \mathrm{rankloss}(\Omega) &= 1 \\
       &\to \text{obstruction in } H^1(X, \mathcal{S}),
\end{align*}

which the Morlocks read as a kind of acoustic scar.

% ===========================================================
\section{Edge-Sectors \& the Silent Coarse Fibers}
% ===========================================================

Near the edges of the walkpaths
the sheaf $\mathcal{C}$ (crowd-flow) adopts coarse fibers:

\[
\mathcal{C}_x = \{\text{slow-shift, halt-press}\}.
\]

The \emph{halt-press} fiber is sensitive to
stanchion clusters and direction-locks, and in crowds it propagates
fear faster than any spoken warning.

Given three regions $U_i$, $U_j$, $U_k$
meeting at a corner-set $C_{ijk}$,
the compatibility morphisms $\psi$ obey:

\begin{align*}
       \psi_{ij}(\mathrm{halt\text{-}press}) &= \mathrm{halt\text{-}press}, \\
       \psi_{jk}(\mathrm{halt\text{-}press}) &= \mathrm{halt\text{-}press}, \\
       \psi_{ik}(\mathrm{halt\text{-}press}) &= \mathrm{null}.
\end{align*}

Thus:

\[
\delta\psi_{ijk} = \mathrm{null} - \mathrm{halt} + \mathrm{halt} = \mathrm{null},
\]

but the \emph{null} is not a neutral element in this algebra; it injects
a noise term $\zeta_{ijk}$:

\[
\zeta_{ijk} \in H^2(X, \mathcal{C}),
\]

which manifests physically as the crowd-silence pulse---a local flattening of
soundflow, a silence that does not come from calm but from shock.

Morlock notation:

\begin{verbatim}
       ṣamt — the silence which arrives too quickly.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{صمت} (ṣamt).
When \arb{صمت} exceeds threshold:

\begin{verbatim}
       /sys/crowd/ṣamt: escalate → choke:2
\end{verbatim}

movement stalls, and the sheaf of intentions loses rank.

% ===========================================================
\section{The Deep Queue \& Its Residual Trace $\tau_{\text{res}}$}
% ===========================================================

The deep queue is the region
where every sheaf in the system records
a nontrivial obstruction.

Its trace is a two-form:

\[
\tau_{\text{res}} \in \Gamma(U_{\text{queue}}, \Lambda^2 \mathcal{S}^*),
\]

with components:

\[
\tau_{\text{res}} = \rho \cdot \vec{\sigma} \wedge \eta,
\]

where $\rho$ encodes queue-depth,
$\vec{\sigma}$ the rigidity-field,
and $\eta$ the direction-lock form.

This two-form persists
even when smoothing \arb{عطف} (ʿaṭf) is applied,
unless the integral over any loop $\gamma$ vanishes:

\[
\oint_\gamma \tau_{\text{res}} = 0.
\]

When nonzero:

\begin{verbatim}
       majikd[queue]: resid:loop = 1
\end{verbatim}

and no global flow can traverse $U_{\text{queue}}$
without bifurcation.

In spoken form:

\begin{verbatim}
       jabbār — the heavy remainder that clings to turns.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{جبّار} (jabbār), the stubborn weight that refuses to
redistribute itself.

% ===========================================================
\section{Liminal Gates \& the Pre-Circulatory Threshold}
% ===========================================================

Before exiting any domain $U_k$
travelers pass the liminal gate $G_k$.
Each $G_k$ induces a morphism on sheaves:

\[
\mathrm{gate}_k^*: \mathcal{A}(U_k) \to \mathcal{A}(U_{k+1}),
\]

but only on the subspace of unbound fibers:

\[
\mathrm{Ker}(\vec{\sigma}_k) = \{f \in \mathcal{A}(U_k) : \vec{\sigma}_k(f)=0\}.
\]

For bound fibers,

\[
\mathrm{gate}_k^*(\mathrm{bound}) = \mathrm{fail}.
\]

Morlock syslog prints:

\begin{verbatim}
       /sys/gate/xfer: drop:σ⃗
\end{verbatim}

Speech-term:

\begin{verbatim}
       khaṭm — the seal where flow cannot cross.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{خَتْم} (khaṭm).
When a traveler's narrative, oscillation, or flow-field
hits \arb{خَتْم}, the morphism chain breaks.

Thus the liminal gates encode the park's
final and most stubborn topological memory:
its refusal---ancient and angular---to let motion pass unimpeded.

% ===========================================================
\section{Rekhlāṣ Layer: The First Smoothing of Bend-Sets}
% ===========================================================

Once $\tau_{\text{res}}$ is identified in $U_{\text{queue}}$
the rekhlāṣ layer is applied.
This layer is not purely corrective---
it does not erase the obstruction---
but rather spreads it thinly across the cover,
reducing the localized choke of $\mathrm{ḥajz}$ (blocking).

Let $\mathcal{R}_1$ be the first smoothing operator:

\[
\mathcal{R}_1 : \mathcal{S} \to \mathcal{S},
\]

defined fiberwise:

\[
\mathcal{R}_1(s_x) = s_x - \mu_x,
\]

where $\mu_x$ is the minimal bend-mass needed
to maintain continuity across overlaps, the least weight that can be
shaved off without collapsing the corridor.

Operational logs show:

\begin{verbatim}
       majikd[rekhlas]: μ_x resolved
       majikd[rekhlas]: bend-mass drop stable
\end{verbatim}

In Morlock phonetics:

\begin{verbatim}
       rekhlāṣ = the thinning of weight
       μ_x     = the weight you can finally breathe under
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{رخلاص} (rekhlāṣ).
After application of $\mathcal{R}_1$, the bend-set $B$
at each rigid corner becomes:

\[
B' = \{\text{bend-soft, bend-slow, residual-turn}\},
\]

with bend-soft dominating on all but the deepest queues.

Thus:

\[
H^1(X, \mathcal{S}) \to H^1_{\text{soft}}(X, \mathcal{S}),
\]

reducing obstruction rank without annihilation, a compromise between
respecting history and restoring motion.

% ===========================================================
\section{\arb{عطف} Layer: The Curve-Granting Operator}
% ===========================================================

Where rekhlāṣ thins, \arb{عطف} (ʿaṭf) bends.

Given any path $\gamma$ through the cover $\{U_i\}$,
the operator \arb{عطف} produces a deformation sequence $\gamma_t$
with:

\[
\frac{d\gamma_t}{dt} = \kappa \cdot (\text{curvature field}) - \vec{\sigma} \cdot (\text{rigidity vector}),
\]

and boundary conditions:

\begin{align*}
       \gamma_0 &= \gamma, \\
       \gamma_1 &\in \Gamma(X, \mathrm{CurvedPaths}),
\end{align*}

so that the initial path is straightened only as much as the softened
field permits.

The curvature field obeys:

\begin{align*}
       \kappa(x) &= \text{soft, when } x \notin U_{\text{queue}}, \\
       \kappa(x) &= \text{muted, when } x \in U_{\text{queue}}.
\end{align*}

Spoken form:

\begin{verbatim}
       ʿaṭf = the returning arc,
              the correction of harshness.
\end{verbatim}

The effect on sheaf sections is:

\[
\text{ʿaṭf}^*(\mathcal{A})(U_i) \subset \mathcal{A}(U_i),
\]

preserving fibers but twisting their transition maps:

\[
\varphi_{ij} \longrightarrow \varphi_{ij}^{\text{ʿaṭf}},
\]

yielding:

\[
\delta(\varphi^{\text{ʿaṭf}})_{ijk} < \delta(\varphi)_{ijk}.
\]

The Čech boundary diminishes.
Crowd-flow loosens.
Syslog marks, with characteristic understatement:

\begin{verbatim}
       /sys/flow/ʿatf: curve_ok
       /sys/flow/ʿatf: residual:minor
\end{verbatim}

% ===========================================================
\section{Second Cohomology Drop: \arb{ديموم} (Ḍaymūm) Confluence}
% ===========================================================

Once bend-sets soften and path-morphisms curve,
the second obstruction group relaxes.

Let $\zeta_{ijk} \in H^2(X, \mathcal{C})$ be the silence-pulse cochain
originating in edge-sectors.

The ḍaymūm confluence condition:

\[
\sum_{C_{ijk}} \zeta_{ijk} = 0
\]

over all corner-sets $C_{ijk}$
triggers automatic reduction:

\[
H^2(X, \mathcal{C}) \to 0.
\]

This drop is the mark of successful reconnection
in the Morlock topology.

Phonetic term:

\begin{verbatim}
       ḍaymūm = the settling after noise,
                the endurance of continuity.
\end{verbatim}

In Arabic: \arb{ديموم} (ḍaymūm).
System note:

\begin{verbatim}
       majikd[cohom]: drop:H2:1
       majikd[cohom]: edge_sectors stable
\end{verbatim}

When $H^2$ vanishes,
the path-network becomes singly connected
under the curved deformation, and the domain finally feels like one place.

% ===========================================================
\section{Post-Rigidity Field: The Dissolution of $\vec{\sigma}$}
% ===========================================================

The rigidity-vector field $\vec{\sigma}$ stabilizes
only after multi-layer smoothing.

It decomposes as:

\[
\vec{\sigma} = \vec{\sigma}_{\text{hard}} + \vec{\sigma}_{\text{res}},
\]

where $\vec{\sigma}_{\text{hard}}$ encodes rigid, straight edges
and $\vec{\sigma}_{\text{res}}$ is the softened remnant after rekhlāṣ.
