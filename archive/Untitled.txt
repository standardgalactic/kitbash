\begin{quote}\ttfamily\small

\begin{verbatim}
…U_i  ∩  U_j … faint… r_{ij} … no return…

There was a line here, believed to say:  
\begin{verbatim}
   "the seam does not open."
\end{verbatim}

\begin{verbatim}
   ʿaṭf… lost curvature…

\end{verbatim}

\begin{verbatim}

\end{verbatim}

\begin{verbatim}
   H¹(… … )  ≠ 0  
   H²(X, … )  =   [blank]  
   ζ… smear of blackened ink…  
\end{verbatim}

Final surviving strokes:

\begin{verbatim}
   zāw…   zāwi…   angle-sleep…  
   … athar remains …  
\end{verbatim}

At the bottom edge:

\begin{verbatim}
   huwa   …   fī …   daw…  
\end{verbatim}

Scholars propose the intended closure was:  
\begin{verbatim}
   huwa fī 'l-dawrān  
\end{verbatim}
but the last three letters dissolved in the polymer layer.

\end{quote}
