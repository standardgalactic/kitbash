Higher-Order Attention Is All We Need 

Self-attention is fundamentally biased toward pairwise relations. 

While effective for many tasks, this design becomes inefficient when reasoning requires higher-order interactions. A classic example is the triple matching problem: deciding whether three elements jointly satisfy a relation. 

In standard Transformers, this requires multiple layers of pairwise hops, introducing unnecessary complexity and instability. Stacking layers does not change the primitive bias.

This limitation motivates research into higher-order attention mechanisms. One approach is tensor attention, which extends the compatibility function from bilinear (query–key) to trilinear (query–key–key). This allows triple relations to be captured directly in a single step, instead of being composed indirectly through depth.

Higher-order extensions (triplet, quadruple, even hypergraph-based attention) may be essential for tasks where the fundamental structure is not dyadic but inherently multi-entity.
