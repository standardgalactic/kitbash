# Research Proposal: Empirical Validation and Theoretical Extension of the Effective Assembly Index for Mind Recognition (EAIMR)

## 1.0 Introduction and Rationale

The scientific study of intelligence is confronted by a central and persistent challenge: the lack of a universal, substrate-neutral definition. Traditional frameworks that identify intelligence with outward behavior, internal symbolic representation, or rational optimization presuppose privileged ontologies of agents, goals, or symbols. These assumptions restrict applicability to familiar biological systems and hinder extension to artificial, alien, or non-traditional forms of organization.

This proposal advances the **Effective Assembly (EA)** framework, which reconceptualizes intelligence not as a faculty or substance, but as a specific *compression regime*. Within this framework, intelligence is characterized by how efficiently a system assembles, reuses, and stabilizes structure over time. Intelligence is thus defined as **compression that survives contact with reality**.

The **Effective Assembly Index for Mind Recognition (EAIMR)** is the central formal construct of this framework. It is a quantitative, non-behavioral, and substrate-neutral criterion designed to identify mind-like organization by measuring how effectively a system balances raw construction complexity against structural efficiencies such as reuse, parallelism, degeneracy, and coherence.

The objective of this proposal is to secure funding for a foundational research program to transform EAIMR from a theoretical construct into a rigorously tested, empirically validated scientific instrument.

## 2.0 Theoretical Foundations of the EAIMR Framework

### 2.1 From Statistical Compression to Structural Assembly

Classical Shannon compression measures statistical entropy reduction but is insufficient for characterizing intelligence. Shannon entropy ignores construction history and functional organization. It fails to capture:

- **Construction depth:** Entropy does not encode how a structure was built.
- **Reuse:** Redundant duplication and abstract reuse are treated identically.
- **Integration:** Coherent systems and disjoint aggregates may share entropy.
- **Robustness:** Fragile and resilient systems can appear equivalent.

These limitations motivate the shift to **Assembly Theory**, which measures the minimal number of distinct construction steps required to produce a structure. This captures *irreducible construction depth*, grounding complexity in replayable generative history.

### 2.2 The Effective Assembly Index for Mind Recognition (EAIMR)

Raw assembly alone does not distinguish mind-like systems from random aggregates. EAIMR corrects this by discounting raw assembly via structural efficiencies and amplifying it by coherence:

- **A(x): Assembly Index** — irreducible construction depth.
- **R(x): Reuse** — repeated invocation of substructures across contexts.
- **P(x): Parallelism** — degree of concurrent construction or operation.
- **D(x): Degeneracy** — multiple structurally distinct implementations of the same function.
- **C(x): Coherence** — ability to localize perturbations and maintain global consistency.

Systems with similar raw complexity may differ radically in EAIMR depending on how effectively complexity is invested rather than merely accumulated.

### 2.3 The Mind Regime: A Dynamical Criterion

EAIMR avoids static thresholds. A system enters the **Mind Regime** when:

> d/dt EAIMR(x(t)) > 0

and this growth is **internally driven**, arising from the system’s own learning or abstraction mechanisms rather than external inscription. Such systems become increasingly efficient compressors of their own structure and environment.

## 3.0 Statement of the Problem and Research Objectives

EAIMR currently exists as a formal theory without empirical validation. The core challenge is operationalizing EAIMR as a falsifiable, predictive instrument.

### Research Objectives

1. **Develop computable estimators** for EAIMR components across substrates.
2. **Empirically validate rankings** across biological and artificial systems.
3. **Theoretically ground EAIMR** in non-equilibrium thermodynamics.

---

## 4.0 Proposed Research Program and Methodology

### 4.1 Research Thrust 1: Computable Estimators

#### Artificial Substrates

- **A(x):** Approximate minimal assembly graphs via dependency analysis.
- **R(x):** Call-graph reuse ratios.
- **P(x):** Critical path length vs. total dependency depth.
- **D(x):** Count of distinct implementations per functional role.
- **C(x):** Perturbation propagation from localized interventions.

#### Biological Substrates

- **A(x):** Developmental and learning depth from time-series data.
- **R(x):** Circuit reuse across behavioral tasks.
- **D(x):** Functional recovery under lesion or inhibition.
- **C(x):** Effective connectivity spread under stimulation.

### 4.2 Research Thrust 2: Empirical Benchmark Validation

Candidate systems:

1. Autocatalytic chemical networks
2. Prokaryotic organisms
3. Simple multicellular organisms
4. Complex mammals
5. Artificial neural networks (varied architectures)

Success criterion: statistically significant monotonic increase in EAIMR across this ordering.

### 4.3 Research Thrust 3: Theoretical Grounding

- Formalize links between EAIMR and entropy production.
- Relate effective assembly growth to free energy dissipation.
- Clarify EAIMR’s relationship to phenomenology (correlate, not identity).

## 5.0 Potential Implications and Broader Impact

### 5.1 AI Safety and Alignment

EAIMR offers a structural early-warning signal for emergent agency. It distinguishes deeply integrated value structures from brittle overlays and enables pre-behavioral safety assessment.

### 5.2 Astrobiology and SETI

EAIMR reframes intelligence detection as a structural problem. Mind-like organization may be detectable without communication, across unconventional substrates.

### 5.3 A Universal Science of Intelligence

EAIMR enables:

- Substrate-neutral comparison
- Scalar (non-binary) intelligence measures
- Unified biological, artificial, and physical intelligence theory

---

## 6.0 Conclusion

This proposal advances a research program to empirically validate a novel theory of intelligence. Intelligence is framed not as behavior but as **effective assembly**—compression that learns to compress itself.

By operationalizing EAIMR through empirical measurement, benchmarking, and physical grounding, this work aims to establish a foundational, falsifiable science of intelligence applicable across natural and artificial systems.
