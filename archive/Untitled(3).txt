And now, to speak of our new mountains: the platforms which once promised to raise up the voices of a people, to bring us tidings from across the earth, to make common what was once hidden in the chambers of the powerful. These, which might have stood as bulwarks for democracy, are now hewn down, hollowed out, and carried off—not for the sake of truth, but for the sake of gain.

For in Canada it has come to pass that the very substance of news is proscribed; the hard-won labors of reporters, the perilous journeys of those who bear witness—all these are declared too costly to share. Yet the streams are left open for trifles: false reports, counterfeit faces, petty scams and imitations, as if the very bowels of civic life might be replaced with marbles of no weight.

Behold the folly: that which could steady the state is set aside, while that which corrodes it is trafficked freely. And men, lulled by the glow of their screens, take their draughts from vessels filled not with water but with shadows, thinking themselves refreshed while the substance of truth is carted away.
