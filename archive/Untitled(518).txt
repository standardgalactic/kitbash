Based on di paper wey you give, na so di “Event-History” idea of how mind dey work be, and how e take connect to modern platforms.

Event-History Way of Thinking About Cognition

Di writer dey talk say real intelligence no dey inside static picture, final answer, or stored symbol. E dey inside di full history of events — how system take build, change, correct, and constrain itself over time. So focus shift from “state” go “event.”

Wetín be Event:
Event na change wey get rule and limit, and once e happen, e permanently change wetín system fit do later.

Memory no be Store Room:
Memory no be warehouse for symbols. Na build-up of structure and limits wey past events don create.

Intelligence na Lineage:
How system take grow and build from start decide which ideas go stable and which new change e fit handle.

Forgetting no be Loss:
Forgetting here mean collapse — system reduce detail from history to meet outside pressure like money, storage, or physical limit.

Comparing With Modern Platforms

Di paper talk say many modern platforms — especially cloud services like Gmail — dey fight against this event-based way of thinking. Dem do am through something wey di author call “information feudalism.”

Gmail as Example

Di author use Gmail as main example. At first, Gmail promise say storage go plenty, no need delete mail. Later dem switch to shared quota system. This happen even when hardware don cheap, so e no be technical problem — na business move.

By charging for every attachment and revision, Gmail dey force users to pay with their memory. You must delete your own history to avoid money wahala. Di paper call this “cognitive tax.”
