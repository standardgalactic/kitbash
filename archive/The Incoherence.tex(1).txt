% --- LOGLINE ---
\sectionhead{LOGLINE}

\action{In 12th-century Córdoba, philosopher-physician Ibn Rushd (Averroes) confronts a series of apparent miracles that threaten the foundations of rational medicine and law. When he discovers the "signs" are being manufactured by the state to control public belief, he must choose between speaking the truth that will cost him everything—or protecting the institutions he has built by remaining silent.}

\vspace{1em}

\sectionhead{THEMES}

\action{THE INCOHERENCE explores the tension between faith and reason, institutional power and intellectual honesty, and the vulnerability of knowledge in times of political crisis. It examines how authority uses spectacle to replace argument, and how ideas survive through exile, translation, and accident rather than triumph.}

\clearpage

% --- BEGIN SCREENPLAY ---

\slug{FADE IN:}

\slug{1\quad EXT. CÓRDOBA -- MEDICAL SCHOOL COURTYARD -- DAY}

\action{Midday. White Andalusian light fractures across limestone paving, producing hard-edged shadows that make every surface legible. Heat shimmers faintly above the stone, as if the air itself were breathing.}

\action{A low wooden table stands beneath a stretched canvas awning. On it: brass instruments laid with deliberate order, ceramic bowls, folded strips of linen, a shallow basin of water reflecting a clipped rectangle of sky. Nothing decorative. Everything chosen.}

\action{IBN RUSHD — AVERROES — early sixties, lean, spare, with the hands of a man who has spent decades alternating between pen and blade, sits cross-legged on a cushion. His fingers are permanently darkened by ink and medicinal stains. His posture is exact, economical.}

\action{Before him sits a PATIENT — forties, laborer, shoulders thick from work — his tunic pulled aside to expose a swollen shoulder joint, skin taut, flushed, angry with heat.}

\action{A semicircle of STUDENTS surrounds the table. Twelve in number. Muslim, Jewish, Christian. Some barely old enough to shave; others older, hands scarred by former trades. They watch with varying mixtures of confidence, anxiety, and hope.}

\action{At the back, half in shadow, a figure in a plain student djellaba sits very still. Hood drawn. This is AʿISHA BINT RUSHD, nineteen, disguised with practiced ease. She does not look away.}

\action{Averroes takes the patient’s wrist between thumb and forefinger. He closes his eyes.}

\action{We do not hear a literal pulse. Instead, the rhythm is suggested — a faint emphasis in the ambient sound, almost imperceptible, syncing breath to beat.}

\action{He releases the wrist. Opens his eyes. Looks not immediately at the injury, but at the basin of water, then the angle of shadow along the courtyard wall. Time, measured without display.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{to the students}
\dialogue{The pulse is fast but regular. The heat is localized. The skin is red, not darkened. This tells us the body is reacting, not failing.}

\action{A STUDENT — eager, sharp-featured — leans forward.}

\character{STUDENT}
\dialogue{Master, how can we be certain this reaction is the cause, and not merely the sign?}

\action{Averroes turns his head slowly. He treats the question as fragile, not foolish.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Because signs repeat. Causes persist. If you confuse them, you will chase shadows until the patient dies of patience.}

\action{A few students exchange glances. One nods. Another frowns.}

\character{STUDENT \contd}
\dialogue{But some say illness is a test. That the body suffers because God wishes it so.}

\action{A murmur moves through the semicircle — agreement, hesitation, relief.}

\action{Averroes does not sigh. He does not rebuke. He adjusts his grip on the lancet, grounding himself in the work.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{If you believe that, you may pray. Prayer is not forbidden here.}

\beat

\dialogue{But if you stay, you will observe first. Intention does not substitute for attention.}

\action{He raises the lancet slightly so all can see it. The blade catches the light, precise, unadorned.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{This tool has no theology. It cuts whether you are righteous or cruel. That is not blasphemy. That is reliability.}

\action{He makes a small, exact incision. The PATIENT flinches, then exhales as pressure releases. Dark, amber-tinged fluid drains into a waiting cup.}

\action{The courtyard holds its breath — then releases it. Life resumes at a lower volume.}

\action{AʿISHA watches closely. Not the blood. The angle of entry. The restraint. The refusal to dramatize pain.}

\action{As Averroes cleans the wound, the camera drifts — not insistently — to a shaded alcove at the courtyard’s edge.}

\action{There: a CLEPSYDRA. A simple water clock. Water drips from one vessel to another. Drip. Drip. Drip.}

\action{The sound is not emphasized. It is simply present — as natural as breath.}

\character{AVERROES}
\parenthetical{as he works}
\dialogue{When you see suffering, do not ask first what God intends. Ask what the body is doing. Healing leaves traces. So does decay.}

\beat

\dialogue{If you refuse to read those traces, you will call ignorance piety and negligence humility.}

\action{He finishes, binds the wound, presses gently to ensure firmness.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\parenthetical{to the patient}
\dialogue{Clean twice daily. If the redness spreads beyond this line, return immediately.}

\action{He traces a careful boundary with his finger. The patient nods, grateful, chastened.}

\action{The patient is helped away. Students disperse slowly, talking in low voices.}

\action{AʿISHA remains seated. Averroes packs his instruments with the same care he used to unpack them.}

\action{He senses her presence without turning. A pause — not acknowledgment, not denial.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{2\quad INT. COURT OF LAW -- HALL OF JURISTS -- DAY}

\action{The same Andalusian light, but disciplined. It enters through high lattice windows and breaks into repeating geometric patterns across the floor. The air is cooler than the courtyard, heavier with bodies and restraint.}

\action{Rows of JURISTS sit on low benches, robes arranged with ceremonial care. Scrolls, tablets, wax seals. The quiet here is not peace; it is containment.}

\action{At the center, on a raised cushion, sits AVERROES in his role as QADI. The physician’s tools are gone. In their place: a writing tablet, a stylus, a small stack of legal precedents. His hands are unchanged. Ink, medicine, law — the same fingers, different grammar.}

\action{Before him stand two men.}

\action{On the left, a BUILDER, mid-fifties, beard dusted permanently white with lime. His hands are thick, knuckles split and healed badly. On the right, a LABORER, younger, arm bound tightly, bruises blooming purple and yellow beneath the cloth.}

\action{Between them, an unspoken absence: a collapsed wall.}

\action{A JUNIOR JURIST rises, scroll in hand. He speaks formally, but his eyes flick toward Averroes, searching.}

\character{JUNIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{The matter before the court concerns injury sustained during the collapse of a retaining wall on the eastern works. The question is whether fault may be assigned.}

\action{A pause. The words “fault” and “assigned” hang uneasily.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Let the builder speak.}

\action{The BUILDER clears his throat. He does not look at the laborer. He looks upward, just above the jurists’ heads, as if addressing a ceiling that might absolve him.}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{The wall stood as walls stand. Stone upon stone. Mortar mixed as my father taught me. It fell because God willed it to fall.}

\action{A murmur ripples through the benches — not outrage, but relief. The phrase lands softly, like a blanket placed over responsibility.}

\character{BUILDER \contd}
\dialogue{Who am I to argue with the decree of the Most High?}

\action{The LABORER shifts. Pain flickers across his face. He speaks hesitantly, unused to rooms like this.}

\character{LABORER}
\dialogue{I was beneath it, my lord. I heard a crack before it fell. Like dry wood.}

\action{The BUILDER stiffens.}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{Stone cracks when God commands it.}

\action{Averroes lowers his eyes to his tablet. He marks something — a small notation, precise.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{How long had the wall stood?}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{Two seasons. Nearly three.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{And the mortar? Lime and sand?}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{As always.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{From which quarry came the stone?}

\action{The BUILDER hesitates. Just a fraction too long.}

\character{BUILDER}
\dialogue{The northern cut. Cheaper transport.}

\action{Averroes looks up now. His gaze is not accusatory. It is diagnostic.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{The northern stone fractures along internal seams when improperly cured. It demands thicker mortar and longer setting time.}

\action{The JUNIOR JURIST shifts uncomfortably. He interjects, carefully.}

\character{JUNIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{Qadi, if I may — does this not presume that the wall obeys necessity rather than divine immediacy?}

\action{The room tightens. Several heads turn. This is the dangerous sentence.}

\action{Averroes does not answer immediately. He looks instead at the LABORER’s bound arm.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{If I said the arm broke because God willed it, would you refuse the splint?}

\action{A ripple of unease.}

\character{JUNIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{No, my lord, but—}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Then you already believe that God’s will includes consistency.}

\beat

\dialogue{Law cannot function if injury is inscrutable. If every collapse is miracle, then no craft exists — only luck and prayer.}

\action{He turns to the BUILDER.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{You chose cheaper stone. You reduced mortar. You hurried the setting. These are causes.}

\beat

\dialogue{God did not conceal them from you.}

\action{The BUILDER’s jaw tightens. His defense is slipping, replaced by something like resentment.}

\action{A SENIOR JURIST leans toward his neighbor, whispers — not quite inaudible.}

\character{SENIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{Careful. He speaks as if the world binds even Heaven.}

\action{Averroes hears. He does not respond to the whisper. He writes.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Judgment: negligence. Compensation is owed. The injured man will receive wages for the season lost, paid by the builder’s guild.}

\action{The stylus presses firmly. Final.}

\action{The LABORER exhales, relief and disbelief mingled. The BUILDER bows stiffly, anger contained.}

\action{As the parties withdraw, the JUNIOR JURIST remains standing.}

\character{JUNIOR JURIST}
\dialogue{Qadi… forgive me. But some will say that this ruling limits God.}

\action{Averroes looks at him — really looks.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{No. It limits excuses.}

\action{The JUNIOR JURIST absorbs this, unsettled.}

\action{As the court moves to the next case, the camera drifts again — to the side wall.}

\action{There, recessed into stone, another CLEPSYDRA. Same rhythm. Drip. Drip.}

\action{But now the sound feels louder. More noticeable. Time as obligation.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{3\quad EXT. C\'ORDOBA -- STREET OUTSIDE THE COURT -- DAY}

\action{The court doors open onto noise. Not chaos, but density — vendors calling, carts creaking, voices overlapping in practiced disorder. The city breathes differently than the courtroom.}

\action{AVERROES steps out, flanked briefly by two COURT SCRIBES who peel away without ceremony. Authority dissolves the moment it exits architecture.}

\action{Across the street, a small CROWD has gathered around a PREACHER. He stands atop an overturned crate, robes rough, voice well-trained for outrage and reassurance in equal measure.}

\character{PREACHER}
\dialogue{—and who are we to say why a wall falls or a child sickens? Are we builders of fate? Are we physicians of decree?}

\action{A few listeners murmur assent.}

\character{PREACHER \contd}
\dialogue{No. We are servants. And servants do not ask how the Master moves His hand.}

\action{Averroes slows, not intentionally. The words catch him like grit in the shoe.}

\action{AʿISHA, still hooded, has emerged from the crowd behind him. She keeps distance, watching both the preacher and her father with equal intensity.}

\character{PREACHER \contd}
\dialogue{Beware those who tell you that stone remembers weight and that flesh obeys rules. They replace wonder with arrogance and call it knowledge.}

\action{The PREACHER’s eyes flick briefly toward Averroes. Recognition, sharpened by rumor.}

\character{PREACHER \contd}
\dialogue{There are men in this city who would rather measure God than fear Him.}

\action{A murmur swells. Not a shout. Something worse — agreement without commitment.}

\action{Averroes stops. He turns. For a moment, it seems he might speak.}

\action{AʿISHA’s breath catches.}

\action{He does not speak. He looks instead at a nearby CHILD, no more than eight, watching the preacher with open concentration, as if trying to memorize the cadence rather than the meaning.}

\action{Averroes turns away.}

\action{AʿISHA falls into step beside him as they walk.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{You could have answered him.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I answered him this morning. In a room that required listening.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{He speaks where listening is optional.}

\action{They pass a PUBLIC FOUNTAIN. Water pours steadily from a lion’s mouth into a basin. Women fill jars. Children splash.}

\action{Averroes watches the flow for half a beat longer than necessary.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{When argument leaves law and enters appetite, it becomes spectacle.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{Spectacle is faster.}

\action{Averroes says nothing.}

\action{As they move on, the camera lingers on the fountain. The flow is steady. Ordinary. Reliable.}

\action{For now.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{4\quad INT. AVERROES' STUDY -- LATE AFTERNOON}

\action{A high-ceilinged room lined with books — philosophy, law, medicine — arranged not by prestige but by use. Margins crowded with notes in several hands.}

\action{Sunlight slants low through a lattice window, illuminating dust and the spine of a large manuscript: \textit{TAH\=AFUT AL-FAL\=ASIFA}.}

\action{Averroes sits at a low desk. Before him: blank parchment. He does not yet write.}

\action{AʿISHA removes her hood. Her hair is bound tightly, scholar’s discipline more than disguise.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{They are repeating it in the market. That you bind God with stone and sinew.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Then they misunderstand both God and stone.}

\action{He finally dips his pen. Writes the title deliberately:}

\action{\textit{TAH\=AFUT AL-TAH\=AFUT}.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{You are answering a book written before I was born.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I am answering its consequences.}

\action{He pauses, rubs his fingers. Ink stains, old scars.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{Al-Ghaz\=ali was sincere. That is what makes him dangerous.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{And you?}

\action{Averroes considers the question more seriously than it may deserve.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I am precise. That may be worse.}

\action{A knock at the door. A SERVANT enters, hesitant.}

\character{SERVANT}
\dialogue{Master, there is talk — in Fez. At al-Qarawiyy\={\i}n.}

\action{AʿISHA’s eyes sharpen at the name.}

\character{SERVANT \contd}
\dialogue{They say the jurists there still teach law beside astronomy. That a woman founded the place, long ago.}

\action{Averroes looks up, surprised despite himself.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{F\=atima al-Fihri. Yes.}

\action{A faint smile — brief, private.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{When institutions remember their origins, they resist decay a little longer.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{Do you think they would hear you there?}

\action{Averroes does not answer immediately.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Fez listens. Bologna debates. C\'ordoba…}

\action{He gestures vaguely, encompassing the city beyond the walls.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{C\'ordoba believes it already knows.}

\action{Outside, distant shouting rises — not violent, but charged.}

\action{Averroes returns his attention to the page. Begins to write in earnest.}

\action{The scratch of the pen becomes rhythmic, almost percussive.}

\action{The camera drifts again — to the corner of the room.}

\action{The CLEPSYDRA. Drip. Drip.}

\action{Time passes. Something is being set in motion.}

\transition{CUT TO:}
\slug{3A\quad EXT. C\'ORDOBA -- MARKET DISTRICT -- LATE AFTERNOON}

\action{The market is dense and loud. Canvas awnings sag under the weight of heat. Flies hover. Merchants shout prices with theatrical despair.}

\action{SALADIN, early 30s, dark-skinned, broad-shouldered, moves through the crowd carrying a basket for his MASTER, who follows several paces behind, distracted by bargaining. Saladin wears a slave’s collar — plain leather, practical, unornamented.}

\action{Saladin’s eyes are alert, not fearful. He watches everything.}

\action{Near a bread stall, a STREET URCHIN — twelve, barefoot, sharp-eyed — waits. He watches the BAKER argue with a CUSTOMER.}

\action{In one smooth motion, the boy lifts a flat loaf and two figs. He disappears into the crowd like a fish slipping water.}

\action{Saladin sees it.}

\action{Their eyes meet for half a heartbeat. The boy freezes — then keeps moving.}

\action{Saladin does nothing.}

\action{Not mercy. Not defiance. Calculation.}

\action{The Baker never notices. The transaction finishes. Life resumes.}

\action{Saladin exhales quietly.}

\character{MASTER}
\dialogue{Did you see that boy lingering?}

\action{Saladin lowers his eyes.}

\character{SALADIN}
\dialogue{No, master.}

\action{The MASTER grunts, uninterested. They move on.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{3B\quad INT. MASTER'S COURTYARD -- NIGHT}

\action{A different silence. Harder. Torches flicker against stone.}

\action{Saladin kneels. His MASTER stands above him, flanked by two HOUSE GUARDS. One holds a short, heavy sword — practical, not ceremonial.}

\action{On the ground between them lies the stolen loaf. Half-eaten. Discovered too late to matter.}

\character{MASTER}
\dialogue{The baker says you saw.}

\action{Saladin does not deny it.}

\character{SALADIN}
\dialogue{I saw hunger.}

\action{The MASTER laughs once — sharply.}

\character{MASTER}
\dialogue{You saw theft. And you chose not to see it.}

\action{He turns to the GUARD.}

\character{MASTER \contd}
\dialogue{If the world works without consequence, then discipline dissolves.}

\action{Saladin looks up. Not pleading. Understanding.}

\action{The sword flashes.}

\action{We do not see the cut. We hear it. A wet, final sound.}

\action{Saladin collapses sideways, clutching his arm. Blood pools fast, dark, undeniable.}

\action{The MASTER steps back, satisfied but not cruel — merely restored.}

\character{MASTER \contd}
\dialogue{Let this remind you: witnessing is action.}

\action{Saladin’s breath comes ragged. His eyes stay open, fixed on the torchlight wavering on stone.}

\action{For the first time, causality is undeniable.}

\transition{CUT TO:}
\slug{4\quad EXT. C\'ORDOBA -- PUBLIC FOUNTAIN AT THE WESTERN SQUARE -- DAY}

\action{The square is fuller than usual. Not festive — attentive. People stand in loose rings, pretending to be occupied while watching the same place.}

\action{At the center, the PUBLIC FOUNTAIN. The same lion’s mouth. The same basin.}

\action{SALADIN stands at the edge of the crowd, his right sleeve pinned and empty. His posture has changed. He now stands slightly sideways, as if the city has pushed him out of alignment.}

\action{AʿISHA is present as well, hood up again, eyes alert.}

\action{A COURT OFFICIAL confers quietly with a pair of CITY WORKERS near the fountain. The exchange is subtle, efficient. No one looks religious.}

\action{A hush spreads — not commanded, but anticipated.}

\action{The call to prayer begins from a nearby minaret. The sound rolls across the square in long, familiar waves.}

\action{As the first phrase reaches its apex, the water in the fountain hesitates.}

\action{Then — impossibly — it begins to flow \emph{upward}.}

\action{Not violently. Calmly. As if reconsidering gravity.}

\action{Gasps. Someone laughs nervously. Someone drops a jug.}

\character{ONLOOKER}
\dialogue{Praise be —}

\action{Saladin stares. His breathing quickens.}

\action{AʿISHA does not react outwardly. She watches the pipes at the fountain’s base. The workers. The timing.}

\action{The water climbs for several seconds — long enough to be undeniable — then resumes its ordinary fall.}

\action{The square erupts.}

\character{PREACHER}
\dialogue{You see? You see how small the laws of men are?}

\action{Saladin drops to his knees. Not in joy. In terror.}

\action{Averroes is not present. His absence is conspicuous.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{5\quad INT. AVERROES' STUDY -- NIGHT}

\action{The room is darker now. Lamps lit. Scrolls open. The manuscript grows.}

\action{Averroes writes, pauses, crosses out a line, rewrites. He is not agitated — but the pen moves more slowly.}

\action{AʿISHA enters, breathless.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{Father. At the fountain.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Describe it.}

\action{She does. Precisely. Timing. Sound. Crowd behavior.}

\action{Averroes listens without interruption. When she finishes, he closes his eyes briefly.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Did the water return to its level?}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{Yes.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Did anything else change?}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{Only the people.}

\action{Averroes exhales. Not relief. Calculation.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Then the pipes are intact.}

\action{AʿISHA studies him.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{You sound certain.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{I sound unfinished.}

\action{He rises, takes a small oil lamp, moves to the wall where architectural diagrams hang. He traces a finger along a conduit sketch — Roman-era engineering.}

\character{AVERROES \contd}
\dialogue{Pressure reversed briefly. Enough to astonish. Not enough to flood.}

\action{AʿISHA absorbs this.}

\character{AʿISHA}
\dialogue{They chose prayer time.}

\character{AVERROES}
\dialogue{Of course they did.}

\action{A knock. ZAYNAB enters — physician, composed, grave.}

\character{ZAYNAB}
\dialogue{Two patients refused treatment today. Said the fountain cured their doubt.}

\action{She notices the diagrams.}

\character{ZAYNAB \contd}
\dialogue{So. It begins.}

\action{Averroes returns to his desk. He writes a heading.}

\action{\textit{On the Necessity of Causes}.}

\action{The clepsydra sounds louder now. Drip. Drip.}

\transition{CUT TO:}

\slug{6\quad EXT. SIDE STREET -- DAWN}

\action{Saladin sits against a wall, wrapped in a filthy cloak. Begging bowl empty.}

\action{A child — the same STREET URCHIN — passes. He slows. Recognizes Saladin.}

\action{The boy places half a loaf into the bowl. Hesitates. Then places the figs.}

\action{Saladin looks up. Their eyes meet again.}

\character{SALADIN}
\dialogue{Did the water rise?}

\character{STREET URCHIN}
\dialogue{Yes. Everyone saw.}

\character{SALADIN}
\dialogue{Then God is very close.}

\action{The boy nods, uncertain, and runs.}
