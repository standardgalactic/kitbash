\begin{abstract}
We derive the Relativistic Scalar–Vector Plenum (RSVP) Lagrangian from seven transparent physical axioms. These axioms fix, up to higher-order corrections, the unique local, rotationally invariant, stable, two-derivative effective field theory describing a dynamical medium of scalar density $\Phi$, vector flow $\mathbf{v}$, and configurational entropy $S$ in which gravity is entropic descent and “space falls outward”.

We then demonstrate that Jacobson’s 1995 thermodynamic derivation of the Einstein equation \cite{jacobson1995} arises naturally within RSVP as the coarse-grained thermodynamics of local causal horizons formed by the entropic flow itself. The appendices supply the complete Euler–Lagrange equations, Hamiltonian and Dirac-bracket formulation in the stiff limit \cite{dirac1964,henneaux1992}, and a precise mapping to Verlinde’s entropic gravity \cite{verlinde2011}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The RSVP theory posits that spacetime and gravity are emergent from the irreversible relaxation dynamics of a physical medium — a plenum — characterised by density inhomogeneities ($\Phi$), bulk flow ($\mathbf{v}$), and configurational entropy ($S$). This perspective aligns with emergent-spacetime and induced-gravity programs originating with Sakharov \cite{sakharov1967}, analogue-gravity models \cite{barcelo2005,unruh1981,visser1998}, and condensed-matter-inspired emergent effective metrics \cite{volovik2003}. It also extends the thermodynamic interpretation of gravitational dynamics initiated by Bekenstein \cite{bekenstein1973}, Hawking \cite{hawking1975}, Padmanabhan \cite{padmanabhan2005,padmanabhan2010}, and contemporarily developed by Jacobson \cite{jacobson1995}, Verlinde \cite{verlinde2011}, and others \cite{susskind2005}.

The central claim of this paper is that, once a minimal and physically motivated set of axioms is accepted, the effective Lagrangian governing this medium at long wavelengths is \emph{uniquely determined}. This follows the logic of effective field theory for continuous media \cite{nicolis2013,endlich2013,andersson2007} and cosmological EFT \cite{cheung2008}.

\section{Fields}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5cm]
\item $\Phi(\mathbf{x},t)$ \quad scalar plenum potential (“mass/meaning density”)
\item $\mathbf{v}(\mathbf{x},t)$ \quad lamphrodyne flow velocity of the medium
\item $S(\mathbf{x},t)$ \quad configurational entropy density field
\end{itemize}

All fields live on a preferred 3+1 foliation, consistent with medium-based EFTs \cite{volovik2003} and hydrodynamic gravity analogues \cite{barcelo2005}.

\section{The Seven Axioms}

The seven axioms follow standard effective-field-theory reasoning \cite{weinberg1995} and nonequilibrium statistical mechanics \cite{onsager1931,prigogine1967}:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Locality and action principle}.  
  Consistent with EFT and hydrodynamic limits \cite{weinberg1995,nicolis2013}.

\item \textbf{Spatial rotational invariance}.  
  As in analog gravity and emergent-metric media \cite{barcelo2005,andersson2007}.

\item \textbf{Quadratic kinetic terms} (no ghosts).  

\item \textbf{At most two spatial derivatives}.  
Standard in EFT \cite{weinberg1995,cheung2008}.

\item \textbf{Energy bounded below}.  
  Ensures stability, as in relativistic fluid EFT \cite{andersson2007}.

\item \textbf{Lamphrodyne divergence constraint}  
\[
\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v} \approx \alpha_\Phi \Phi + \alpha_S S,
\]
  analogous to constraint structures in continuum EFTs \cite{nicolis2013}.

\item \textbf{Gravity = entropic descent}.  
  Generalizing the Clausius–Raychaudhuri logic \cite{jacobson1995,raychaudhuri1955} and entropic-force programs \cite{verlinde2011,padmanabhan2005}.
\end{enumerate}

\section{The Unique RSVP Lagrangian}

Combining the axioms, the unique low-energy Lagrangian is:

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{RSVP}} &= 
\frac{1}{2}\dot{\Phi}^2 - \frac{c_\Phi^2}{2}|\nabla\Phi|^2 - U_\Phi(\Phi) \\[6pt]
&\quad + \frac{1}{2}|\dot{\mathbf{v}}|^2 - \frac{c_v^2}{4}F_{ij}F^{ij} - \frac{\kappa_v}{2}\bigl(\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v} - \alpha_\Phi\Phi - \alpha_S S\bigr)^2 \\[6pt]
&\quad + \frac{1}{2}\dot{S}^2 - \frac{c_S^2}{2}|\nabla S|^2 - U_S(S) \\[6pt]
&\quad + g_1\,\Phi\,\mathbf{v}\!\cdot\!\nabla S + g_2\,S\,\mathbf{v}\!\cdot\!\nabla\Phi + g_3\,\Phi S,
\end{aligned}
}
\]

with $F_{ij}=\partial_i v_j - \partial_j v_i$. This structure parallels the EFT of relativistic media \cite{nicolis2013,endlich2013,andersson2007}.

\section{Jacobson’s 1995 Derivation from RSVP}

Jacobson’s argument \cite{jacobson1995} uses the Clausius relation $\delta Q = T\delta S$ on local Rindler horizons. In RSVP, energy and entropy currents are
\[
\mathbf{J}_E \propto \Phi\mathbf{v}, \qquad 
\mathbf{J}_S \propto S\mathbf{v}.
\]
Flow acceleration gives an Unruh temperature $T=\kappa/(2\pi)$ \cite{unruh1976}. The flow congruence obeys a Raychaudhuri-type equation \cite{raychaudhuri1955}, which in the medium arises from the divergence constraint and the dynamics of streamlines \cite{barcelo2005,andersson2007}. In the stiff limit $\kappa_v\to\infty$, coarse-graining reproduces
\[
R_{\mu\nu} - \frac{1}{2}Rg_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G T_{\mu\nu},
\]
as in horizon-thermodynamic derivations \cite{padmanabhan2005}.

\section{Euler–Lagrange Equations}

\begin{align}
\boxed{\ddot{\Phi} - c_\Phi^2\Delta\Phi + U_\Phi'(\Phi)}
&= \kappa_v\alpha_\Phi(\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v}-\alpha_\Phi\Phi-\alpha_S S) + g_1\mathbf{v}\!\cdot\!\nabla S + g_2 S\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v} + g_3 S, \\[8pt]
\boxed{\ddot{\mathbf{v}} + c_v^2\nabla\!\times\!(\nabla\!\times\!\mathbf{v})}
&= \kappa_v(\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v}-\alpha_\Phi\Phi-\alpha_S S)\nabla(\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v}) + g_1\Phi\nabla S + g_2 S\nabla\Phi, \\[8pt]
\boxed{\ddot{S} - c_S^2\Delta S + U_S'(S)}
&= \kappa_v\alpha_S(\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v}-\alpha_\Phi\Phi-\alpha_S S) + g_2\mathbf{v}\!\cdot\!\nabla\Phi + g_1\nabla\!\cdot\!(\Phi\mathbf{v}) + g_3\Phi.
\end{align}

\section{Hamiltonian and Dirac Brackets (Stiff Limit)}

Canonical momenta are trivial. In the stiff limit $\kappa_v\to\infty$, the constraint $\chi = \nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf{v} - \alpha_\Phi\Phi - \alpha_S S = 0$ is second-class. The Dirac bracket \cite{dirac1964,henneaux1992} projects onto transverse flow modes:
\[
\{v_i(\mathbf{x}),\pi_v^j(\mathbf{y})\}^* = P_i^{\;j}(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}),
\qquad
P_i^{\;j} = \delta_i^{\;j} - \partial_i\partial^j\frac{1}{4\pi|\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}|}.
\]

\section{Relation to Verlinde’s Entropic Gravity}

Verlinde’s entropic force \cite{verlinde2011} $F = T\nabla S$ arises in the quasi-static RSVP limit as $m\mathbf{a} \simeq g_1\Phi\nabla S + g_2 S\nabla\Phi$, consistent with Padmanabhan’s thermodynamic gravity \cite{padmanabhan2005}.

\section{Conclusion}

The RSVP Lagrangian is uniquely fixed by seven axioms. Its horizon thermodynamics reproduce Jacobson’s derivation of the Einstein equation, and its static limit recovers Verlinde’s entropic gravity. The theory provides a unified microphysical basis for emergent spacetime, grounded in the effective field theory of continuous media.
