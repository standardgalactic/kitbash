\begin{abstract}
This paper develops a formal and conceptual analogy between the biochemical theory of collectively autocatalytic sets and the epistemic dynamics of memeplexes—coherent complexes of mutually reinforcing ideas, narratives, and practices. Building on Kuhn’s and Hanson’s notion that all observation is theory-laden, we argue that memes (as units of cultural transmission) form self-sustaining networks of justification and inference. Within such networks, facts and theories co-generate each other, producing a self-referential closure analogous to metabolic autocatalysis.

We extend the prior formulation with explicit food-set mappings, spectral and Lyapunov analyses, global stability conditions, a hypergraph generalization, and an empirical interpretive model using the RSVP (Relativistic Scalar–Vector Plenum) field formalism. A three-node numerical example and the case of \textit{The Technological Republic} illustrate the framework’s explanatory and predictive power.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\begin{narrative}
The term “meme,” introduced by Dawkins as a cultural analogue to the gene, has been interpreted as a unit of replication, mutation, and selection. Yet cultural ideas seldom replicate independently; they cohere into structures of mutual reinforcement. The notion of an isolated meme is analogous to an enzyme without a metabolic network—it cannot persist. 
\end{narrative}

We thus model memeplexes as collectively autocatalytic systems, adapting Kauffman’s biochemical formalism. Each meme \(m_i\) participates in inferential relations \(r_i\) catalyzed by other memes \(C(r_i)\). The external food set \(F\) provides raw perceptual or experiential input, converted to memetic form via \(u_i = \sum_{f\in F} \gamma_{if}\). 

A kinetic model describes their temporal evolution:
\[
\dot{x}_i = \beta_i \sigma\!\left(\sum_j A_{ij} x_j^\alpha\right) + u_i - \lambda_i x_i,
\]
where \(\sigma\) is a saturating nonlinearity, \(\beta_i\) catalytic rate, and \(\lambda_i\) decay.  Autocatalysis corresponds to feedback among nodes such that some subset \(S\subseteq M\) sustains \(x_i>0\) without external drive.

\section{From Autocatalytic Sets to Memeplexes}
\begin{narrative}
Kauffman’s autocatalytic framework describes molecular self-organization: molecules that catalyze reactions forming other molecules in the same set constitute a collectively autocatalytic network. Translating this logic to cognition, each meme becomes both product and catalyst of interpretation.
\end{narrative}

An autocatalytic network satisfies closure and self-sufficiency.  Transposed:
\[
\text{molecules} \leftrightarrow \text{memes},\quad
\text{reactions} \leftrightarrow \text{inferential relations},\quad
\text{catalysts} \leftrightarrow \text{theories or narratives}.
\]
A memeplex achieves closure when every inference is catalyzed by other members and each meme derives justification from within the system.  

The adjacency \(A_{ij}\) quantifies catalytic strength: \(A_{ij}>0\) if meme \(m_j\) reinforces \(m_i\). Each reaction \(r_i\) has catalyst set \(C(r_i)\subseteq M\), defining \(A_{ij}=1\) when \(m_j\in C(r_i)\).

\section{Theory-Ladenness and Epistemic Closure}
\begin{narrative}
Observation is not neutral but filtered through theory. Hanson and Kuhn described how seeing itself is structured by concepts. In cultural systems, this recursive dependence yields epistemic closure: facts require theories to be perceived, and theories require facts to be justified.
\end{narrative}

Formally,  
\[
\mathrm{Fact}_i = f(\mathrm{Theory}_j,\mathrm{Observation}_k),\qquad
\mathrm{Theory}_j = g(\mathrm{Fact}_i,\mathrm{Norm}_\ell),
\]
and fixed points of \((f,g)\) define self-sustaining networks of knowledge.

\section{Spectral and Stability Analysis}
For the linearized system \(\dot{x}=Gx\), with \(G = c\,\mathrm{diag}(\beta)A - \mathrm{diag}(\lambda)\), a positive equilibrium exists if the spectral radius satisfies
\[
\rho(A_{SS}) > \min_i (\lambda_i / \beta_i)/c.
\]
The Lyapunov candidate \(V = \frac{1}{2}\sum_i(x_i-x_i^*)^2\) yields \(\dot{V}<0\) when \(\lambda_i > c\beta_i \rho(A)\).  

\begin{narrative}
This expresses a balance between memetic decay and catalytic excitation: belief networks remain stable only when forgetting exceeds self-reinforcement below a critical connectivity. Above that threshold, self-sustaining ideology emerges.
\end{narrative}

\section{RSVP Embedding and Functional Microfoundation}
\begin{narrative}
To connect discrete memetic kinetics with continuous cognitive dynamics, we embed the system in RSVP fields: interpretive potential \(\Phi(\mathbf r,t)\), attention flow \(\mathbf v(\mathbf r,t)\), and entropy \(S(\mathbf r,t)\).
\end{narrative}

Regional averaging links discrete and continuous domains:
\[
x_i(t) \approx \frac{1}{|R_i|}\!\int_{R_i}\!\rho(\mathbf r,t)\,d\mathbf r,
\]
\[
\sum_jA_{ij}x_j^\alpha \approx 
\frac{1}{|R_i|}\!\int_{R_i}\!\frac{\mathcal R(\Phi,S,\rho)}{\rho}\,d\mathbf r.
\]

Representative PDEs:
\begin{align*}
\partial_t \Phi &= D_\Phi \Delta \Phi - \kappa_\Phi \Phi + \eta_\Phi\rho - U'(\Phi),\\
\partial_t S &= D_S \Delta S - \kappa_S S + \xi_S,\\
\partial_t \rho + \nabla\!\cdot(\rho\mathbf v) &= D_\rho \Delta\rho + \rho h(\Phi)e^{-S} - \lambda\rho^p.
\end{align*}
These follow from coarse-graining the discrete dynamics. The reaction term \(\rho h(\Phi)e^{-S}\) approximates the mean catalytic rate; the decay term \(\lambda\rho^p\) models saturation.  

Entropy production balance includes boundary flux:
\[
\partial_t\mathcal D = \Pi - \Phi_{\text{diss}} - \nabla\!\cdot J_\mathcal D.
\]

\section{Numerical Example: The Three-Node Cycle}
Consider 
\[
A=\begin{pmatrix}0&1&0\\0&0&1\\1&0&0\end{pmatrix},
\]
with identical \(\beta,\lambda\).  
Eigenvalues of \(G=\beta cA-\lambda I\) are \(\beta c e^{2\pi i k/3}-\lambda\).  
Bifurcation occurs at \(\beta_c = \lambda / c\).  

\begin{narrative}
Below the threshold, beliefs decay exponentially; above it, oscillatory cycles of reinforcement appear, representing periodic resurgences of interconnected memes—a mathematical analog of cultural resonance.
\end{narrative}

\section{Cultural and Cognitive Implications}
\begin{narrative}
Autocatalytic closure yields internal coherence and resistance to external perturbation.  Memes act as mutual justifications; novelty is either assimilated or rejected.  This explains the tenacity of ideologies and scientific paradigms.
\end{narrative}

When distinct memeplexes overlap in semantic space, nonlinear couplings can merge them into new attractors—a process analogous to syncretism or paradigm fusion.  
Entropy influx, representing novelty or critique, increases \(S\); when it exceeds a threshold, closure fails and the system undergoes reorganization.

\section{The Technological Republic as a Case Study}
\begin{narrative}
Karp and Zamiska’s \textit{The Technological Republic} portrays modern Western civilization as an epistemically closed system of technological faith: efficacy validates itself, and critique becomes proof of vitality.  In memetic terms, it is a two-node autocatalytic loop between belief in progress and technological mastery.
\end{narrative}

Let \(m_1=\) “technological progress,” \(m_2=\) “instrumental success,” with feedback rates \(a,b\).  
\[
\dot{x}_1 = \beta a x_2 - \lambda x_1,\qquad
\dot{x}_2 = \beta b x_1 - \lambda x_2.
\]
Stability requires \(\sqrt{ab} > \lambda / (\beta c)\).  
\begin{narrative}
Empirically, the loop persists because each term reinforces the other’s legitimacy: progress proves technique, and technique proves progress.  Collapse corresponds to an entropy influx \(\delta S>0\)—informational noise, moral dissonance, or systemic critique—that lowers effective \(a,b\) until the spectral criterion fails.
\end{narrative}
