4. Evaluation contexts and strategy

We present small-step semantics  with evaluation contexts. Two standard choices:

Call-by-value (CBV): evaluate arguments to values before popping:


\mathcal{E} ::= [\ ] \mid \mathrm{Pop}(\mathcal{E},E) \mid \mathrm{Pop}(V,\mathcal{E}) \mid \mathrm{Merge}(\mathcal{E},E) \mid \mathrm{Merge}(E,\mathcal{E}) \mid \mathrm{Nest}(\mathcal{E},E) \mid \mathrm{Nest}(E,\mathcal{E})

Call-by-name (CBN): do not evaluate the argument prior to pop:


\mathcal{E} ::= [\ ] \mid \mathrm{Pop}(\mathcal{E},E) \mid \mathrm{Merge}(\mathcal{E},E) \mid \mathrm{Merge}(E,\mathcal{E}) \mid \mathrm{Nest}(\mathcal{E},E) \mid \mathrm{Nest}(E,\mathcal{E})

Below, rules are strategy-agnostic; contexts determine where rules may fire.

5. Core reduction rules (small-step)

We write  for the least relation closed under the context rule:

\frac{E \to E'}{\mathcal{E}[E] \to \mathcal{E}[E']}

(Pop-β) (application of a sphere)

\mathrm{Pop}(\mathrm{Sphere}(x.\,E),\, N) \;\to\; E[N/x]

(Nest-unfold) (explicit scope is delayed application)

\mathrm{Nest}(E_1, E_2) \;\to\; \mathrm{Pop}(E_1, E_2)

(Sphere-transparency) (optional, if you want spheres to be inert unless popped, omit this)

\mathrm{Sphere}(x.\,E) \;\to\; \mathrm{Sphere}(x.\,E) \quad\text{(no step)}

(Merge-congruence) reduction propagates into either side by context rule. No primitive step besides structural congruence:

\frac{E_1 \to E_1'}{\mathrm{Merge}(E_1,E_2) \to \mathrm{Merge}(E_1',E_2)}\quad
\frac{E_2 \to E_2'}{\mathrm{Merge}(E_1,E_2) \to \mathrm{Merge}(E_1,E_2')}

(Merge-comm/assoc) are equivalences via , not reduction steps; evaluators often implement them as canonical re-associations or as multisets.

(Congruence under Sphere)

6. Determinism / concurrency notes

With CBV/CBN and no parallel interleaving semantics, reduction is deterministic up to structural congruence (as in λ-calculus), provided you fix an order in  via a canonical representative (e.g., multiset with deterministic enumeration).

If you give  true parallel dynamics (both sides may step independently), you get confluent but non-deterministic interleavings. Confluence can be shown by standard diamond arguments if interactions are only via  and scoping obeys capture-avoidance.

10. Normalization and confluence (remarks)

The untyped core has the same (non-)normalization properties as untyped λ-calculus (divergence possible).

Simply-typed SPC (without recursive types and with Merge typed as finite products) enjoys strong normalization and confluence, by standard reducibility (Tait/Girard) adapted to the additional congruence for Merge. Proofs proceed by encoding SPC into a simply-typed λ-calculus with pairs (interpret Sphere as λ, Pop as application, Merge as pairing, Nest as syntactic sugar), and inheriting meta-theory.
