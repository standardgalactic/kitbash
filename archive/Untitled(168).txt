


A. Questions About the Economic Model Itself

What exactly is the mathematical object the model is trying to solve for?
Is it a value function, a policy function, an equilibrium mapping, or something else?

Does the solution object live in a scalar space, a vector space, or a higher-dimensional functional space?

What features of the model generate the nonlinearities that traditional methods struggle with?
For example: expectations, constraints, heterogeneous agents, distributional states, etc.

What are the characteristics of the operator ?
Is it:

a contraction mapping?

a fixed-point problem?

a system of nonlinear equations?

a variational or Euler-equation condition?

B. Questions About the Geometry of the Problem

How is the state space structured?

How many state variables are typical?

Are they continuous, discrete, or mixed?

Is the domain naturally a Euclidean space, or is the geometry more complex?

Where does “curvature” or complexity originate in the model’s solution surface?
(Even if the lecture does not use the word curvature, this refers to any source of shape/complexity.)

Does Fernández-Villaverde give examples of solutions that are “hard to approximate” using standard bases (e.g., polynomials)?
If so, which features make them hard?

What intuition does the presenter give about the shape of the unknown function
(e.g., highly nonlinear, kinked, high-frequency components, discontinuities)?

C. Questions About Neural Networks as Representations

How does the presenter describe the action of a single neural network layer?
Does he emphasize linear combinations, nonlinear activations, feature bending, or something else?

Does he explain why deep networks (multiple layers) can represent functions more efficiently than shallow ones?

Does he provide examples where a deep network discovers a representation or change of variables
that makes the problem easier to solve?

What is the nature of the activation functions being used?
Do they introduce smoothness, non-smoothness, sparsity, or threshold effects?

Does he discuss the internal geometry or learned features of the neural network?
For example:

learned latent variables

compressed representations

“unfolding” of high-dimensional surfaces

D. Questions About High-Dimensional Efficiency

What evidence does he present that deep learning alleviates the curse of dimensionality?

Does he show scaling results (e.g., number of variables vs computation time)?

Does he explain why neural networks avoid the need for full tensor-product grids?

Does he mention whether the approximated function tends to have low intrinsic dimensionality,
and whether the network is exploiting this?

E. Questions About the Training Process

What loss function is used when training the neural network to approximate the solution?
Is it directly the equilibrium condition , or some residual?

How is data generated for training?
Are states drawn from:

random sampling,

simulated trajectories,

predefined grids,

or adaptive schemes?

Does he discuss stability, convergence, or error bounds for the training process?

Does he compare optimization methods (SGD, Adam, etc.) in terms of performance on economic models?

F. Questions About Practicality and Use-Cases

What types of economic models benefit most from the deep learning approach?
(e.g., heterogeneous-agent models, high-dimensional shocks, non-smooth value functions)

Does he mention limitations or cases where deep learning underperforms traditional methods?

Does he discuss the trade-off between speed and accuracy?

Does he describe how researchers should structure their code or workflow
when implementing deep-learning-based solvers?

G. Questions About Conceptual Interpretation

How does he frame the role of deep learning rhetorically?
As:

a numerical workhorse,

an automatic change-of-variables machine,

a more flexible basis function,

or a fundamentally new way of representing economic structure?

Does he explicitly compare neural networks to traditional basis expansions (Chebyshev, B-splines, polynomials)?

Does he frame deep networks as discovering structure in the model that the researcher does not specify?

Does he refer to the method as “geometric” or describe the solution manifold’s shape explicitly?

Does he acknowledge theoretical or philosophical implications
(e.g., what it means for an algorithm to “find” an economic equilibrium)?
