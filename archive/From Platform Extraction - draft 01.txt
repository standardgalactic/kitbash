\begin{abstract}
Building on the RSVP--Yarncrawler framework and Tim Wu's diagnosis of platform extraction, this paper develops a comprehensive synthesis positioning PlenumHub not merely as a computational tool, but as a thermodynamic intervention in the political economy of information. Platform extraction is reformulated as a geometric and entropic instability: a transition from catalytic manifolds to curvature-concentrating wells. In response, PlenumHub embeds structural balancing directly into the substrate through typed semantic spheres, entropy budgets, crystal conservation laws, and sheaf-theoretic closure. The resulting architecture replaces external regulatory correction with internal invariants, demonstrating that collaboration can be entropy-bounded rather than entropy-maximizing. Knowledge is treated as a physical field configuration governed by conservation principles, and prosperity emerges not from unregulated growth, but from constraint-first design.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Crisis of Geometric Centralization}

Recent political-economic analysis has identified a structural shift in the nature of platforms. Early platforms functioned catalytically: they lowered transaction costs, reduced friction between agents, and enabled decentralized participation. Contemporary dominant platforms, by contrast, operate extractively. They do not merely facilitate exchange; they deepen curvature around themselves, siphoning surplus through positional control of infrastructure.

In the language of the Relativistic Scalar--Vector Plenum (RSVP), this shift corresponds to a geometric transition. A catalytic platform approximates a low-resistance manifold in which agent trajectories follow intrinsic geodesics. Vector flows remain tangent to the manifold, and scalar density remains distributed. An extractive platform, by contrast, becomes a scalar well. Curvature increases locally, and flows drift toward the center. What appears as efficiency at the micro-scale manifests as concentration at the macro-scale.

As platforms become infrastructural, they accumulate predictive resolution. By aggregating behavioral data and increasing model capacity, they pre-structure the decision space of agents. The manifold on which agency operates is no longer neutral. Instead, it becomes pre-curved. This phenomenon may be described as manifold collapse: the dense, externally computed manifold of the platform begins to dominate the sparse, internal manifold of the agent. The agent experiences choice, yet their trajectory increasingly coincides with the precomputed geodesics of the host.

The instability is not merely distributive; it is geometric. When curvature concentrates beyond a stability threshold, tangent diversity diminishes. The system becomes brittle. Extraction is therefore not only unfair; it is a form of ultraviolet instability in the social field.

\section{PlenumHub as Structural Antitrust}

Traditional antitrust mechanisms attempt to regulate platforms from outside the system through litigation, breakups, or structural remedies. Such interventions function as exogenous diffusion terms, introduced after curvature concentration has already occurred. PlenumHub proposes a different strategy. It embeds structural balancing directly into the compute substrate.

Rather than relying on regulatory oversight, PlenumHub formalizes semantic physics through type theory and entropy accounting. The atomic unit of the system is the semantic sphere
\[
\sigma = (I, T, M, E, S),
\]
where $I$ is an immutable, content-hash bound identity; $T$ is a required set of modalities; $M$ assigns payloads to each modality; $E$ is a measurable semantic entropy; and $S$ is a finite directed acyclic graph of derivations ensuring verifiable provenance.

This representation replaces untyped messages and line-based diffs with structured, multimodal objects. Identity is intrinsic rather than platform-assigned. Provenance is formal rather than reputational. Entropy is measurable rather than implicit.

Extraction in contemporary platforms is fueled by entropy inflation. Engagement-driven drift accumulates noise, ambiguity, and modality fragmentation without structural accounting. PlenumHub introduces entropy budgets $\epsilon_r$ attached to every rule application. For an execution trace $\tau = r_1; \dots; r_n$, cumulative entropy potential is defined as
\[
\Phi(\tau) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_{r_i}.
\]
Entropy soundness ensures that
\[
E(\sigma_n) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \Phi(\tau).
\]

This bound functions as a structural barrier against negative diffusion. A platform cannot silently degrade meaning in order to increase engagement because any such degradation would violate the declared entropy budgets and fail type validation. The substrate enforces conservation constraints that external regulation attempts to approximate.

In this sense, PlenumHub performs antitrust at the level of algebra. Structural balancing is not imposed by policy but guaranteed by invariants.

\section{The Crystal Economy: Rewarding Coherence over Capture}

To replace the rent-seeking dynamics of extractive platforms, PlenumHub introduces a dual currency system that acts as a conservation law of the semantic field. Texture Crystals measure spatial semantic coherence, while Time Crystals measure provenance-weighted persistence.

Texture valuation is defined as
\[
TC(\sigma) = H(\sigma) - \sum_{i \neq j} MI(M(k_i), M(k_j)),
\]
where $H(\sigma)$ is total semantic entropy and $MI$ denotes mutual information between modalities. Mutual information operates as a complexity tax on redundancy. A sphere cannot increase its valuation by duplicating correlated content across modalities. To earn texture credit, structure must increase without inflating correlation.

Time valuation is defined as
\[
TiC(\sigma) = \sum_{r_i \in S} e^{-\lambda (t_{\text{now}} - t_i)} q(r_i),
\]
where $\lambda > 0$ is a decay constant and $q(r_i)$ is a quality metric derived from rule validation. Influence decays exponentially in the absence of semantic descendants. This prevents lock-in dynamics where early or central nodes accumulate permanent dominance solely by virtue of arrival time.

Together, these currencies enforce a conservation principle. No sequence of rule applications can generate net positive crystal mass without the injection of genuinely novel semantic information. Rent extraction, in the form of value capture without structural contribution, becomes algebraically impossible.
