Version 2 Instructions: The RSVP Monograph

Overall Scope and Structure

Format: Multi-volume monograph series (3 volumes total)

Target Length:

Volume I: ~400 pages

Volume II: ~350 pages

Volume III: ~250 pages

Total: ~1000 pages

Style: Rigorous mathematical physics monograph with pedagogical elements—think Kobayashi & Nomizu's Foundations of Differential Geometry meets Costello's Renormalization and Effective Field Theory, but with substantial original research content. Formal but not impenetrable. Each major claim should be traceable to either a proof, a detailed heuristic, or an explicit open problem statement.

Audience Stratification

Primary audience: Mathematical physicists and geometers with strong background in:

Differential geometry and gauge theory

Category theory and homological algebra

General relativity and quantum field theory

Secondary audience: Theoretical physicists willing to learn derived geometry

Tertiary audience: Philosophers of physics, cognitive scientists (for Volume III)

Pedagogical strategy: Each volume should have:

A gentler introductory chapter assuming less background

Technical chapters with full rigor

Appendices reviewing prerequisites

Worked examples throughout

Rigor Specifications

Volume I (Foundations): Maximum Rigor

Theorems: Full statements with complete proofs or detailed proof sketches (minimum 80% of major results proven)

Conjectures: Clearly labeled, with evidence, plausibility arguments, and connections to known results

DAG/AKSZ formalism: Full categorical rigor—definitions, constructions, diagrams, coherence conditions

Shifted symplectic structures: Prove all claimed properties, construct explicitly

PDE analysis: Complete proofs for well-posedness, energy estimates, regularity

Volume II (Physical Applications): Mixed Rigor

Cosmology: Both theoretical and empirical—derive predictions rigorously, then compare with data quantitatively

Emergent gravity: Rigorous derivation of field equations, then heuristic/phenomenological exploration

Observational tests: Real data, plots, statistical analysis

Theorems vs heuristics: ~60% rigorous theorems, ~40% physical arguments and phenomenology

Volume III (Extensions & Interpretations): Moderate Rigor

SpherePop: Rigorous categorical foundations, then computational/pedagogical applications

Cymatics/cognition: More heuristic, but mathematically coherent

Creative projects: Formal commentary connecting narrative to mathematics

Theorems vs heuristics: ~40% rigorous, ~60% conceptual/interpretive
