% ============================================================
\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

Recent debates about the limits of large language models (LLMs) have converged on a recurring diagnosis: despite their fluency, such systems lack a stable understanding of the world. Yann LeCun has argued forcefully that autoregressive language models cannot achieve human-level intelligence because they lack \emph{world models}—internal systems capable of representing causal structure, predicting the consequences of actions, and supporting planning under constraints. In parallel, Elliot Murphy’s ROSE architecture advances a mechanistic theory of linguistic competence grounded in invariant structural operations rather than probabilistic sequence continuation.

This essay argues that these critiques are not merely compatible but formally equivalent when expressed at the correct level of abstraction. Both LeCun’s world models and Murphy’s so-called Platonic constraints can be understood as enforcing the same functional requirement: the existence of an \emph{authoritative internal history} that stabilizes cognition against statistical drift.

We develop this equivalence by introducing a deterministic event-log formalization—here called a \emph{Spherepop-style operating semantics}—in which intelligence is grounded not in token prediction but in invariant-preserving replay. On this view, autoregressive models fail not because they use language, but because they operate entirely in a \emph{view-only loop} disconnected from any authoritative causal order.

% ============================================================
\section{LeCun’s World Model Requirement}
% ============================================================

LeCun’s critique of LLMs is architectural rather than empirical. He does not deny that such systems can approximate many human behaviors; instead, he argues that they lack the internal machinery required for reliable, sample-efficient, and grounded intelligence.

At the core of this critique is the concept of a \emph{world model}. Importantly, LeCun does not define a world model as a pixel-level simulator. Rather, it is a predictive system operating in an abstract latent space that:
\begin{itemize}
\item filters out unpredictable detail,
\item predicts state transitions conditioned on actions,
\item and supports planning as optimization over predicted trajectories.
\end{itemize}

Language-only systems, on this account, are disconnected from reality because they never commit to a stable internal notion of state. They predict the next token rather than the next state of the world.

\begin{definition}[World Model]
A \emph{world model} is an internal predictive system
\[
W : (\sigma_t, a_t) \mapsto \widehat{\sigma}_{t+1}
\]
that operates in an abstract representation space, preserves task-relevant invariants, and supports counterfactual evaluation of action sequences.
\end{definition}

This definition already implies a separation between surface-level externalizations (text, images) and internal authoritative state.

% ============================================================
\section{Autoregression as a View-Only Loop}
% ============================================================

Autoregressive language models operate by iteratively extending a surface representation:
\[
x_{t+1} \sim P(x_{t+1} \mid x_{\le t}).
\]
While this procedure can produce locally coherent sequences, it lacks any mechanism for enforcing long-range causal consistency. Each step is conditioned on prior outputs rather than on an independently maintained internal state.

This structural limitation can be expressed as a failure to distinguish between \emph{views} and \emph{commitments}.

\begin{proposition}[Autoregressive Drift]
In a purely autoregressive system with no invariant-governed internal state, projection error accumulates monotonically with generation depth. As a result, long-horizon coherence cannot be guaranteed even when local predictions are accurate.
\end{proposition}

What is commonly described as “hallucination” is therefore not an anomaly but an inevitable consequence of view-only iteration without authoritative replay.

% ============================================================
\section{Deterministic Event Logs and Authoritative History}
% ============================================================

To make LeCun’s critique precise, we introduce the notion of an \emph{authoritative history}: a deterministic event log that defines the system’s internal state independently of any particular external description.

\begin{definition}[Authoritative Event Log]
An authoritative event log is a finite ordered sequence
\[
\mathcal{L} = (e_1, e_2, \dots, e_T)
\]
together with a deterministic replay operator
\[
\mathrm{Replay}(\mathcal{L}) = \sigma_T,
\]
where $\sigma_T$ is the system’s internal state obtained by applying events in order under fixed transition rules.
\end{definition}

External inputs—text, images, sensory signals—are treated as \emph{views} that may or may not be committed to the log. Crucially, commitment is constrained by invariants.

\begin{theorem}[Replay-Stabilized Consistency]
If all state updates occur via invariant-preserving replay from an authoritative log, then incoherent or contradictory views cannot corrupt the system’s internal state. Errors remain quarantined at the view level.
\end{theorem}

This property is precisely what autoregressive systems lack.

% ============================================================
\section{ROSE and Structural Causality in Language}
% ============================================================

Murphy’s ROSE architecture provides a domain-specific instantiation of this general principle for language. Rather than modeling syntax as statistical sequence regularity, ROSE decomposes linguistic competence into Representation, Operation, Structure, and Encoding.

Merge operations act as constrained state transitions; hierarchical structure functions as an invariant; and encoding ensures deterministic propagation across neural systems. In effect, ROSE specifies a \emph{language-domain world model} whose authority is enforced by structural admissibility rather than probability.

\begin{remark}
From this perspective, Murphy’s “Platonic Forms” and LeCun’s “world models” are not metaphysically distinct entities but different manifestations of the same architectural requirement: invariant-governed internal state construction.
\end{remark}

% ============================================================
\section{Planning, Objectives, and Safety}
% ============================================================

LeCun emphasizes that intelligence requires planning: the ability to evaluate action sequences with respect to objectives under constraints. This presupposes:
\begin{itemize}
\item a predictive model of state transitions,
\item a cost or objective function,
\item and hard constraints that cannot be violated.
\end{itemize}

In a log-based system, safety constraints can be implemented as commit rules: actions whose predicted consequences violate invariants are simply inadmissible. This reframes safety not as filtering outputs but as enforcing causal legality.

% ============================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================================

The limitations of autoregressive language models do not stem from their use of language, symbols, or learning at scale. They stem from the absence of an authoritative internal history governed by invariant-preserving replay. Without such a structure, prediction remains unconstrained imitation.

LeCun’s world models and Murphy’s Platonic constraints converge on the same conclusion: intelligence requires a system that can commit to what happened, reason about what could happen, and reject what cannot happen. Deterministic event logs provide the minimal formal substrate for this capability.

AGI, on this view, is not a matter of better statistics. It is a matter of building systems that can tell the difference between a view and a fact.
