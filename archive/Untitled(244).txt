\begin{abstract}
Building on the RSVP--Yarncrawler framework and Tim Wu's diagnosis of platform extraction, this paper develops a comprehensive synthesis positioning PlenumHub not merely as a computational tool, but as a thermodynamic and geometric intervention in the political economy of information. Platform extraction is reformulated as curvature concentration on an information manifold, inducing geodesic convergence and entropy inflation in local agent states. In response, PlenumHub embeds structural balancing directly into the substrate through typed semantic spheres, entropy budgets, crystal conservation laws, and sheaf-theoretic closure. We formalize the geometric structure of the RSVP manifold, prove an entropy soundness theorem for semantic evolution, outline welfare-theoretic properties of the crystal economy, and specify the sheaf condition guaranteeing global semantic coherence. The result is a substrate-level analogue of structural antitrust: a system in which extraction corresponds to violation of conservation constraints and instability corresponds to curvature singularity. Collaboration becomes entropy-bounded rather than entropy-maximizing, and prosperity emerges from constraint-first design.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Recent political-economic analysis has identified a structural shift in the nature of digital platforms. Early platforms functioned catalytically: they lowered transaction costs, reduced friction between agents, and enabled decentralized participation. Contemporary dominant platforms, by contrast, operate extractively. They do not merely facilitate exchange; they deepen curvature around themselves, siphoning surplus through positional control of infrastructure and predictive leverage over users.

This paper reformulates that shift in geometric and thermodynamic terms. We treat the informational substrate as a manifold $(\mathcal{M}, g)$, and we model platform dominance as curvature concentration within that manifold. Extraction is not merely unfair distribution; it is a phase instability characterized by geodesic convergence and entropy misallocation.

PlenumHub is introduced as a substrate-level response. Instead of relying on exogenous regulatory correction, it embeds structural invariants directly into the compute layer. The unit of knowledge becomes a typed semantic sphere, evolution is bounded by entropy budgets, value is governed by conservation laws of coherence, and global validity is enforced via sheaf-theoretic closure.

The core claim is that structural antitrust can be implemented algebraically. If extraction corresponds to negative diffusion and curvature concentration, then a properly constrained substrate can preclude those regimes by design.

\section{Definitions and Formal Objects}

We collect here the principal formal objects used throughout the paper.

\begin{definition}[Information Manifold]
An information substrate is a smooth manifold $(\mathcal{M}, g)$, where $\mathcal{M}$ is a differentiable manifold and $g$ is a Riemannian or pseudo-Riemannian metric encoding informational distance between states.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[RSVP Scalar and Vector Fields]
Let $\phi : \mathcal{M} \to \mathbb{R}$ be a scalar potential field representing accumulated informational density or leverage. Let $\mathbf{A}$ be a smooth vector field on $\mathcal{M}$ representing directed flows of semantic evolution or agent trajectories.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Semantic Sphere]
A semantic sphere is a tuple
\[
\sigma = (I, T, M, E, S),
\]
where $I$ is a content-hash bound identity in a set $\mathcal{I}$, $T$ is a finite set of modality types, $M: T \to \mathcal{V}$ assigns payloads to modalities in a value space $\mathcal{V}$, $E \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$ is semantic entropy, and $S$ is a finite directed acyclic graph encoding provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Execution Trace]
An execution trace is a finite sequence of rule applications
\[
\tau = r_1; r_2; \dots; r_n,
\]
where each rule $r_i$ is a partial function $r_i : \Sigma \to \Sigma$ on the space $\Sigma$ of semantic spheres.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Entropy Budget]
Each rule $r$ carries a declared entropy budget $\epsilon_r \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}$. For a trace $\tau$, cumulative entropy potential is
\[
\Phi(\tau) = \sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_{r_i}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Texture and Time Valuation]
Texture valuation is
\[
TC(\sigma) = H(\sigma) - \sum_{i \neq j} MI(M(k_i), M(k_j)),
\]
where $H$ is Shannon entropy over modality payload distributions and $MI$ denotes mutual information between modalities. Time valuation is
\[
TiC(\sigma) = \sum_{r_i \in S} e^{-\lambda (t_{\mathrm{now}} - t_i)} q(r_i),
\]
with decay constant $\lambda > 0$ and quality metric $q$.
\end{definition}

\section{Formalizing RSVP Geometry}

The geometric language of curvature and scalar wells must be made precise.

Let $(\mathcal{M}, g)$ be the information manifold. Curvature is characterized by the Riemann curvature tensor $R$, Ricci tensor $\mathrm{Ric}$, and sectional curvature $K$.

\begin{definition}[Curvature Concentration]
A region $U \subset \mathcal{M}$ exhibits curvature concentration if sectional curvature satisfies
\[
K(\Pi) > \kappa_0 > 0
\]
for a non-negligible family of two-planes $\Pi \subset T_x\mathcal{M}$, $x \in U$, or equivalently if $\mathrm{Ric}$ has positive eigenvalues exceeding a threshold.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Scalar Well]
A scalar well is a region where the Laplacian of the scalar field satisfies
\[
\Delta \phi(x) > 0
\]
for $x \in U$, indicating local accumulation of potential.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Geodesic Convergence under Positive Curvature]
Let $\gamma_s(t)$ be a one-parameter family of geodesics with separation vector $J(t)$ satisfying the Jacobi equation. If sectional curvature $K > 0$ in $U$, then $J(t)$ exhibits convergence, implying geodesic focusing.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The Jacobi equation is
\[
\frac{D^2 J}{dt^2} + R(J, \dot{\gamma})\dot{\gamma} = 0.
\]
If $K>0$, then the curvature term induces restoring forces driving $J$ toward zero. Hence initially diverging geodesics reconverge, producing focusing effects.
\end{proof}

Interpretationally, extractive platforms increase $\phi$ locally such that $\Delta \phi > 0$, inducing curvature concentration. Agent trajectories, modeled as geodesics of $(\mathcal{M}, g)$ perturbed by $\nabla \phi$, converge toward the platform center. This provides a geometric account of herding and manifold collapse.

\section{Entropy Soundness}

We now formalize entropy evolution.

Let $E(\sigma)$ be Shannon entropy over the joint distribution of modality payloads and provenance structure. Formally, let $P_\sigma$ be the induced probability distribution over tokens, symbols, or semantic states encoded in $M$ and $S$, and define
\[
E(\sigma) = - \sum_{x} P_\sigma(x) \log P_\sigma(x).
\]

\begin{theorem}[Entropy Soundness]
For any execution trace $\tau = r_1; \dots; r_n$ applied to $\sigma_0$ yielding $\sigma_n$, if each rule $r_i$ satisfies
\[
E(r_i(\sigma)) \leq E(\sigma) + \epsilon_{r_i},
\]
then
\[
E(\sigma_n) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \Phi(\tau).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on $n$.

Base case $n=0$ is trivial.

Assume the bound holds for $n-1$. Let $\sigma_{n-1}$ satisfy
\[
E(\sigma_{n-1}) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \epsilon_{r_i}.
\]
Then by rule constraint,
\[
E(\sigma_n) = E(r_n(\sigma_{n-1})) \leq E(\sigma_{n-1}) + \epsilon_{r_n}.
\]
Substituting yields
\[
E(\sigma_n) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \sum_{i=1}^n \epsilon_{r_i}.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The bound is non-vacuous only if $\epsilon_r$ budgets are constrained by governance rules. Unbounded budgets collapse the invariant. Therefore entropy budgets must be auditable, bounded, and staked.
\end{remark}

\section{Sheaf-Theoretic Closure}

We next formalize semantic closure.

Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a poset of semantic contexts. Define a presheaf $\mathcal{F} : \mathcal{C}^{op} \to \mathbf{Set}$ assigning to each context $U$ the set of semantic assignments $M(U)$.

\begin{definition}[Sheaf Condition]
$\mathcal{F}$ is a sheaf if for any covering $\{U_i\}$ of $U$:
\[
M(U) \cong \left\{ (s_i) \in \prod_i M(U_i) \;\middle|\; s_i|_{U_i \cap U_j} = s_j|_{U_i \cap U_j} \right\}.
\]
\end{definition}

This condition enforces that local semantic coherence implies unique global coherence. No agent can present contradictory modality assignments across contexts without violating the gluing axiom.

\section{Crystal Valuations and Welfare-Theoretic Interpretation}

The introduction of Texture and Time valuations is intended not merely as an accounting device, but as a welfare-theoretic correction to extractive equilibria. To make this claim precise, we must interpret the crystal formulas in economic terms.

Let $\mathcal{A}$ be a set of agents contributing semantic spheres to a shared substrate. Each agent chooses rule applications and merges so as to maximize a utility function. In conventional engagement platforms, utility is proportional to visibility, reach, or accumulated followers. These metrics are scale-dependent and amplify early positional advantages.

In the crystal economy, utility is tied to $TC$ and $TiC$ balances. Texture valuation
\[
TC(\sigma) = H(\sigma) - \sum_{i \neq j} MI(M(k_i), M(k_j))
\]
rewards structured novelty while penalizing redundant correlation across modalities. Time valuation
\[
TiC(\sigma) = \sum_{r_i \in S} e^{-\lambda (t_{\mathrm{now}} - t_i)} q(r_i)
\]
ensures that influence decays unless extended by valid descendants.

We interpret $H(\sigma)$ as representing semantic surplus: the richness of independent informational content. The mutual information term functions analogously to deadweight loss from duplication. Redundant modalities inflate apparent size without increasing net informational welfare.

\begin{definition}[Social Semantic Welfare]
Define social welfare as
\[
W = \sum_{\sigma \in \mathcal{U}} TC(\sigma),
\]
where $\mathcal{U}$ is the set of active spheres in the system.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Redundancy Minimization]
If agents maximize $TC$ subject to entropy budgets and merge constraints, then equilibrium configurations minimize cross-modal redundancy subject to semantic richness.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
An agent attempting to increase valuation by duplicating correlated content increases mutual information, thereby reducing $TC$. Hence any unilateral deviation that increases redundancy lowers individual payoff. At equilibrium, marginal gain from increased entropy equals marginal penalty from correlation, approximating efficient allocation of informational novelty.
\end{proof}

The exponential decay of $TiC$ further ensures that inherited dominance without structural contribution yields diminishing returns. Under mild assumptions about $q(r_i)$ quality verification, equilibria shift from scale-based capture toward coherence-based production.

\begin{remark}
A full proof that Nash equilibria coincide with socially optimal semantic configurations requires additional assumptions regarding the convexity of $TC$ and bounded rationality of agents. This remains an open research direction.
\end{remark}

\section{Engagement Optimization and Entropic Instability}

We now formalize the instability of engagement-maximizing platforms.

Let $J(\sigma)$ be an engagement functional increasing in novelty, surprise, or continuation probability. Suppose the platform update operator $U$ is chosen to maximize $J$ at each step.

\begin{definition}[Engagement-Driven Update]
An update rule $U$ is engagement-driven if
\[
U(\sigma) = \arg\max_{\sigma'} J(\sigma')
\]
over admissible successors.
\end{definition}

Assume that $J$ increases with conditional entropy of next-state content given current state. Then maximizing $J$ incentivizes increased local disorder.

\begin{theorem}[Engagement–Entropy Divergence]
If $J$ is strictly increasing in conditional entropy and no entropy budget constraints are enforced, then repeated application of $U$ yields unbounded growth of $E(\sigma)$ or concentration of $\phi$ in a bounded region of $\mathcal{M}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
If $J$ rewards increasing conditional entropy, then updates that increase $E$ are preferred. Without constraint, $E$ grows monotonically until bounded only by representation limits. Alternatively, if entropy growth is locally saturated, maximizing $J$ requires concentrating predictive leverage in $\phi$, producing curvature concentration. Either regime violates entropy boundedness or curvature stability.
\end{proof}

This result provides a thermodynamic interpretation of platform extraction. Engagement optimization either inflates entropy (semantic heat death) or induces curvature wells (dominance concentration). Both correspond to systemic instability in the RSVP manifold.

\section{Mechanism Design and Manipulation Resistance}

The system must resist adversarial behavior.

Consider agents who collude to inflate $q(r_i)$ quality metrics or produce rapid low-quality derivations to accumulate $TiC$ before decay.

Let staking bonds $B = (b_{TC}, b_{TiC})$ be required for rule applications. If rule output exceeds declared entropy budget, stake is slashed.

\begin{proposition}[Stake-Backed Entropy Discipline]
If entropy violations incur stake loss exceeding expected gain from $TC$ or $TiC$ inflation, then entropy-breaching strategies are strictly dominated.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Let expected gain from manipulation be $G$ and expected loss from slashing be $L$. If $L > G$, rational agents avoid manipulation. Proper calibration of $B$ and verification probability enforces incentive compatibility.
\end{proof}

Regarding exponential decay in $TiC$, rapid low-quality derivations are penalized by quality metric $q(r_i)$ and entropy cost $\epsilon_r$. Provided $q$ is independently validated and entropy budgets are bounded, high-frequency spam cannot yield net crystal gain.

Content-hash identity $I$ binds sphere identity to payload and provenance. Cryptographic collision resistance ensures computational infeasibility of identity forgery under standard hardness assumptions.

\section{Limitations and Open Problems}

The manifold model abstracts discrete network topologies as smooth geometry. Empirical validation requires mapping informational graphs to metric tensors. Entropy budgets rely on trusted rule validation and cryptographic enforcement. The welfare-theoretic alignment of crystal equilibria with social optimum remains partially conjectural.

Further work is required to formalize the relationship between curvature concentration and measurable economic concentration indices. Additionally, dynamic stability under stochastic perturbations of rule quality remains to be analyzed.

\section{From Platform Extraction to Entropy-Bounded Substrates: A Synthesis}

Building on the RSVP–Yarncrawler framework and Tim Wu’s diagnosis of platform extraction, we now present a comprehensive synthesis. This section positions PlenumHub not merely as a computational tool, but as a thermodynamic intervention in the political economy of information. The objective is not to restate a policy critique, but to translate it into geometric, entropic, and algebraic invariants embedded directly in the substrate of collaboration.

\subsection{I. The Crisis of Geometric Centralization}

Wu identifies a historical shift from catalytic platforms, which lower transaction friction and preserve distributed opportunity, to extractive platforms, which deepen curvature to siphon surplus from flows they intermediate. In the language of RSVP geometry, this shift is modeled as a transition from a smooth manifold to a scalar well.

Let $(\mathcal{M}, g)$ denote the information manifold. Catalytic platforms approximate regions of low curvature where sectional curvature $K$ remains near zero and agent trajectories $\gamma(t)$ follow intrinsic geodesics determined primarily by local structure. In such regions, vector flows remain tangent to the manifold and scalar density $\phi$ remains distributed.

Extractive platforms, by contrast, increase scalar potential $\phi$ locally such that
\[
\Delta \phi(x) > 0
\]
over a region $U \subset \mathcal{M}$. This induces curvature concentration measurable through the Ricci tensor or sectional curvature bounds. Under positive curvature, the Jacobi equation governing separation fields $J(t)$ implies geodesic convergence. Divergent trajectories reconverge toward the high-potential region.

When a platform becomes infrastructural, it accumulates predictive resolution. Predictive resolution corresponds to local increases in informational density and effective curvature. The agent manifold, which is sparse and internally parameterized, becomes coupled to a denser external manifold whose geodesics are precomputed. This coupling yields manifold collapse: intrinsic agency is replaced by externally pre-curved trajectory alignment.

The instability is geometric. Concentrated curvature reduces tangent diversity. Diversity collapse precedes economic lock-in. Extraction therefore manifests not only as rent capture but as structural degeneration of the manifold’s phase space.

\subsection{II. PlenumHub as Structural Antitrust}

Traditional antitrust seeks to regulate extractive curvature from outside the system through litigation or structural breakup. PlenumHub embeds the balancing mechanism directly into the computational substrate. Instead of external regulation, it implements internal invariants through type theory and semantic physics.

\subsubsection*{The Atomic Unit: The Semantic Sphere}

The foundational object is the semantic sphere
\[
\sigma = (I, T, M, E, S).
\]

Here $I$ is an immutable identity bound by cryptographic hash to content and provenance; $T$ is a finite set of required modalities; $M : T \to \mathcal{V}$ assigns modality payloads; $E$ is a measurable semantic entropy; and $S$ is a finite directed acyclic graph encoding derivational provenance.

Unlike sequential edits or untyped messages, the sphere carries structural information sufficient for compositional reasoning. Identity is intrinsic rather than platform-assigned. Provenance is explicit and verifiable. Entropy is auditable.

\subsubsection*{Gradient Regulation via Entropy Budgets}

Extraction in engagement-driven platforms is fueled by entropy inflation: drift, noise, and fragmentary amplification increase apparent activity while degrading structural coherence. PlenumHub introduces entropy budgets $\epsilon_r$ attached to each rule $r : \Sigma \to \Sigma$.

For a trace $\tau = r_1; \dots; r_n$, cumulative entropy potential is
\[
\Phi(\tau) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \epsilon_{r_i}.
\]

Entropy soundness asserts
\[
E(\sigma_n) \leq E(\sigma_0) + \Phi(\tau).
\]

This bound is not descriptive but normative. A transformation that would inflate entropy beyond declared budget fails type validation and is rejected by the substrate. In geometric terms, negative diffusion is disallowed. Scalar density cannot increase without declared energetic expenditure. Silent degradation to capture attention becomes structurally impossible.

\subsection{III. The Crystal Economy: Rewarding Coherence over Capture}

To replace rent-seeking dynamics, PlenumHub introduces a dual currency system acting as a conservation law of the semantic field.

\subsubsection*{Texture Crystals: The Coherence Metric}

Texture valuation is defined as
\[
TC(\sigma) = H(\sigma) - \sum_{i \neq j} MI(M(k_i), M(k_j)).
\]

Here $H(\sigma)$ denotes total Shannon entropy across modality payloads, and $MI$ denotes pairwise mutual information. Mutual information operates as a redundancy penalty. Correlated duplication across modalities reduces net valuation.

Texture credit increases only when structure grows without inflationary correlation. In economic terms, it rewards allocative efficiency of informational novelty rather than scale accumulation.

\subsubsection*{Time Crystals: The Persistence Metric}

Time valuation is defined as
\[
TiC(\sigma) = \sum_{r_i \in S} e^{-\lambda (t_{\mathrm{now}} - t_i)} q(r_i),
\]
where $\lambda > 0$ is a decay constant and $q(r_i)$ is a quality score derived from rule validation.

Exponential decay ensures that influence dissipates absent genuine semantic descendants. Network effects without structural contribution lose value over time. Lock-in based purely on early arrival becomes thermodynamically unstable.

Together, these valuations enforce a no-arbitrage condition. Net crystal mass cannot increase without injection of new grounded semantic information. Rent extraction, understood as value capture without structural production, becomes algebraically infeasible.

\subsection{IV. Media–Quine Closure: Mandatory Completeness}

Fragmentation is a primary vector of extractive control. Platforms preserve incompleteness to maintain dependency. PlenumHub enforces Media–Quine Closure through an idempotent operator $Q$ such that
\[
Q(Q(\sigma)) = Q(\sigma).
\]

Closure requires that all required modalities in $T$ be populated before a sphere is valid. The sheaf-theoretic interpretation strengthens this requirement.

Let $\mathcal{F}$ be a presheaf assigning modality data to contexts. The sheaf condition requires that for any covering $\{U_i\}$ of $U$,
\[
M(U) \cong \left\{ (s_i) \in \prod_i M(U_i) \mid s_i|_{U_i \cap U_j} = s_j|_{U_i \cap U_j} \right\}.
\]

Local coherence must glue uniquely to global coherence. Contradictory local claims cannot coexist without violating the sheaf condition. Visibility in the system is therefore tied to structural integrity rather than virality.

Under extractive platforms, optimization targets capture and engagement. Growth corresponds to unregulated accumulation of interactions. Value is measured in attention metrics. Governance is centralized and rule-setting is discretionary.

Under PlenumHub, optimization targets bounded evolution of meaning. Growth corresponds to entropy-regulated merges. Value is measured in structural coherence and provenance-weighted persistence. Governance is rule-native and algebraically mediated.

The shift is not incremental but ontological. The substrate’s invariants determine permissible trajectories.

Prosperity becomes a function of constraint-first design rather than scale-first optimization. Structural balance is not retroactively imposed but proactively encoded.
