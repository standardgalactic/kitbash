# Coherence Before Engagement  
## An Architecture for Persistent Meaning in Digital Systems

## Core Argument

Current social media platforms are fundamentally flawed because their feed-based,
engagement-driven architecture inherently produces semantic drift,
misinformation, polarization, and erosion of trust. These failures are not the
result of poor moderation or imperfect algorithms, but structural consequences
of platform design.

## Proposed Solution: A Coherence-First Semantic Architecture

This paper proposes a new digital communication substrate based on the following
principles:

### 1. Semantic Objects Replace Posts

- Semantic objects are the fundamental unit of interaction.
- Each object has:
  - An immutable identity
  - Required modalities (e.g., text, data, code)
  - Explicit, append-only provenance
- Objects must be well-formed (all required modalities satisfied) to be valid.

### 2. Entropy-Bounded Evolution

- Meaning evolves through typed semantic transformations (revision, extension,
  reconciliation).
- Each transformation is constrained by an explicit entropy budget.
- This prevents unbounded semantic drift and enforces long-term coherence.

### 3. Provenance-Anchored Identity

- Identity is derived from transformation history, not follower counts or likes.
- All changes are traceable, append-only, and non-repudiable.
- Accountability is structural rather than reputational.

### 4. Spatial Navigation, Not Feeds

- Users navigate semantic neighborhoods in a latent space.
- There are no infinite scrolling feeds.
- Relevance is determined by semantic proximity, not recency or engagement.

### 5. Governance by Invariants, Not Moderation

- Rules are enforced structurally via invariants:
  - Entropy bounds
  - Modality completeness
  - Provenance integrity
- Disagreements result in explicit forks rather than suppression or deletion.

### 6. AI as a Constrained Operator, Not a Curator

- AI performs only typed semantic operations (e.g., summarization, translation).
- All AI actions are subject to entropy budgets and validation.
- AI does not rank content or optimize for engagement.

## Key Benefits

- **Persistent, traceable meaning** — knowledge accumulates and remains accessible.
- **Trust through consistency** — derived from provenance and long-term behavior.
- **No viral amplification** — no feeds, likes, or shares to exploit.
- **Learning by doing** — interaction develops programming and systems literacy
  (e.g., via SpherePOP).
- **Scalable through locality** — system load grows with local density, not total
  user count.

## Challenges and Tradeoffs

- Higher cognitive load due to structured interaction.
- Slower adoption without viral growth mechanics.
- Incompatibility with ad-based revenue models.
- Difficult migration from existing platforms due to architectural mismatch.

## Conclusion

This work argues for a paradigm shift away from engagement-optimized systems
toward coherence-first architectures in which trust, meaning, and understanding
emerge as structural properties. The proposal is not an incremental reform but a
new foundation for digital discourse, collaboration, and long-term knowledge
preservation.
