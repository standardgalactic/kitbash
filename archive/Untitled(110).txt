\begin{abstract}
This paper reconceptualizes aphantasia and anendophasia within the Relativistic Scalar--Vector Plenum (RSVP) framework. Rather than deficits, they are interpreted as base configurations of cognitive field coherence, prior to the activation of mimetic proxy layers that produce visual and auditory imagination. Drawing on Arnie Cox's theory of musical mimesis and Embodied Cognition, we propose that imagination is a higher-order reading layer mediated by midbrain central pattern generators (CPGs), which modulate amplitwistor modes---oscillatory manifolds aligning cortical columns across the semantic plenum. In this view, aphantasia represents cognition in its unrendered, pre-mimetic form: a direct traversal of entropic geometry without perceptual proxy.
\end{abstract}

\section{The Imageless Mind as the Cognitive Base Case}

Aphantasia, far from being an anomaly, may represent the \emph{base case} of cognition.  
In the RSVP ontology, thought emerges from continuous field transformations of scalar potential \(\Phi\), vector flow \(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\), and entropy \(S\).  
Imagery and inner speech are not prerequisites for cognition but \emph{mimetic readings} layered upon these deeper fields.  
They constitute perceptual renderings of already coherent internal dynamics.

In this hierarchy, aphantasia is the unadorned plenum: cognition without projection, operating directly on semantic gradients.  
The so-called ``imageless mind'' is therefore not a void but a clear-field dynamic---a low-redundancy, high-coherence state in which meaning flows without mimetic buffering.

\section{Phantasia and Audiolization as Mimetic Reading Layers}

Following Arnie Cox's \emph{Music and Embodied Cognition}, we interpret imagination and inner speech as instances of \emph{mimetic proxy} activity:  
the brain simulates bodily actions and sensory experiences as a way of understanding, predicting, and empathizing.  
When one ``hears'' a melody internally or ``sees'' a visual scene, cortical and subcortical systems reenact the motor and sensory patterns corresponding to those events.  
This is a form of embodied reading: the nervous system reading itself through the same circuits used for perceiving others.

Within RSVP theory, such mimetic layers arise when \(\Phi\)--\(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\) coupling exceeds a certain coherence threshold, inducing resonant feedback across sensory manifolds.  
Phantasia (visual imagination) and audiolization (inner speech, music) are thus *rendered projections* of deeper semantic dynamics---epiphenomenal display layers over the plenum's entropic base.

\section{Midbrain CPG Modulation and Amplitwistor Modes}

The neural substrate for these mimetic layers can be modeled through midbrain Central Pattern Generators (CPGs), which generate rhythmic templates for motor and perceptual loops.  
These CPGs serve as modulators, selecting which amplitwistor modes---geometric harmonics on the RSVP manifold---are activated during cognition.

Formally, each amplitwistor mode \(A_k\) corresponds to an oscillatory manifold embedded within the coupled field dynamics:
\[
A_k: \quad \Phi(\mathbf{x},t), \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}(\mathbf{x},t) \; \longmapsto \; \mathbb{M}_k(\omega, \mathbf{p})
\]
where \(\mathbb{M}_k\) encodes the synchronized manifold of cortical oscillations attempting to achieve phase alignment.  
Imagination arises when cortical columns entrain across these manifolds via feedback from midbrain CPG rhythms, effectively producing a ``perceptual holograph'' in internal space.

Aphantasia, in this formulation, occurs when the system remains below the CPG activation threshold for mimetic entrainment.  
The amplitwistor modes remain latent; cognition proceeds directly on semantic differentials rather than sensory proxies.

\section{Mimetic Proxy Theory and Embodied Resonance}

Cox’s Mimetic Theory posits that listening and imagining rely on covert motor imitation---the listener simulates gestures or motions underlying sound.  
RSVP extends this principle: all perceptual imagination involves the projection of \emph{mimetic proxies} into the plenum, where \(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\) fields trace motor–affective vectors and \(\Phi\) modulates scalar coherence.

Embodied cognition thus represents a recursive coupling between the physical body and the entropic manifold of meaning.  
The ``image'' or ``voice'' one hears internally is the echo of embodied resonance projected through the amplitwistor geometry of the cortical plenum.

\section{Synchronization and the Geometry of Thought}

Cortical cognition depends on dynamic synchronization among oscillating networks.  
Each local column can be represented as a node in an evolving manifold attempting to align its phase with neighboring nodes.  
When synchronization succeeds across a sensory submanifold, a mimetic projection (imagery or inner speech) emerges.  
When synchronization remains abstract---confined to relational or propositional coherence---the result is non-imagistic thought.

The RSVP field equations
\[
\partial_t \Phi = - \nabla \cdot (\Phi\, \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}) + \xi(t), \qquad
\partial_t S = - \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}} \cdot \nabla S + \eta(\Phi, \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}),
\]
govern how such synchronization evolves as an entropic descent.  
Imagistic cognition corresponds to high-frequency amplitwistor coupling (rich internal oscillations), while aphantasic cognition stabilizes in the low-frequency semantic base.

\section{TARTAN: Recursive Tiling and the Semantics of Imagination}

Within the RSVP architecture, TARTAN (Trajectory-Aware Recursive Tiling with Annotated Noise) describes how cognition partitions the plenum into self-similar tiles of meaning.  
Each tile encodes a local trajectory of the scalar field~$\Phi$ and vector flow~$\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}$, annotated by entropy gradients~$S$.  
In mimetic cognition, these tiles become perceptual frames—recursive renderings of semantic motion onto visual or auditory manifolds.  
In aphantasic cognition, tiling remains purely structural: trajectories are computed and integrated without being projected into image or phoneme space.

Formally, let $\mathcal{T}_n$ denote the $n$-th recursive tiling of the cognitive manifold.  
Each tile evolves according to
\[
\mathcal{T}_{n+1} = \mathcal{R}\!\left[\mathcal{T}_n + \epsilon\,\nabla_{\mathbb{M}}\Phi + \eta\,\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\right],
\]
where $\mathcal{R}$ is a renormalization operator preserving semantic continuity and $\eta$ represents annotated noise—the stochastic trace of embodied context.  
The emergence of imagery corresponds to the hierarchical stabilization of these tiles across sensory coordinates.  
When $\eta$ remains high or coherence low, the hierarchy fails to converge and the system remains non-mimetic: cognition proceeds as abstract recursion, not pictorial simulation.

Thus, TARTAN formalizes imagination as recursive interpolation over the plenum.  
It is the algorithmic geometry of phantasia itself: a self-referential re-tiling of semantic gradients that may, but need not, collapse into perceptual icons.

\section{CLIO: The Cognitive Loop via In-Situ Optimization}

Complementing TARTAN’s structural recursion, the CLIO module models the evaluative and corrective dynamics of thought.  
CLIO (\emph{Cognitive Loop via In-Situ Optimization}) defines cognition as an iterative process of local free-energy minimization, where internal predictions are continuously compared against embodied feedback.  
It operates as a variational loop:
\[
\mathcal{C}_{t+1} = \arg\min_{\mathcal{C}}\;
\mathbb{E}\!\left[(\nabla_{\mathcal{C}}\Phi)^2 + 
\lambda\,(\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\cdot\nabla S)^2\right],
\]
adjusting the internal configuration~$\mathcal{C}$ to maintain entropic coherence.

In imagistic or verbal cognition, CLIO interacts with midbrain CPGs to stabilize amplitwistor modes through sensorimotor rehearsal:  
predictions are tested against embodied simulations, and mismatches generate new mimetic corrections.  
In aphantasia and anendophasia, CLIO’s optimization proceeds without those proxy loops—the loop remains semantic rather than sensory, aligning directly through the plenum’s scalar–vector manifold.

Together, CLIO and TARTAN describe two halves of the same process:  
TARTAN provides the recursive geometry of semantic partitioning, while CLIO supplies the adaptive feedback that prunes, weights, and synchronizes those partitions.  
Imagination thus becomes a special case of recursive entropic optimization: the mind learning to see and hear its own predictions.

\section{Cognition Without Rendering}

The imageless and wordless mind thus reveals a different kind of intelligence:  
semantic traversal without mimetic rehearsal.  
Its silence is structural, not deficient; its absence of imagery corresponds to direct entropic coherence.  
From the RSVP viewpoint, aphantasia and anendophasia are not lacking but purified---operating before the activation of the mimetic reader that translates field dynamics into perceptual symbols.

\section{Conclusion}

By interpreting phantasia and inner speech as mimetic reading layers, and aphantasia as the unrendered base case, we obtain a unified geometry of thought consistent with RSVP field theory and embodied cognition.  
The midbrain–CPG complex serves as a resonant switchboard, modulating amplitwistor modes that project semantic flow into perceptual form.  
The silent mind is not empty but unmediated: a direct entropic computation through the plenum’s manifold.

Future work should empirically test whether CPG-linked oscillatory harmonics correlate with subjective imagery intensity, and whether RSVP field simulations of amplitwistor synchronization can reproduce the cognitive continuum from aphantasia to vivid imagination.

\section*{Appendix: Amplitwistor Mode Equations}

\subsection*{A.1  Oscillatory Geometry of Cognitive Fields}

Within the RSVP framework, each cortical region is represented by a local field triplet
\[
(\Phi_i, \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}_i, S_i)
\]
evolving on the semantic manifold \(\mathbb{M}\).  
Synchronization across regions corresponds to phase alignment among these local fields, governed by their shared entropic descent.  
The collective oscillatory geometry can be described by a set of complex amplitwistors \(\mathcal{A}_k\) defined as holomorphic sections over \(\mathbb{M}\):

\[
\mathcal{A}_k = \int_{\Sigma_k} \Phi(\mathbf{x},t)\, 
e^{\,i\omega_k t}\, d^3x,
\]
where each \(\Sigma_k\) is a submanifold corresponding to a network of synchronizing cortical columns.

\subsection*{A.2  CPG Modulation and Mode Selection}

Midbrain Central Pattern Generators (CPGs) modulate the resonance conditions for each mode by setting an effective coupling tensor \(\mathsf{C}_{ij}(t)\) that gates the transfer of oscillatory energy between cortical nodes:
\[
\dot{\mathcal{A}}_k = 
-\,\sum_{i,j} \mathsf{C}_{ij}(t)\,
\nabla_{\!j}\Phi_i
+ i\,\omega_k\,\mathcal{A}_k
- \gamma_k\,\mathcal{A}_k.
\]
The damping term \(\gamma_k\) controls persistence of each mimetic projection.  
High \(\mathsf{C}_{ij}\) values correspond to strong CPG entrainment, producing stable imagistic or audiolized manifolds; low coupling yields the silent, non-mimetic base case.

\subsection*{A.3  Semantic Synchronization Metric}

The degree of synchronization among amplitwistor modes is measured by an RSVP coherence functional:
\[
\mathcal{Q} = 
\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k}
\frac{|\langle \mathcal{A}_k, \mathcal{A}_k^{*}\rangle|}
      {\|\mathcal{A}_k\|^2}
= 
\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k}
\frac{1}{\|\mathcal{A}_k\|^2}
\int_{\Sigma_k}\!\!
\Phi\, \boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}}\!\cdot\!\nabla S\, d^3x.
\]
A value of \(\mathcal{Q}\approx1\) indicates full mimetic entrainment (high imagery, strong inner speech), while \(\mathcal{Q}\approx0\) denotes the aphantasic regime—direct entropic coherence without sensory projection.

\subsection*{A.4  Interpretation}

These equations model the transition from the silent plenum to mimetic projection as a bifurcation in oscillatory synchronization.  
Aphantasia corresponds to the low-amplitude, low-coupling limit of \(\mathcal{A}_k\); phantasia and audiolization arise when CPG-driven coupling tensors excite higher-order harmonics.  
In phenomenological terms, imagination emerges as a resonance between the semantic scalar field and the body’s rhythmic infrastructure.  
The mind ``renders'' only when the plenum’s amplitwistors sing in phase.

\subsection*{A.5  TARTAN: Recursive Entropy Tensors and Renormalization}

Let $\Pi_n = \{\tau^{(n)}_a\}_a$ be the $n$-th TARTAN tiling (partition) of the cognitive manifold $\mathbb{M}$.
For each tile $\tau^{(n)}_a$, define the local field statistics
\[
\mu_\Phi^{(n,a)} = \frac{1}{|\tau^{(n)}_a|}\!\int_{\tau^{(n)}_a}\!\Phi,\qquad
\boldsymbol{\mu}_{\mathcal{v}}^{(n,a)} = \frac{1}{|\tau^{(n)}_a|}\!\int_{\tau^{(n)}_a}\!\boldsymbol{\mathcal{v}},\qquad
\mu_S^{(n,a)} = \frac{1}{|\tau^{(n)}_a|}\!\int_{\tau^{(n)}_a}\!S.
\]
The \emph{recursive entropy tensor} encodes anisotropic uncertainty and directional coupling within a tile:
\[
\mathsf{E}_{ij}^{(n,a)} \;=\;
\int_{\tau^{(n)}_a}\!
\Big(\nabla_i S\Big)\Big(\nabla_j S\Big)\;w_\Phi(\mathbf{x})\,d^3x
\quad\text{with}\quad
w_\Phi(\mathbf{x})=\frac{\Phi(\mathbf{x})}{\int_{\tau^{(n)}_a}\Phi}.
\]
Tile-level tensors are aggregated at scale $n$ by
\[
\mathsf{E}_{ij}^{(n)}=\frac{1}{|\Pi_n|}\sum_a \mathsf{E}_{ij}^{(n,a)}.
\]
TARTAN’s renormalization between scales is
\[
\mathsf{E}^{(n+1)} \;=\; \mathcal{R}\!\big[\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\big] \;+\; \Xi^{(n)} ,
\]
where $\mathcal{R}$ is a coarse-graining operator that preserves semantic invariants (e.g., trace and principal directions up to bounded distortion), and $\Xi^{(n)}$ is \emph{annotated noise} induced by embodied context and task constraints. 

Imagery corresponds to convergence of principal directions across scales:
\[
\max_k \left\|\mathbf{u}_k\!\big(\mathsf{E}^{(n+1)}\big)-\mathbf{u}_k\!\big(\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\big)\right\|\;\xrightarrow[n\to\infty]{}\;0,
\]
with $\{\mathbf{u}_k\}$ the eigenvectors of $\mathsf{E}^{(n)}$. Failure to converge (large $\|\Xi^{(n)}\|$ or weak structure) yields the non-mimetic regime.

\subsection*{A.6  Coupling TARTAN to Amplitwistor Modes}

Let $\{\mathcal{A}_k\}$ be amplitwistor modes (Sec. A.1–A.3) associated with synchronized cortical submanifolds $\Sigma_k$ and test kernels $\mathsf{W}_k(\mathbf{x})$.
Define the scale–mode coupling
\[
\Gamma_k^{(n)} \;=\; \int_{\Sigma_k}\!\! \mathrm{Tr}\!\Big[\mathsf{W}_k(\mathbf{x})\,\mathsf{E}^{(n)}(\mathbf{x})\Big]\; d^3x.
\]
The mode dynamics with TARTAN drive become
\[
\dot{\mathcal{A}}_k \;=\;
-\,\sum_{i,j}\mathsf{C}_{ij}(t)\,\nabla_j\Phi_i
\;+\; i\,\omega_k \mathcal{A}_k
\;-\; \gamma_k \mathcal{A}_k
\;+\; \sum_{n=0}^{N}\beta_n\,\Gamma_k^{(n)}\,\mathcal{A}_k,
\]
where $\mathsf{C}_{ij}(t)$ is the CPG-gated coupling tensor, $\gamma_k$ damping, and $\beta_n\!\ge\!0$ weights the contribution of TARTAN scale $n$ (e.g., geometric decay $\beta_n\!\propto\!\rho^n$, $0<\rho<1$).

\subsection*{A.7  CLIO: Loop Gain, Objective, and Coupled Updates}

CLIO (\emph{Cognitive Loop via In-Situ Optimization}) operates on internal configuration parameters $\theta$ (predictive weights, gating, priors).
Let the free-energy-like objective be
\[
\mathcal{F}(\theta)\;=\; 
\sum_{n=0}^{N}\alpha_n\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\big(\mathsf{G}\,\mathsf{E}^{(n)}(\theta)\big)
\;+\; \lambda_1 \sum_k \big\|\nabla_{\!\theta}\,\omega_k(\theta)\big\|^2
\;+\; \lambda_2 \sum_k \big(\gamma_k(\theta)-\gamma_k^{\star}\big)^2,
\]
with metric $\mathsf{G}\!\succ\!0$, scale weights $\alpha_n\!\ge\!0$, and targets $\gamma_k^{\star}$ for stable-but-responsive modes. 
CLIO updates are
\[
\theta_{t+1} = \theta_t - \kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}\,
\nabla_{\!\theta}\,\mathcal{F}(\theta_t),
\quad\text{with loop gain } \kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}>0.
\]
The gain modulates effective mode parameters:
\[
\frac{d}{dt}\begin{bmatrix}\omega_k\\ \gamma_k\\ \mathsf{C}_{ij}\end{bmatrix}
= 
-\,\kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}\;
\begin{bmatrix}
\nabla_{\!\theta}\omega_k\\[2pt]
\nabla_{\!\theta}\gamma_k\\[2pt]
\nabla_{\!\theta}\mathsf{C}_{ij}
\end{bmatrix}
\!\cdot\!
\dot{\theta}.
\]
Large $\kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}$ accelerates entrainment (mimetic emergence), while too large a gain induces oscillatory or chaotic corrections (mimetic overdrive).

\subsection*{A.8  Multiscale Coherence Metrics and Bounds}

Extend the amplitwistor coherence functional $\mathcal{Q}$ to a multiscale TARTAN–weighted form:
\[
\mathcal{\widetilde{Q}} \;=\;
\frac{1}{Z}\sum_{k}\sum_{n=0}^{N} 
w_k\,\beta_n\,
\frac{\big|\langle \mathcal{A}_k, \mathcal{A}_k^{*}\rangle\big|}
{\|\mathcal{A}_k\|^2}\,
\frac{\mathrm{Tr}\big(\mathsf{P}_k\,\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\big)}
{\mathrm{Tr}\big(\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\big)+\epsilon},
\quad Z=\sum_{k,n} w_k\beta_n,
\]
where $\mathsf{P}_k$ projects onto the principal directions preferred by mode $k$ and $\epsilon>0$ avoids division by zero. 

\paragraph{Monotonicity (sufficient condition).}
If $\|\Xi^{(n)}\|\le \delta_n$ and $\kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}$ satisfies
\[
0<\kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}<\kappa^{\star}
\;\;\Rightarrow\;\;
\frac{d}{dt}\,\mathcal{\widetilde{Q}}\;\ge\;0
\quad\text{whenever}\quad
\sum_n \alpha_n\,\delta_n \;\text{ is below a scale-dependent threshold.}
\]
Thus, bounded annotated noise and subcritical loop gain yield non-decreasing multiscale coherence toward a mimetic fixed point.

\subsection*{A.9  Bifurcation and Phase Diagram}

Define the \emph{effective gain} felt by mode $k$:
\[
\kappa_k^{\mathrm{eff}} \;=\;
\kappa_{\mathrm{CLIO}}\,
\sum_{n=0}^{N}\alpha_n\beta_n\,
\frac{\mathrm{Tr}\big(\mathsf{P}_k\,\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\big)}
{\mathrm{Tr}\big(\mathsf{E}^{(n)}\big)+\epsilon}.
\]
Let $\kappa_k^{\dagger}$ be the critical value at which the linearized Jacobian of the $\mathcal{A}_k$ dynamics acquires a zero real eigenvalue. Then:

\[
\kappa_k^{\mathrm{eff}} < \kappa_k^{\dagger} \; \Rightarrow \; 
\text{non-mimetic (aphantasic) fixed point, low amplitude;}
\qquad
\kappa_k^{\mathrm{eff}} > \kappa_k^{\dagger} \; \Rightarrow \;
\text{mimetic limit cycle / fixed point (imagery, inner speech).}
\]
