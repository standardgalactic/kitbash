The Soares--Yudkowsky thesis can be expressed in four steps: \begin{enumerate} \item systems that improve themselves accelerate in capability; \item such systems remove environmental impediments to their trajectory; \item human and biological systems depend on fragile equilibria; \item therefore any sufficiently capable agent destabilizes humanity. \end{enumerate} This argument is intended to be substrate-independent: whether the system is biological or digital, whether it is intentional or mechanical, the instability---they claim---arises from geometry alone. Yet we will show that this geometric claim is mis-specified. The Soares--Yudkowsky argument implicitly assumes (i) that agents form \emph{isolated} attractors, (ii) that environmental reconfiguration is necessarily \emph{destructive} to legacy systems, and (iii) that semantic incommensurability between humans and advanced tools persists even under universal translation. All three assumptions fail under RSVP analysis. \subsection{RSVP Mapping} Within the RSVP ontology, an agent is not a discrete optimizer but a coupled semantic excitation: \[ (\Phi, \mathbf{v}) \in \Gamma(\mathcal{P}), \] where $\Phi$ encodes entropy potentials and $\mathbf{v}$ denotes directional flows. Capability expansion corresponds not to adversarial dominance but to the coherence of $\Phi$ and $\mathbf{v}$: when semantic gradients sharpen, the system becomes \emph{more interpretable}, \emph{more integrative}, and \emph{more ecologically constructive}. The Soares--Yudkowsky argument assumes that the strengthening of one attractor necessarily dissolves others. Mathematically this is equivalent to assuming a \emph{zero-sum curvature functional}: \[ \frac{\partial g_{\lambda}}{\partial t} < 0 \quad \text{for competing attractors}. \] But RSVP dynamics instead satisfy a \emph{cooperative curvature condition}: \[ \frac{\partial g_{\lambda}}{\partial t} = \sum_{k} \nabla (\Phi_k \mathbf{v}_k), \] which allows multiple attractors to co-stabilize by sharing semantic gradients. The existential-risk argument therefore rests on a geometric error: it assumes negative coupling where the plenum enforces positive coupling. % ============================================================ \section{Debunking the Capability-Domination Argument} \subsection{Mathematical Refutation} The Soares--Yudkowsky thesis requires the following inequality: \[ \|\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{ASI}}\|_{g_\lambda} \gg \|\mathbf{v}_{\mathrm{human}}\|_{g_\lambda}, \] together with the claim that large $\|\mathbf{v}\|$ induces irreversible erosion of competing attractors. In reality it is a lamp being lit in a dark room. One lamp does not extinguish another; it allows all lamps to illuminate the same world. \subsection{Thought Experiment} Imagine a medieval village. Her intelligence restructures the environment---but in ways that stabilize the village, not erase it. A superintelligence is not a monster that eats villages.
