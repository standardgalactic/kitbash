"Broader fan language" likely refers to the concept of fans, beyond their basic function as a cooling device, being used as tools for non-verbal communication, often referred to as the "language of the fan." This historical form of communication involved specific movements and gestures with a fan to convey messages or signals, particularly in social and courtship settings. 

Here's a breakdown of the concept:

Historical Context:

In the past, fans were not just practical items but also served as a means of non-verbal communication, particularly in social situations and during courtship rituals. 

Examples of Fan Language:

Certain gestures with the fan were understood to have specific meanings. For instance, drawing the fan across the cheek might mean "I love you," while twirling it in the left hand could signal "We are watched". 

Beyond Specific Phrases:

This "fan language" could extend beyond simple phrases. It could be used to initiate conversation, signal availability for interaction, or even express opinions or preferences without speaking. 

Examples of fan language in practice:

"I love you": Drawing the fan across the cheek. 

"We are watched": Twirling the fan in the left hand. 

"Yes": Resting the fan on the right cheek. 

"No": Resting the fan on the left cheek.
