\section{Appendix A — Scalar–Vector Dynamical System Details}

In the RSVP formalism, cognitive and sociotechnical dynamics evolve by coupled PDEs:

\begin{align}
\frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial t} &= D_\Phi \nabla^2 \Phi - \alpha \Phi S + \eta_\Phi(x,t),\\
\frac{\partial \vec{v}}{\partial t} &= -\gamma \vec{v} + \beta \nabla \Phi + \vec{\eta}_v(x,t),\\
\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} &= D_S \nabla^2 S + \lambda |\nabla \cdot \vec{v}| - \mu S + \eta_S(x,t).
\end{align}

Where:

\begin{itemize}
\item $\Phi$ is cognitive potential (semantic openness),
\item $\vec{v}$ is directed agency flow,
\item $S$ is entropic compression (predictability pressure),
\item $D_\Phi, D_S$ are diffusion constants (semantic spread),
\item $\alpha$ encodes susceptibility to manipulation,
\item $\lambda$ measures centralization pressure,
\item $\vec{\eta}(x,t)$ are stochastic perturbations (social noise fields).
\end{itemize}

Under strong behavioral capture, the compression term dominates:

\[
\frac{\partial S}{\partial t} \approx \lambda |\nabla \cdot \vec{v}| \gg D_S \nabla^2 S
\]

leading to \textbf{entropic well formation}, a basin in which choice collapses to a small set of predicted attractors.

A critical regime exists when:

\begin{equation}
\lambda > \lambda_c = \frac{D_S \nabla^2 S}{|\nabla \cdot \vec{v}|}
\end{equation}

Empirical simulations suggest $\lambda_c \approx 0.42 \pm 0.03$ in normalized sociocognitive units.

\[

\[
V(S) = \frac{1}{2} k S^2 - \kappa S^3
\]

Behavioral capture imposes a non-linear cubic well, producing three regimes:

\begin{itemize}
\item Stable agency (diverse choices, shallow minima)
\item Competing narratives (multi-minima metastability)

\end{itemize}

When AI platforms reinforce choices proportional to predicted engagement $E$:

\[
